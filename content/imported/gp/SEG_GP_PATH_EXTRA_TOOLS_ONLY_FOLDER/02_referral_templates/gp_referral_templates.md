# GP Referral Templates

These templates are for safe communication from GP/primary care to ED/ambulance/specialist services.

They are not local protocols. Adapt to local policy.

---

## Universal ED Referral Template

**Opening:**  
I am referring a patient with **[presentation]** and **[red flags]**. I am concerned about **[danger diagnosis/differential]**.

**Patient:**  
- Name / age:
- Relevant comorbidities:
- Pregnancy/postpartum status if relevant:
- Medications/allergies:
- Anticoagulants/immunosuppression if relevant:

**Presentation:**  
- Main complaint:
- Onset:
- Progression:
- Associated red flags:

**Current status:**  
- Appearance:
- Mental status:
- Breathing:
- Circulation/perfusion:
- Vitals if available:
- ECG/labs if available:

**Reason for referral:**  
- Danger concern:
- Why clinic care is not enough:
- Transport plan:
- Patient/family informed:

## Chest Pain Referral

I am referring a patient with chest pain and high-risk features.  
Concern includes ACS, PE, dissection, pneumothorax, or other serious chest pathology depending on assessment.

Include:
- onset and duration
- character/radiation
- dyspnea/sweating/syncope
- cardiac/PE/dissection risks
- ECG status if available
- current stability
- transport plan

---

## Dyspnea Referral

I am referring a patient with shortness of breath and red flags.  
Concern includes respiratory failure, PE, pneumonia/sepsis, pulmonary edema, pneumothorax, anaphylaxis, or metabolic/toxicologic cause.

Include:
- work of breathing
- ability to speak
- cyanosis/confusion/exhaustion
- chest pain/syncope
- wheeze/fever/cough
- oxygenation if available
- current stability

---

## Sepsis / Serious Infection Referral

I am referring a patient with suspected serious infection and systemic red flags.

Include:
- suspected source
- fever/rigors or absence of fever in high-risk patient
- confusion/weakness
- perfusion concerns
- breathing concerns
- rash/meningism if present
- immunosuppression/pregnancy/elderly/infant status
- current stability

---

## Pediatric Referral

I am referring a child with red flags requiring urgent assessment.

Include:
- age
- feeding/urine output
- behavior compared with baseline
- work of breathing
- rash
- fever duration
- dehydration signs
- caregiver concern
- chronic disease/immunization context if relevant

---

## Pregnancy / Postpartum Referral

I am referring a pregnant/postpartum patient with high-risk symptoms.

Include:
- pregnancy status/gestational age/postpartum timing
- pain/bleeding
- headache/visual symptoms/seizure
- chest pain/dyspnea
- fever/toxic appearance
- fetal movement if relevant
- current stability

---

## Trauma Referral

I am referring a trauma patient with concerning mechanism or red flags.

Include:
- mechanism
- time of injury
- head injury/loss of consciousness/vomiting/seizure
- anticoagulant use
- chest/abdominal/pelvic symptoms
- neurovascular findings
- safeguarding concern if present
- current stability

---

## Overdose / Toxicology Referral

I am referring a patient after suspected overdose/poisoning.

Include:
- substance if known
- time
- estimated amount
- intent
- co-ingestions
- mental status
- breathing/circulation
- ECG/glucose if available
- containers or scene information
- self-harm risk

---

## Mental Health / Self-Harm Referral

I am referring a patient with acute mental health safety concern.

Include:
- suicidal thoughts/plan/intent
- access to means
- recent self-harm/overdose
- psychosis/agitation/risk to others
- substance use
- support/supervision
- medical stability
- whether the patient is safe to wait or leave
