# Abdominal Pain Red Flags

## Purpose

Help GP recognize abdominal pain requiring ED/surgical evaluation.

## Dangerous causes

- appendicitis
- perforation/peritonitis
- bowel obstruction
- mesenteric ischemia
- AAA in appropriate patient
- ectopic pregnancy
- ovarian/testicular torsion
- pancreatitis
- biliary sepsis
- GI bleeding
- DKA/metabolic cause
- sepsis

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- peritonitis or guarding
- shock appearance
- severe pain out of proportion
- syncope
- GI bleeding
- persistent vomiting
- abdominal distension with obstruction concern
- pregnancy or possible pregnancy with pain/bleeding
- elderly with new severe abdominal/flank/back pain
- testicular pain
- severe pelvic pain
- fever with toxic appearance
- immunocompromised patient
- anticoagulated patient with concerning pain

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- onset and progression
- location and migration
- vomiting/diarrhea/constipation
- urinary symptoms
- GI bleeding
- pregnancy possibility
- testicular/pelvic symptoms
- vascular risk
- previous surgery
- anticoagulant use

Check:

- general appearance
- hydration/perfusion
- abdominal tenderness pattern
- peritonism
- hernia sites if appropriate
- pregnancy test when relevant and available
