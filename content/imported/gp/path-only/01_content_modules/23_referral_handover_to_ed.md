# Referral & Handover to ED

## Purpose

Teach GP how to communicate referrals clearly.

## Referral structure

Use:

1. Who is the patient?
2. What is the current concern?
3. Why is this urgent?
4. What red flags are present?
5. What has been checked?
6. What has changed?
7. What relevant history matters?
8. What medications/allergies matter?
9. How is the patient coming?
10. What do you need from ED?

## Include

- name/age
- presentation
- onset
- red flags
- vitals if available
- exam findings
- ECG/labs if available
- medications/allergies
- pregnancy status if relevant
- comorbidities
- reason for referral
- transport plan
- safety advice

## Avoid

- vague referral: â€œplease assessâ€
- no onset time
- no risk factors
- no red flags documented
- unsafe self-driving
- no explanation of why ED is needed
- false reassurance

## Documentation

Document:

- presenting complaint
- red flags
- differential concern
- discussion with patient/family
- ED/ambulance contact if done
- transport advice
- refusal of referral if applicable
- safety-net instructions
