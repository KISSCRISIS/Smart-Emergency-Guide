# Hypertension Red Flags for GP

## Purpose

Help GP identify patients with high blood pressure who may have acute end-organ danger.

## Dangerous concerns

- stroke/TIA
- intracranial bleed
- ACS
- acute heart failure/pulmonary edema
- aortic dissection
- acute kidney injury concern
- pregnancy-related hypertensive emergency concern
- hypertensive encephalopathy concern

## Red flags

Urgent referral if high BP is associated with:

- chest pain
- severe shortness of breath
- neurologic deficit
- confusion
- seizure
- severe sudden headache
- visual loss
- syncope
- pregnancy/postpartum symptoms
- tearing chest/back pain
- pulmonary edema symptoms
- reduced urine output with systemic illness

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- symptoms, not just the number
- neurologic symptoms
- chest/back pain
- dyspnea
- pregnancy/postpartum
- kidney disease
- medication adherence
- stimulant/drug use

Check:

- mental status
- focused neuro screen
- heart/lungs
- perfusion
- eye/visual red flags if relevant

## Common GP traps

- Treating the BP number alone.
- Reassuring severe symptoms because the patient is young.
- Missing pregnancy/postpartum red flags.
- Rapidly lowering BP in clinic without senior/local guidance.
