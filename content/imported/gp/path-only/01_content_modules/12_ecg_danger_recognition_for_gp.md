# ECG Danger Recognition for GP

## Purpose

Teach GP-level ECG danger recognition, not advanced interpretation.

## ECG findings needing urgent ED/senior review

- ST elevation or STEMI-equivalent concern
- new ischemic changes with symptoms
- wide complex tachycardia
- complete heart block
- severe bradycardia with symptoms
- rapid irregular rhythm with instability
- long QT with syncope/seizure concern
- Brugada/WPW pattern with syncope or palpitations
- hyperkalemia pattern concern
- toxicology-related ECG abnormality concern

## GP ECG principles

- ECG must match the patient, not be read alone.
- A normal ECG does not automatically make chest pain safe.
- Compare old ECG if available.
- If uncertain and the patient is symptomatic, escalate.
- Do not delay transfer for perfect interpretation.

## Common GP traps

- Reassuring chest pain after one ECG.
- Calling wide complex tachycardia benign.
- Missing heart block in dizziness/syncope.
- Ignoring ECG changes in overdose.
