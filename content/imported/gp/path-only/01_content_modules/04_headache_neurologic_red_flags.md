# Headache & Neurologic Red Flags

## Purpose

Help GP separate common headache from dangerous headache or neurologic emergency.

## Dangerous causes

- subarachnoid hemorrhage
- meningitis/encephalitis
- stroke/TIA
- intracranial mass/raised pressure
- cerebral venous thrombosis
- temporal arteritis in appropriate patients
- acute glaucoma/eye emergency
- hypertensive emergency concern
- pregnancy/postpartum neurologic emergency

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- thunderclap headache
- worst headache of life
- new neurologic deficit
- seizure
- fever with neck stiffness or confusion
- reduced consciousness
- papilledema concern
- new headache in pregnancy/postpartum
- new headache with cancer/immunosuppression
- new headache in older patient
- headache after trauma, especially anticoagulated
- acute visual loss or painful red eye
- progressive worsening pattern

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- onset speed
- time to maximum intensity
- previous similar headaches
- fever/neck stiffness
- neurologic symptoms
- trauma
- pregnancy/postpartum
- anticoagulants
- cancer/immunosuppression
- visual symptoms

Check:

- mental status
- cranial nerves
- limb power/sensation
- speech
- gait if safe
- neck stiffness
- eye/red-flag visual signs

## Common GP traps

- Treating thunderclap headache as migraine.
- Ignoring fever/confusion with headache.
- Missing pregnancy/postpartum risk.
- Reassuring after temporary analgesic response.
