# Pediatric Danger Signs for GP

## Purpose

Help GP identify children who need urgent ED/pediatric assessment.

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- poor feeding in infant
- lethargy or reduced interaction
- persistent inconsolable crying
- respiratory distress
- cyanosis
- apnea
- seizure
- non-blanching rash
- dehydration concern
- reduced urine output
- bilious vomiting
- persistent vomiting with drowsiness
- fever in very young infant
- toxic appearance
- neck stiffness
- bulging fontanelle
- trauma with concerning features
- safeguarding concern
- parent says â€œthis is not my childâ€

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- age
- feeding
- wet diapers/urine output
- behavior compared with baseline
- breathing
- fever duration
- vomiting/diarrhea
- rash
- immunization status
- chronic disease
- trauma/safeguarding concern

Check:

- appearance
- work of breathing
- hydration
- perfusion
- mental state
- rash
- abdominal tenderness
- neck stiffness if appropriate

## Common GP traps

- Treating children as small adults.
- Reassuring because the child briefly looks better.
- Ignoring caregiver concern.
- Missing dehydration or sepsis.
