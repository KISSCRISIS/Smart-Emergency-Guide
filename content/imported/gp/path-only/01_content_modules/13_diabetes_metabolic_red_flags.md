# Diabetes & Metabolic Red Flags for GP

## Purpose

Help GP recognize diabetic and metabolic presentations that need urgent ED assessment.

## Dangerous causes

- DKA/HHS concern
- severe hypoglycemia or recurrent hypoglycemia
- sepsis in diabetic patient
- dehydration with metabolic deterioration
- hyperkalemia or renal failure concern
- medication-related complication
- diabetic foot infection with systemic illness
- altered mental status from metabolic cause

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- altered mental status
- severe weakness with dehydration
- vomiting with diabetes
- abdominal pain with diabetes
- rapid breathing or severe fatigue
- suspected DKA/HHS
- recurrent hypoglycemia
- hypoglycemia with long-acting medication concern
- fever/toxic appearance
- diabetic foot with spreading infection, systemic symptoms, ischemia, or deep tissue concern
- kidney disease with systemic illness
- pregnancy with diabetes and concerning symptoms

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- diabetes type and medications
- recent missed medications or illness
- vomiting/poor intake
- glucose trend if known
- infection symptoms
- urine output/thirst
- abdominal pain
- mental status change
- kidney disease

Check:

- appearance
- hydration/perfusion
- mental status
- breathing pattern
- glucose if available
- focused infection/foot exam when relevant

## Common GP traps

- Treating glucose number without assessing the patient.
- Missing sepsis in diabetic patients.
- Reassuring after temporary glucose correction.
- Underestimating vomiting and abdominal pain in diabetes.
