# Elderly & Frailty Red Flags for GP

## Purpose

Help GP recognize serious illness in older or frail patients where presentations may be subtle.

## Why elderly patients are high-risk

Older patients may present without classic symptoms. Serious disease may appear as:

- weakness
- fall
- confusion
- reduced intake
- functional decline
- dizziness
- vague pain
- caregiver concern

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- new confusion or delirium
- fall with head injury or anticoagulant use
- chest pain, dyspnea, syncope, or collapse
- severe weakness with inability to mobilize
- suspected sepsis
- dehydration with frailty
- GI bleeding concern
- new neurologic deficit
- severe pain
- failure of home support
- unsafe living situation
- medication toxicity concern
- caregiver reports sudden major change

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- baseline function
- what changed today
- falls
- medications/anticoagulants
- infection symptoms
- intake/urine output
- cognition baseline
- support at home
- caregiver concern

Check:

- appearance
- mental status
- hydration/perfusion
- mobility if safe
- focused cardio/resp/neuro exam
- injury screen after falls

## Common GP traps

- Reassuring because symptoms are vague.
- Missing ACS/sepsis/stroke presenting as weakness.
- Underestimating anticoagulant head injury.
- Ignoring caregiver concern.
