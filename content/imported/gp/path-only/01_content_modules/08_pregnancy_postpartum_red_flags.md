# Pregnancy & Postpartum Red Flags

## Purpose

Help GP recognize high-risk pregnancy/postpartum presentations.

## Dangerous causes

- ectopic pregnancy
- miscarriage with instability
- preeclampsia/eclampsia concern
- PE
- sepsis
- postpartum hemorrhage
- stroke/neurologic emergency
- severe hyperemesis/dehydration
- abdominal/pelvic surgical emergency
- domestic violence/safeguarding issue

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- pregnancy possible plus abdominal/pelvic pain or bleeding
- syncope/collapse
- severe headache or visual symptoms
- seizure
- severe hypertension concern according to local policy
- shortness of breath or chest pain
- unilateral leg swelling/pain with concerning context
- fever/toxic appearance
- heavy bleeding
- reduced fetal movement in later pregnancy
- postpartum chest pain, dyspnea, headache, fever, heavy bleeding, or confusion
- severe vomiting with dehydration

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- pregnancy status/possibility
- gestational age if known
- bleeding/pain
- syncope
- headache/visual symptoms
- chest pain/dyspnea
- fever
- postpartum timing
- fetal movement if relevant
- domestic safety

Check:

- appearance
- perfusion
- mental status
- focused abdomen/pelvis context
- vitals if available
- urine/pregnancy test if relevant and available, without delaying referral if unstable

## Common GP traps

- Forgetting ectopic pregnancy.
- Underestimating postpartum risk.
- Treating headache in pregnancy as routine migraine.
- Missing PE in pregnancy/postpartum.
