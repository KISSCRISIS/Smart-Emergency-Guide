## Purpose

Help GP recognize wounds, bites, and burns that need ED/specialist care.

## Burn red flags

Urgent referral if:

- airway/face/neck burn concern
- inhalation injury concern
- circumferential burn
- hand, foot, genital, major joint, or face involvement
- chemical or electrical burn
- deep burn concern
- large burn area concern
- child/elderly/high-risk patient
- associated trauma
- safeguarding concern

## Bite red flags

Urgent referral if:

- human bite with hand/face involvement or deep injury
- animal bite with deep tissue injury
- neurovascular concern
- rapidly spreading infection
- immunocompromised patient
- high-risk location
- possible rabies/tetanus public health concern according to local policy
- systemic symptoms

## Wound red flags

Urgent referral if:

- uncontrolled bleeding
- tendon/nerve/vascular injury concern
- open fracture
- foreign body concern
- deep puncture with high-risk location
- infection with systemic symptoms
- diabetic foot wound with deep or spreading infection
- crush injury

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- mechanism
- time
- contamination
- bite source
- tetanus/rabies policy context
- comorbidities
- pain out of proportion
- function/sensation changes

Check:

- bleeding
- neurovascular status
- depth/location
- infection/systemic signs
- burn location and depth concern
- safeguarding concern

## Common GP traps

- Underestimating hand bites.
- Missing tendon/nerve injury.
- Treating diabetic foot wounds as simple skin infection.
- Missing inhalation injury risk in burns.
