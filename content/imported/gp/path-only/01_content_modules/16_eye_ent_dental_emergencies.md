# Eye / ENT / Dental Emergencies for GP

## Purpose

Help GP recognize eye, ENT, and dental presentations needing urgent specialist/ED care.

## Eye red flags

Urgent referral if:

- acute visual loss
- painful red eye with visual symptoms
- severe eye pain
- chemical injury
- penetrating injury concern
- contact lens user with painful red eye
- new diplopia with neurologic concern
- flashes/floaters with visual field loss
- suspected acute glaucoma
- eye trauma with reduced vision

## ENT red flags

Urgent referral if:

- stridor
- drooling or airway concern
- deep neck infection concern
- severe tonsillar/neck swelling with systemic illness
- epistaxis not controlled or with instability
- sudden hearing loss
- mastoiditis concern
- severe vertigo with neurologic signs
- facial weakness with concerning features

## Dental/oral red flags

Urgent referral if:

- facial/neck swelling with airway concern
- trismus
- dysphagia/drooling
- fever/toxic appearance
- floor-of-mouth swelling concern
- immunocompromised patient with spreading infection
- uncontrolled bleeding
- trauma with avulsed tooth or facial injury concern

## Common GP traps

- Treating painful red eye as conjunctivitis without checking vision.
- Missing airway risk in dental/neck infection.
- Missing sudden hearing loss urgency.
- Reassuring vertigo without neurologic screen.
