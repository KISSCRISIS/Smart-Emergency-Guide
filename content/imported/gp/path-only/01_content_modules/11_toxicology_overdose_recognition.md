# Toxicology & Overdose Recognition for GP

## Purpose

Help GP identify overdose/poisoning needing ED or poison center discussion.

## Red flags

Urgent referral/escalation if:

- reduced consciousness
- respiratory depression
- seizure
- severe agitation
- abnormal behavior with abnormal vitals
- chest pain after stimulant use
- suspected intentional overdose
- pediatric ingestion
- unknown substance
- multiple co-ingestions
- long-acting/sustained-release medication concern
- abnormal ECG if available
- pregnancy
- occupational/chemical exposure
- carbon monoxide concern
- recurrent symptoms after initial improvement

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- what was taken
- when
- how much might be missing
- intent
- co-ingestions
- alcohol or sedatives
- medication containers
- pregnancy possibility
- scene exposure for carbon monoxide/chemicals

Check:

- airway/breathing/circulation
- mental status
- pupils
- temperature
- glucose if available
- ECG if available and does not delay referral

## Common GP traps

- Believing the first story completely.
- Reassuring because the patient currently looks well.
- Missing acetaminophen/salicylate or mixed ingestion.
- Letting intentional overdose leave without emergency/mental health evaluation.
