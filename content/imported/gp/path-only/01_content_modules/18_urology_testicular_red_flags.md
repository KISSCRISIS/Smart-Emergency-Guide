# Urology & Testicular Red Flags for GP

## Purpose

Help GP identify urologic and genital presentations needing urgent ED/urology assessment.

## Dangerous causes

- testicular torsion
- obstructed infected kidney/stone concern
- acute urinary retention with severe symptoms
- pyelonephritis/sepsis
- Fournier gangrene concern
- trauma to genital/urinary tract
- ectopic/pelvic emergencies that mimic urinary symptoms

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- sudden testicular pain
- high-riding testis or severe scrotal pain
- flank pain with fever/toxic appearance
- urinary symptoms with sepsis concern
- severe renal colic with vomiting/dehydration/systemic illness
- anuria or severe urinary retention with distress
- genital/perineal pain with systemic illness
- rapidly spreading genital/perineal swelling/redness
- trauma with hematuria
- pregnancy possibility with abdominal/pelvic pain

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- onset and severity
- fever
- vomiting
- urinary symptoms
- testicular/scrotal symptoms
- pregnancy possibility
- kidney disease
- single kidney/transplant
- diabetes/immunosuppression
- trauma

Check:

- appearance
- hydration/perfusion
- abdomen/flank
- scrotal exam when appropriate and consented
- fever/systemic signs

## Common GP traps

- Delaying torsion referral for tests.
- Treating flank pain with fever as routine colic.
- Missing Fournier gangrene in diabetic/immunocompromised patients.
- Missing ectopic pregnancy in urinary/pelvic complaints.
