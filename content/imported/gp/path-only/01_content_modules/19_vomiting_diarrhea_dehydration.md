# Vomiting / Diarrhea / Dehydration Red Flags

## Purpose

Help GP distinguish simple gastroenteritis from dangerous dehydration, sepsis, surgical, pregnancy, or metabolic disease.

## Dangerous causes

- severe dehydration
- sepsis
- bowel obstruction
- appendicitis/surgical abdomen
- DKA/metabolic illness
- intracranial cause when vomiting with neuro signs
- pregnancy-related emergency
- toxin/overdose
- GI bleeding

## Red flags

Urgent referral if:

- shock appearance
- confusion or drowsiness
- severe dehydration
- persistent vomiting with inability to keep fluids
- bilious vomiting
- blood in vomit or stool
- severe abdominal pain or peritonism
- fever with toxic appearance
- elderly/frail deterioration
- infant/child with poor feeding or reduced urine
- pregnancy with concerning symptoms
- diabetes with vomiting/abdominal pain
- severe headache or neurologic signs with vomiting
- immunocompromised patient

## GP first-contact assessment

Ask:

- duration and frequency
- fluid intake and urine output
- blood/bile
- fever
- abdominal pain
- travel/food exposure
- sick contacts
- diabetes/pregnancy
- medications/toxins
- age and frailty risk

Check:

- mental status
- hydration
- perfusion
- abdominal exam
- temperature
- glucose if diabetes/AMS and available
