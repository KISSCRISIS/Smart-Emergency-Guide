## Core Structure

### 1. ECG First-Pass System

Suggested sequence:

1. Patient and ECG context
2. Rate
3. Rhythm
4. Axis
5. Intervals
6. QRS width
7. ST segment
8. T waves
9. QT concern
10. Compare with old ECG if available
11. Match ECG with patient symptoms
12. Decide: safe / concerning / senior now

### 2. Dangerous ECG First

Patterns that should trigger senior review:

- STEMI or STEMI equivalent concern
- Posterior MI concern
- Right ventricular MI concern
- Wellens pattern
- De Winter pattern
- Left main / diffuse ischemia pattern concern
- Wide complex tachycardia
- Complete heart block
- Severe bradycardia with symptoms
- Hyperkalemia pattern concern
- TCA / sodium channel blocker pattern concern
- Brugada pattern with syncope concern
- WPW with tachyarrhythmia concern
- Long QT / torsades risk
- Pericarditis vs MI diagnostic uncertainty
- PE-related ECG clues with unstable patient
