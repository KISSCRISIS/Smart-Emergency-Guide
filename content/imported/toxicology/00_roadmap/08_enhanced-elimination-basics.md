# Enhanced Elimination Basics

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Core Concept

Enhanced elimination uses interventions such as urinary alkalinization, multiple-dose activated charcoal, or extracorporeal treatment for selected toxins with favorable pharmacokinetics and severe clinical toxicity.

## High-Yield Recognition Clues
- Severe salicylate, lithium, toxic alcohol, theophylline, or valproate patterns
- Low molecular weight, low protein binding, low volume of distribution
- Persistent severe acidosis, neurologic toxicity, shock, or rising levels despite care

## ED Priorities
- Do not delay resuscitation while arranging elimination.
- Use toxicologist/nephrology guidance early.
- Know the concept; verify thresholds locally.
- Monitor acid-base status, electrolytes, and clinical trajectory.

## Common Trap

Hemodialysis is not chosen just because the toxin is scary; it depends on toxin properties and patient severity.

## Assessment Question

**Which pharmacokinetic profile makes a toxin more dialyzable?**

A) Large volume of distribution and high protein binding
B) Low molecular weight, low protein binding, low volume of distribution
C) Purely intracellular distribution
D) Irreversible tissue binding only

**Correct Answer:** B

**Arabic reasoning:** السم القابل للغسيل يجب أن يكون موجوداً في الدم ومتاحاً للعبور عبر الغشاء.
