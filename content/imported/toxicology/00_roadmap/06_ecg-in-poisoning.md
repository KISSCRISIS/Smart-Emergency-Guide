# ECG in Poisoning

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Core Concept

The ECG is a toxicology vital sign. It reveals sodium-channel blockade, potassium-channel effects, QT prolongation, digoxin-like patterns, hyperkalemia, and ischemia from sympathomimetics.

## High-Yield Recognition Clues
- QRS widening or terminal R in aVR
- QTc prolongation
- Bradycardia with AV block
- Ventricular ectopy or bidirectional VT
- Peaked T waves or sine-wave pattern
- ST changes after cocaine/amphetamine exposure

## ED Priorities
- Repeat ECGs if symptoms evolve.
- Treat ECG patterns, not only drug names.
- Look specifically at QRS width, aVR, QTc, rate/rhythm, and potassium-related changes.

## Common Trap

A normal first ECG does not guarantee safety after delayed-release, long-acting, or evolving toxic ingestion.

## Assessment Question

**In suspected tricyclic antidepressant overdose, which ECG feature is a major warning sign?**

A) Short PR only
B) Wide QRS / prominent terminal R in aVR
C) Isolated sinus bradycardia with normal intervals
D) Low voltage only

**Correct Answer:** B

**Arabic reasoning:** TCA يضرب قنوات الصوديوم، فيتوسع QRS وتظهر علامة aVR؛ هذه ليست تفصيلاً بل إنذار.
