# Toxidrome Recognition: Pattern Before Poison Name

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Core Concept

A toxidrome is a recognizable cluster of vital signs, pupils, skin findings, bowel/bladder activity, neurologic tone, and mental status that suggests a class of toxins before laboratory confirmation.

## High-Yield Recognition Clues
- Pupil size: miosis vs mydriasis
- Skin: wet vs dry, flushed vs pale
- Bowel/bladder secretions
- Mental status: coma, delirium, agitation
- Neuromuscular findings: clonus, rigidity, seizures, fasciculations

## ED Priorities
- Classify into opioid, sedative, sympathomimetic, anticholinergic, cholinergic, serotonergic, or dopamine-blockade patterns.
- Look for mixed toxidromes in polypharmacy.
- Use the toxidrome to choose monitoring and antidote questions, not to skip resuscitation.

## Common Trap

Do not force every patient into one toxidrome. Mixed overdoses are common.

## Assessment Question

**A delirious patient has dry axillae, mydriasis, urinary retention, tachycardia, and flushed skin. Which toxidrome is most likely?**

A) Opioid
B) Anticholinergic
C) Cholinergic
D) Sedative-hypnotic

**Correct Answer:** B
