# Initial Investigations in Poisoning

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Core Concept

Initial testing should identify immediately reversible threats and poison-specific complications, not chase an indiscriminate toxicology screen.

## High-Yield Recognition Clues
- Altered mental status
- Seizure
- Shock or dysrhythmia
- Acid-base disturbance
- Intentional ingestion or unknown exposure

## ED Priorities
- Point-of-care glucose.
- ECG for all serious or unknown overdoses.
- Acetaminophen and salicylate levels in many intentional/unknown ingestions.
- Electrolytes, renal function, acid-base status, lactate/ketones when clinically indicated.
- Pregnancy test when relevant.
- Targeted levels for lithium, valproate, digoxin, iron, carboxyhemoglobin/methemoglobin, alcohols, or other specific concerns.

## Common Trap

Routine urine drug screens rarely answer the emergency question and may mislead because of false positives, false negatives, and poor timing.

## Assessment Question

**Which test is highest-yield early in an unknown serious overdose because it detects sodium-channel blockade and dysrhythmia risk?**

A) ECG
B) Chest CT
C) Routine urine drug screen only
D) Stool culture

**Correct Answer:** A

**Arabic reasoning:** التخطيط يكشف QRS واسع، aVR، QT طويل، واضطرابات قاتلة بسرعة.
