# Decontamination Basics

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Core Concept

Decontamination aims to stop ongoing exposure. It includes removing contaminated clothing, skin/eye irrigation, avoiding secondary contamination of staff, and selective gastrointestinal decontamination when benefits outweigh risks.

## High-Yield Recognition Clues
- Chemical odor or wet clothing
- Skin or eye irritation
- Industrial/lab exposure
- Caustic ingestion
- Ingestion of radiopaque tablets or packets
- Unprotected airway

## ED Priorities
- Protect staff first.
- Remove clothing and irrigate skin/eyes when contaminated.
- Avoid charcoal for caustics, metals like iron, many alcohols, and unprotected airway.
- Consider GI decontamination only after risk-benefit review with toxicology.

## Common Trap

Activated charcoal is not a universal antidote; it can harm patients with depressed mental status or substances it does not bind.

## Assessment Question

**Which situation is a poor candidate for activated charcoal?**

A) Awake cooperative patient after a charcoal-adsorbable medication exposure
B) Patient with caustic alkali ingestion
C) Stable patient after selected early ingestion discussed with poison center
D) Alert patient with minor benign ingestion

**Correct Answer:** B

**Arabic reasoning:** المواد الكاوية لا يمتصها الفحم جيداً وقد يعيق التنظير ويزيد الخطر.
