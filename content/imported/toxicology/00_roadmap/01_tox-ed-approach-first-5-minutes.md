# Toxicology ED Approach: The First 5 Minutes

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Core Concept

Toxicology in the ED begins as resuscitation, not diagnosis. The first task is to identify immediate threats to oxygenation, ventilation, circulation, temperature, glucose, and rhythm while collecting enough exposure history to guide antidotal thinking.

## High-Yield Recognition Clues
- Unknown ingestion or exposure
- Altered mental status with unexplained vital-sign abnormality
- Mixed neurologic, autonomic, GI, or ECG findings
- Multiple patients from the same scene or chemical odor

## ED Priorities
- Protect staff: PPE and decontamination when contamination is possible.
- Stabilize airway, breathing, circulation, disability, exposure.
- Check glucose and temperature early.
- Place on cardiac monitoring and obtain ECG for cardiotoxic poisons.
- Call poison center/toxicologist early when serious, unknown, pediatric, intentional, or long-acting exposures are possible.

## Common Trap

Do not wait for a named toxin before treating a crashing poisoned patient. Pattern recognition plus resuscitation wins.

## Assessment Question

**A confused patient with unknown ingestion arrives tachycardic, hyperthermic, and agitated. What is the safest initial framing?**

A) Wait for confirmatory toxicology screening before treatment
B) Resuscitate, recognize a toxidrome, obtain ECG/glucose/temperature, and call poison center
C) Give activated charcoal to all poisoned patients
D) Discharge if the initial vital signs improve

**Correct Answer:** B

**Arabic reasoning:** السموم لا تنتظر نتيجة المختبر. نثبت المريض ونقرأ النمط ونطلب المساعدة مبكراً.
