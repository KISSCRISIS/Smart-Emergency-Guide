# Methylene Blue

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Acquired methemoglobinemia with clinically significant hypoxia/cyanosis as guided by protocols.

## Mechanism

Reduces methemoglobin back to functional hemoglobin via NADPH-dependent pathways.

## When to Think of It

Cyanosis after benzocaine/nitrates/dapsone; chocolate-brown blood; saturation gap.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor oxygenation, methemoglobin level, hemolysis risk, and contraindications.

## Dangerous Trap

Pulse oximetry can mislead.
