# Naloxone

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Opioid-induced respiratory depression or coma with ventilatory compromise.

## Mechanism

Competitive opioid receptor antagonist that reverses opioid effect temporarily.

## When to Think of It

Miosis plus hypoventilation; respiratory depression after suspected opioid; recurrent toxicity after long-acting agents.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor ventilation, recurrence, agitation/withdrawal, pulmonary complications, and co-ingestions.

## Dangerous Trap

Naloxone response is not disposition. Long-acting opioids may outlast naloxone.

## Assessment Question

**Which statement best describes Naloxone in ED toxicology?**

A) It should be used blindly in every poisoning
B) It is considered when the clinical mechanism matches its indication: Opioid-induced respiratory depression or coma with ventilatory compromise.
C) It replaces airway and circulation management
D) It removes the need to call poison center

**Correct Answer:** B

**Arabic reasoning:** الترياق لا يُستخدم عشوائياً؛ يُستخدم عندما يطابق آلية السم وخطر المريض.
