# Pyridoxine

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Isoniazid-induced seizures or strong suspicion of INH overdose.

## Mechanism

Replaces functional vitamin B6 needed for GABA synthesis.

## When to Think of It

Status epilepticus after TB medication access; refractory seizures.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor seizure control, acid-base status, airway, and co-ingestions.

## Dangerous Trap

Benzodiazepines alone may not stop INH seizures.

## Arabic Simplification

يعيد الوقود الذي يصنع فرامل الدماغ GABA.

## Assessment Question

**Which statement best describes Pyridoxine in ED toxicology?**

A) It should be used blindly in every poisoning
B) It is considered when the clinical mechanism matches its indication: Isoniazid-induced seizures or strong suspicion of INH overdose.
C) It replaces airway and circulation management

**Correct Answer:** B
