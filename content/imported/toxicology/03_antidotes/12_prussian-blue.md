# Prussian Blue

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Thallium poisoning and selected radioactive cesium exposures.

## Mechanism

Ion-exchange resin that interrupts enterohepatic circulation and enhances fecal elimination.

## When to Think of It

Alopecia + painful ascending neuropathy after GI illness or suspected poisoning.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor neurologic symptoms, electrolytes, GI function, and exposure investigation.

## Dangerous Trap

Initial GI symptoms may mimic food poisoning.
