# Digoxin-Specific Antibody Fragments

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Severe digoxin/cardiac glycoside toxicity with dangerous rhythm, hyperkalemia, or instability per protocol.

## Mechanism

Binds free cardiac glycoside, reducing active toxin effect.

## When to Think of It

Halos, GI symptoms, bradyarrhythmia/ectopy, hyperkalemia, toad/cardiac glycoside exposure.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor potassium, rhythm, renal function, and interpretation issues with digoxin levels after Fab.

## Dangerous Trap

Do not rely on digoxin level quantitatively in non-digoxin cardiac glycosides.
