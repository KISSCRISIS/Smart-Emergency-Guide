# Atropine

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Severe muscarinic cholinergic effects such as bronchorrhea, bronchospasm, and bradycardia; also symptomatic bradycardia in selected toxins.

## Mechanism

Antimuscarinic agent that dries secretions and improves muscarinic excess.

## When to Think of It

Wet patient: salivation, lacrimation, bronchorrhea, bradycardia, miosis.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor secretions, ventilation, heart rate, temperature, and recurrence.

## Dangerous Trap

Atropine does not reverse nicotinic weakness; pralidoxime concept may be needed for organophosphates.
