# Sodium Bicarbonate

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Sodium-channel blocker toxicity with wide QRS, TCA-like ECG pattern, dysrhythmia, or shock as guided by protocols.

## Mechanism

Increases serum sodium and alkalinizes blood, improving sodium-channel conduction in selected poisonings.

## When to Think of It

Wide QRS, terminal R in aVR, hypotension, seizures in TCA-like overdose.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor ECG narrowing, pH, potassium, sodium, ventilation, and volume status.

## Dangerous Trap

Do not use as generic antidote for every abnormal ECG.
