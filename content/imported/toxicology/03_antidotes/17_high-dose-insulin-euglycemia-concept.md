# High-Dose Insulin Euglycemia Concept

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Severe beta-blocker or calcium-channel blocker poisoning with cardiogenic shock, per protocol and expert guidance.

## Mechanism

Improves myocardial carbohydrate utilization and inotropy while maintaining euglycemia.

## When to Think of It

Bradycardia/hypotension, hyperglycemia in CCB, refractory shock.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor glucose, potassium, hemodynamics, and ICU-level complications.

## Dangerous Trap

This is not ordinary diabetes insulin use; it requires protocolized monitoring.
