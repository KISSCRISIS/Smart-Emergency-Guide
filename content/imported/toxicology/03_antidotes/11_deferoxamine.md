# Deferoxamine

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Systemic iron toxicity with shock, metabolic acidosis, severe symptoms, or concerning levels per toxicology guidance.

## Mechanism

Chelates iron to form ferrioxamine for excretion.

## When to Think of It

Iron tablets, GI symptoms, radiopaque tablets, acidosis, shock.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor hemodynamics, acid-base status, iron trends, urine color, and adverse effects.

## Dangerous Trap

Activated charcoal does not bind iron well.
