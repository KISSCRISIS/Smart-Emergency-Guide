# N-Acetylcysteine

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Acetaminophen poisoning when indicated by timing/level, unknown timing with concern, or evidence of hepatic injury.

## Mechanism

Replenishes glutathione and supports detoxification of the toxic metabolite.

## When to Think of It

Do not wait for symptoms. Consider in delayed/uncertain or high-risk patterns after expert review.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor liver enzymes, INR, acetaminophen level trend, renal function, and adverse reactions.

## Dangerous Trap

Normal early exam does not exclude danger.
