# Calcium Therapy Concept

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Hydrofluoric acid exposure, calcium-channel blocker toxicity, or hyperkalemia membrane stabilization depending on protocol.

## Mechanism

Provides calcium to counter specific physiologic effects or replenish bound calcium.

## When to Think of It

HF pain/hypocalcemia, CCB shock, hyperkalemia ECG changes.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor ECG, ionized calcium, hemodynamics, tissue injury, and local protocols.

## Dangerous Trap

Calcium indication differs by toxin; do not treat all shock the same.
