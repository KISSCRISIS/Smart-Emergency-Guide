# Fomepizole

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Methanol or ethylene glycol poisoning when suspected/confirmed per local criteria.

## Mechanism

Inhibits alcohol dehydrogenase, preventing formation of toxic metabolites.

## When to Think of It

High anion gap/osmolar gap, visual symptoms, renal injury/crystals, suspicious antifreeze/solvent exposure.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor acid-base status, levels, renal function, and dialysis indications with toxicology/nephrology.

## Dangerous Trap

Not helpful for isolated isopropanol and may prolong intoxication.
