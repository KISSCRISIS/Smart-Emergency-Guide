# Hydroxocobalamin

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Indication

Suspected cyanide poisoning, especially smoke inhalation with shock/severe lactic acidosis.

## Mechanism

Binds cyanide to form cyanocobalamin for elimination.

## When to Think of It

Smoke inhalation, coma, shock, severe lactate; industrial cyanide exposure.

## Key Monitoring

Monitor clinical response, blood pressure, lab interference/discoloration, and co-exposures like CO.

## Dangerous Trap

Do not wait for cyanide levels in a crashing patient.
