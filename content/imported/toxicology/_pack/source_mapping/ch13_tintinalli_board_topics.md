# Source Mapping: Tintinalli Board-Style Toxicology Themes

Primary source used for the initial board-style topic map: **Tintinalli’s Emergency Medicine Examination and Board Review, 2023 — Chapter 13: Toxicologic Disorders**.

This pack does **not** reproduce the chapter questions or answers. It uses the chapter to identify high-yield exam themes, then rewrites the content as original SEG educational pages.

## Chapter 13 High-Yield Themes Reflected in This Pack

- Caustic ingestion and airway/endoscopy thinking.
- Isoniazid seizures and pyridoxine.
- Cardiac glycoside toxicity and digoxin-specific antibody fragments.
- Alcoholic ketoacidosis and dextrose-containing fluids.
- Chlorine exposure and decontamination/supportive care.
- Organophosphate/cholinergic toxicity and the importance of the killer B’s.
- Methemoglobinemia and methylene blue concept.
- GHB intoxication and rapid recovery trap.
- Wernicke encephalopathy.
- Chronic lithium toxicity from dehydration/renal impairment.
- Valproate-associated hyperammonemia.
- Anticholinergic syndrome.
- MDMA-associated hyponatremia.
- Iron toxicity and limitations of activated charcoal.
- Clonidine/alpha-2 agonist mimicry of opioid toxicity.
- TCA/sodium-channel blocker ECG pattern.
- Serotonin syndrome.
- Isopropanol ketosis without acidosis.
- Salicylate acid-base pattern.
- Toxic alcohols and dialyzable poison concepts.
- Body packing rupture risk.
- Thallium poisoning and Prussian blue.
- Hydrogen sulfide exposure and scene safety.
- Bufo toad digoxin-like toxicity.
- Methadone recurrence after naloxone.

## Content Rule

SEG pages are short, standalone, ED-focused, and site-ready. Final medication doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and local disposition times are intentionally excluded until a verified protocol layer is created.
