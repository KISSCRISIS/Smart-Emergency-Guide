# Rapid Prompts: What Can Kill the Patient Now?

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Rapid Prompts

1. Unknown overdose + wide QRS → sodium-channel blockade / dysrhythmia risk.
2. Opioid toxidrome + long-acting agent → recurrent respiratory depression.
3. Cholinergic patient + bronchorrhea → airway and ventilation death.
4. Salicylate + tachypnea → do not casually suppress ventilation.
5. Anticholinergic delirium + hyperthermia → agitation/heat/seizure risk.
6. Serotonin syndrome + clonus + hyperthermia → muscle activity heat death.
7. CO exposure cluster + normal pulse ox → hidden hypoxia.
8. Acetaminophen overdose + normal exam → delayed liver failure.
9. Toxic alcohol + acidosis → metabolite injury and need for expert elimination decisions.
10. Body packer + abdominal pain → packet rupture/ischemia emergency.
11. Lithium + dehydration + ataxia → chronic neurotoxicity and renal clearance problem.
12. Iron tablets + acidosis → mitochondrial toxicity/shock.
13. Methemoglobinemia + chocolate blood → impaired oxygen carriage.
14. Cyanide + smoke inhalation + lactate → cellular asphyxiation.
