# Toxicology MCQ Bank

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Board-Style MCQs

### Question 1

Unknown overdose with wide QRS and terminal R in aVR. Best mechanism-targeted concept?

A) Naloxone
B) Sodium bicarbonate
C) Vitamin K
D) Fomepizole

**Answer:** B

**Arabic explanation:** Wide QRS/aVR suggests sodium-channel blockade.

### Question 2

Dry delirious teen with mydriasis and urinary retention. Toxidrome?

A) Opioid
B) Anticholinergic
C) Cholinergic
D) Carbon monoxide

**Answer:** B

**Arabic explanation:** Dry + delirious + mydriasis = anticholinergic.

### Question 3

Wet patient with bronchorrhea, miosis, bradycardia after pesticide. Key toxin class?

A) Organophosphate
B) TCA
C) Lithium
D) Isopropanol

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Killer B’s suggest cholinergic crisis.

### Question 4

Cyanosis after benzocaine spray with chocolate-brown blood. Antidote concept?

A) Methylene blue
B) Deferoxamine
C) Naloxone
D) Prussian blue

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Methemoglobinemia.

### Question 5

Alcohol-dependent patient with confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia. Diagnosis?

A) Wernicke encephalopathy
B) Opioid toxidrome
C) NMS
D) MDMA toxicity

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Classic Wernicke pattern.

### Question 6

Poor intake and alcohol use with anion-gap ketosis. Therapy concept?

A) Dextrose-containing fluids
B) Insulin for all
C) Activated charcoal
D) Hyperbaric oxygen

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Alcoholic ketoacidosis.

### Question 7

Miosis, somnolence, respiratory depression that recurs after naloxone in child. Likely exposure?

A) Methadone
B) Atropine
C) MDMA
D) Chlorine

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Long-acting opioid recurrence.

### Question 8

Rave patient agitated, hyperthermic, confused; sodium is low. Likely agent?

A) MDMA
B) Iron
C) CO
D) Superwarfarin

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** MDMA hyponatremia/hyperthermia.

### Question 9

Visual symptoms after toxic alcohol ingestion. Which toxin?

A) Methanol
B) Isopropanol
C) Nicotine
D) Chlorine

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Methanol → formic acid optic toxicity.

### Question 10

Alopecia + painful ascending neuropathy after GI illness. Toxin?

A) Thallium
B) Carbon monoxide
C) GHB
D) Chlorine

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Classic thallium.

### Question 11

Radiopaque iron tablets after overdose. Charcoal?

A) Reliably effective
B) Not useful; consider WBI/deferoxamine concepts when indicated
C) Only antidote
D) Mandatory for caustics

**Answer:** B

**Arabic explanation:** Charcoal does not bind iron well.

### Question 12

Smoke inhalation, coma, shock, severe lactate. Antidote concept?

A) Hydroxocobalamin
B) Vitamin K
C) Physostigmine
D) Naloxone only

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Cyanide concern.

### Question 13

Ketosis without acidosis suggests ingestion of:

A) Isopropanol
B) Methanol
C) Ethylene glycol
D) Salicylate

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Isopropanol → acetone.

### Question 14

Tinnitus, tachypnea, mixed acid-base disorder. Toxin?

A) Salicylate
B) Clonidine
C) GHB
D) Superwarfarin

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Salicylates.

### Question 15

Bradycardia, hyperkalemia, ventricular ectopy after “heart pills.” Antidote concept?

A) Digoxin-specific Fab
B) Atropine only
C) Prussian blue
D) Pyridoxine

**Answer:** A

**Arabic explanation:** Cardiac glycoside toxicity.
