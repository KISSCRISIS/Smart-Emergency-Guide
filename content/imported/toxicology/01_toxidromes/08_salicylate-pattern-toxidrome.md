# Salicylate Pattern

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Salicylates uncouple oxidative phosphorylation and stimulate respiration, classically producing tinnitus, vomiting, hyperventilation, and mixed acid-base disorders.

## ECG / Clinical Clues

Tinnitus, nausea/vomiting, tachypnea, fever, confusion, respiratory alkalosis with metabolic acidosis, pulmonary edema in severe cases.

## Why It Matters in the ED

Avoid intubation unless absolutely necessary and expertly managed; alkalinization/dialysis concepts require toxicology guidance; monitor serial levels and acid-base status.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway loss, respiratory failure, seizures, hyperthermia, shock, or dysrhythmia depending on the toxidrome.
- Delayed recurrence when the exposure is long-acting or mixed.

## Common Trap

Intubating without matching the patient’s high minute ventilation can worsen acidosis and drive salicylate into the CNS.
