# Cholinergic / Organophosphate Toxidrome

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Cholinergic excess produces muscarinic secretions, bronchospasm, bradycardia, plus nicotinic weakness/fasciculations and seizures in severe cases.

## ECG / Clinical Clues

DUMMBBBELS pattern: defecation, urination, miosis, muscle weakness, bronchorrhea, bronchospasm, bradycardia, emesis, lacrimation, salivation.

## Why It Matters in the ED

Protect staff, decontaminate, manage airway/secretions, use atropine/pralidoxime concepts per protocol, treat seizures.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway loss, respiratory failure, seizures, hyperthermia, shock, or dysrhythmia depending on the toxidrome.
- Delayed recurrence when the exposure is long-acting or mixed.

## Common Trap

Using SLUDGE alone misses the killer B’s: bronchorrhea, bronchospasm, bradycardia.
