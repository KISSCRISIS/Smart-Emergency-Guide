# Serotonin Syndrome

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Serotonin toxicity causes mental status change, autonomic instability, and neuromuscular hyperactivity, especially clonus and hyperreflexia.

## ECG / Clinical Clues

Agitation, hyperthermia, diaphoresis, diarrhea, tremor, inducible/spontaneous clonus, lower extremity hyperreflexia.

## Why It Matters in the ED

Stop serotonergic agents, sedate, cool, support fluids, treat severe hyperthermia aggressively, consider cyproheptadine with expert guidance.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway loss, respiratory failure, seizures, hyperthermia, shock, or dysrhythmia depending on the toxidrome.
- Delayed recurrence when the exposure is long-acting or mixed.

## Common Trap

Confusing it with NMS: serotonin syndrome is usually faster and more clonus/hyperreflexia dominant.
