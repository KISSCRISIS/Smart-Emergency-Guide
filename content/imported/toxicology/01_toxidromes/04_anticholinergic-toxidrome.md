# Anticholinergic Toxidrome

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Antimuscarinic toxicity blocks parasympathetic tone and causes delirium with dry, hot skin and large pupils.

## ECG / Clinical Clues

Delirium, mydriasis, dry axillae, urinary retention, tachycardia, hyperthermia, flushed skin, decreased bowel sounds.

## Why It Matters in the ED

Supportive care, control agitation/temperature, consider physostigmine only in selected cases after expert review and ECG assessment.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway loss, respiratory failure, seizures, hyperthermia, shock, or dysrhythmia depending on the toxidrome.
- Delayed recurrence when the exposure is long-acting or mixed.

## Common Trap

Giving physostigmine reflexively when QRS is wide or mixed TCA toxicity is possible.
