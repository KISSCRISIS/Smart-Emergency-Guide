# Wernicke Encephalopathy

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Wernicke encephalopathy is a thiamine-deficiency neurologic emergency, classically with confusion, ataxia, and ocular motor abnormalities, especially in malnutrition or alcohol use disorder.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Confusion without delirium
- Ataxia
- Ophthalmoplegia or nystagmus
- Malnutrition/alcohol use
- May coexist with other alcohol-related disorders

## Why It Matters in the ED

Treat thiamine deficiency early and correct nutrition/electrolytes; do not wait for the full triad.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Missing it because the full classic triad is incomplete.

## Assessment Question

**A malnourished alcohol-dependent patient has confusion, ataxia, and ophthalmoplegia. Diagnosis?**

A) Wernicke encephalopathy
B) Opioid intoxication
C) Serotonin syndrome
D) Anticholinergic delirium

**Correct Answer:** A
