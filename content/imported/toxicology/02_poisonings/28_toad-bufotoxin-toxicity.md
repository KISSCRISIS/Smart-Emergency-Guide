# Toad / Bufotoxin Cardiac Glycoside Toxicity

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Some Bufo toads contain cardioactive steroids that cause digoxin-like toxicity, sometimes with hallucinations from psychoactive compounds.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Exposure to toad venom/licking
- GI symptoms
- Bradycardia, hyperkalemia, ventricular ectopy
- Digoxin assay may be qualitatively positive

## Why It Matters in the ED

Treat as cardiac glycoside physiology; digoxin-specific antibody fragment concept may apply in severe cases with toxicology input.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Using digoxin level quantitatively as if it were pharmaceutical digoxin.

## Assessment Question

**Bufo toad exposure with bradycardia and hyperkalemia resembles which toxicity?**

A) Cardiac glycoside/digoxin-like toxicity
B) Pure opioid toxicity
C) Salicylate toxicity
D) Chlorine gas

**Correct Answer:** A
