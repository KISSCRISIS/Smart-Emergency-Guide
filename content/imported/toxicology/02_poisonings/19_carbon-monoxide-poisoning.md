# Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Carbon monoxide binds hemoglobin and impairs oxygen delivery; pulse oximetry may appear falsely reassuring.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Headache, dizziness, nausea
- Multiple patients from same location
- Fire/heater/generator exposure
- Cherry-red skin is unreliable/rare
- Normal pulse oximeter can mislead

## Why It Matters in the ED

Give high-concentration oxygen, measure carboxyhemoglobin, assess pregnancy/neurologic/cardiac risk, consider hyperbaric discussion in selected severe cases.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Trusting normal SpO2 and missing exposure cluster.

## Assessment Question

**Which finding does NOT rule out CO poisoning?**

A) Normal pulse oximetry
B) Multiple people ill in same house
C) Headache after generator exposure
D) Carboxyhemoglobin elevation

**Correct Answer:** A

**Arabic reasoning:** SpO2 لا يميز جيداً بين oxyhemoglobin وcarboxyhemoglobin.
