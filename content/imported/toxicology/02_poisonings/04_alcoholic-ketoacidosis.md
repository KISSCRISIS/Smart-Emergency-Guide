# Alcoholic Ketoacidosis

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Alcoholic ketoacidosis occurs after poor intake and alcohol use, producing anion-gap acidosis with ketones; glucose may be low, normal, or mildly elevated.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Alcohol use disorder
- Poor oral intake/vomiting
- Tachypnea, dehydration
- Anion gap with ketones
- No major clonus/hyperreflexia pattern

## Why It Matters in the ED

Treatment concept is dextrose-containing fluids plus supportive correction of dehydration and electrolyte problems; insulin is not the primary fix unless another diagnosis requires it.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Mislabeling it as DKA and reflexively starting insulin.

## Assessment Question

**A chronic alcohol user with poor intake has anion-gap ketosis and confusion. Poison center recommends which concept?**

A) Insulin-first strategy for all cases
B) Dextrose-containing crystalloid after evaluation
C) Activated charcoal
D) Immediate dialysis

**Correct Answer:** B

**Arabic reasoning:** السكر والسوائل يعيدان الجسم من وضع الحرق الكيتوني إلى الطاقة الطبيعية.
