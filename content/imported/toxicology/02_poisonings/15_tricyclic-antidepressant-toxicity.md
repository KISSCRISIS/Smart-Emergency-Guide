# Tricyclic Antidepressant Toxicity

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

TCAs cause sodium-channel blockade, anticholinergic effects, seizures, hypotension, and malignant dysrhythmias.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Tachycardia + altered mental status
- Wide QRS
- Terminal R in aVR
- Seizures
- Hypotension
- Anticholinergic signs may coexist

## Why It Matters in the ED

Sodium bicarbonate is the core mechanism-targeted therapy concept for conduction toxicity; manage seizures and shock aggressively.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Cardioverting or choosing antidysrhythmics before addressing sodium-channel blockade.

## Assessment Question

**Which ECG clue supports TCA sodium-channel blockade?**

A) Terminal R wave in aVR with wide QRS
B) Only short QT
C) Isolated U waves
D) Normal ECG rules it out forever

**Correct Answer:** A
