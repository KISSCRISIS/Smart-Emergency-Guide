# Nicotine / Liquid Nicotine Toxicity

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Concentrated nicotine solutions can cause rapid cholinergic-like toxicity with both nicotinic and muscarinic effects; children are especially vulnerable.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Exposure to vape liquid or pesticide
- Early tachycardia/hypertension, dizziness
- Severe cases: bradycardia, hypotension, seizures, respiratory failure
- GI symptoms

## Why It Matters in the ED

Support airway/ventilation, treat seizures/bradycardia per protocols, decontaminate dermal exposure, consult poison center.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Thinking atropine fixes all nicotine toxicity; it helps muscarinic effects but not nicotinic paralysis/shock.

## Assessment Question

**Why is atropine incomplete therapy in severe nicotine toxicity?**

A) It reverses only muscarinic effects, not all nicotinic toxicity
B) It neutralizes nicotine chemically
C) It removes nicotine by dialysis
D) It treats only visual symptoms

**Correct Answer:** A
