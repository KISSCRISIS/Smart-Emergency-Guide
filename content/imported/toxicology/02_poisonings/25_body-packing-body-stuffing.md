# Body Packing / Body Stuffing

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Body packing involves swallowing or inserting drug packets to evade detection; rupture can cause massive overdose, bowel obstruction, or ischemia. Body stuffing is hurried ingestion of smaller less secure packets.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Travel/smuggling context
- Multiple radiopaque packets
- Sympathomimetic crisis if cocaine packet ruptures
- Abdominal pain may suggest ischemia/obstruction
- Asymptomatic packers differ from symptomatic patients

## Why It Matters in the ED

Symptomatic packet rupture or abdominal catastrophe needs urgent surgical/toxicology pathway. Whole-bowel irrigation is a concept for selected asymptomatic cases.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Treating severe symptomatic rupture with observation alone.

## Assessment Question

**A body packer has severe hypertension, tachycardia, chest pain, abdominal pain, and many packets on x-ray. What is the definitive concern?**

A) Packet rupture with severe toxicity/ischemia requiring urgent intervention
B) Simple anxiety only
C) Benign constipation
D) Vitamin overdose

**Correct Answer:** A

**Arabic reasoning:** الأعراض الشديدة مع ألم البطن تعني احتمال تمزق/نقص تروية وليس مجرد مراقبة.
