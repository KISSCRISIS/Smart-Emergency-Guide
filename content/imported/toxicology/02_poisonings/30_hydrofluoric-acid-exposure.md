# Hydrofluoric Acid Exposure

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Hydrofluoric acid causes deep tissue injury and systemic fluoride toxicity by binding calcium and magnesium, creating risk of severe pain, hypocalcemia, dysrhythmia, and shock.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Severe pain out of proportion
- Glass/etching/rust remover/industrial exposure
- Delayed pain possible
- Hypocalcemia/hypomagnesemia risk
- ECG changes/dysrhythmia in severe exposure

## Why It Matters in the ED

Immediate decontamination and calcium-based therapy concepts require urgent toxicology/burn guidance; monitor electrolytes and ECG.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Underestimating a small-appearing burn because skin findings look mild.
