# Chlorine Exposure

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Chlorine is a water-soluble irritant gas/liquid exposure causing mucous membrane, eye, upper airway, and skin irritation; severe exposures can injure the lungs.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Pungent odor
- Eye/skin irritation
- Cough, wheeze, upper airway irritation
- Lab/pool/industrial exposure

## Why It Matters in the ED

Remove contaminated clothing, irrigate exposed skin/eyes, provide oxygen/bronchodilator supportive care, monitor respiratory trajectory.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Intubating mild exposure without clinical signs of severe airway/lung injury, or giving prophylactic antibiotics.

## Assessment Question

**A lab worker spills dilute chlorine with eye irritation and minimal wheeze. What is the best ED concept?**

A) Cutaneous/ocular decontamination and supportive care
B) Methylene blue
C) Prophylactic antibiotics for all
D) Ignore odor and discharge immediately

**Correct Answer:** A

**Arabic reasoning:** التطهير والدعم هما الأساس في التعرض الخفيف.
