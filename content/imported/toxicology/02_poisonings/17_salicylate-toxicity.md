# Salicylate Toxicity

> **SEG Safety Layer**
> This page is for emergency medicine learning and board-style pattern recognition. It is not a local treatment protocol. For real patients, stabilize ABCs, call your local poison center/toxicologist early, and follow local guidelines. Doses, infusion rates, dialysis thresholds, and disposition times must be verified locally before use.


## Definition

Salicylates stimulate respiration and disrupt metabolism, producing tinnitus, vomiting, fever, hyperventilation, and mixed respiratory alkalosis/metabolic acidosis.

## ECG / Clinical Clues
- Tinnitus
- Tachypnea/hyperventilation
- Vomiting, fever, confusion
- Mixed acid-base disorder
- Oil of wintergreen can be highly concentrated

## Why It Matters in the ED

Serial levels and acid-base status matter. Alkalinization and dialysis concepts require expert guidance. Preserve ventilation if intubation unavoidable.

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?

- Airway or ventilation failure.
- Seizure, hyperthermia, shock, malignant dysrhythmia, severe acidosis, or delayed organ injury depending on toxin.
- Recurrence after short-acting reversal or delayed absorption in long-acting/extended-release exposures.

## Common Board Trap

Missing severe salicylate in a tachypneic patient and suppressing ventilation.

B) Pure metabolic alkalosis only
C) Ketosis without acidosis
D) Normal acid-base always
