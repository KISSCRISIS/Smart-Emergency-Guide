# Behavioral & Self-Harm Safety

## Purpose

Help EMS manage scene and patient safety in behavioral emergencies.

## Red Flags

- active suicidal intent
- recent self-harm
- overdose
- psychosis with unsafe behavior
- severe agitation
- violence risk
- intoxication with unsafe behavior
- delirium/medical cause concern
- safeguarding/domestic violence concern
- patient cannot remain safe

## EMS Priorities

- scene safety
- call for additional support according to local policy
- do not manage violent scenes alone
- consider medical/toxicologic causes
- protect patient dignity
- communicate risk clearly to receiving team
- document objective behavior and statements

## What To Communicate

- risk to self/others
- substances/overdose
- medical abnormalities
- injuries
- statements made
- need for security/support
- trend during transport

## Common Prehospital Trap

Assuming agitation is purely psychiatric.
