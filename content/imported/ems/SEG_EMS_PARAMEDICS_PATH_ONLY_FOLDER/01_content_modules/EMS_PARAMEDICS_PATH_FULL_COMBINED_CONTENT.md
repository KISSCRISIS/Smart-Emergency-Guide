# EMS Scene Safety & First Impression

## Purpose

Teach the first prehospital priority: safe approach before patient contact.

## Scene Safety Priorities

Before touching the patient, consider:

- traffic risk
- violence risk
- fire/smoke/electrical danger
- chemical/toxic exposure
- unstable structures
- weapons or crowd risk
- infection risk
- need for additional resources
- number of patients
- mechanism of injury
- escape route

## First Impression

From the doorway/approach, note:

- patient position
- work of breathing
- color
- consciousness
- bleeding
- distress
- environment clues
- bystander information
- number of casualties

## What EMS Should Communicate

- unsafe scene elements
- need for police/fire/additional EMS if relevant
- number of patients
- major mechanism or exposure
- immediate life threats

## Common Prehospital Trap

Rushing to the patient before checking scene safety.

## Arabic Pearl

إذا أصبح المسعف ضحية، خسر المريض والمسعف معًا.

# EMS Primary Survey: ABCDE

## Purpose

Organize prehospital recognition of life threats.

## A — Airway

Look for:
- inability to speak
- obstruction sounds
- vomiting/secretions
- facial/neck trauma
- reduced consciousness
- swelling/allergy concern

## B — Breathing

Look for:
- severe work of breathing
- cyanosis
- exhaustion
- silent chest
- chest injury
- unequal breath sounds
- toxic inhalation concern

## C — Circulation

Look for:
- major bleeding
- shock appearance
- weak/rapid pulse
- pale/clammy skin
- poor perfusion
- chest pain/syncope
- trauma mechanism

## D — Disability

Look for:
- AVPU/GCS trend
- seizure
- focal neurologic deficit
- glucose concern
- pupils
- intoxication/overdose clues

## E — Exposure / Environment

Look for:
- hidden bleeding
- rash
- trauma
- temperature exposure
- burns
- safeguarding concerns

## EMS Role

- recognize life threats
- follow local protocol/medical direction
- communicate deterioration
- reassess continuously
- do not delay transport for nonessential details

## Common Prehospital Trap

Finishing a full history while the patient is unstable.

## Arabic Pearl

ABCDE ليس ورقة حفظ؛ هو طريقة لاكتشاف الخطر قبل التفاصيل.

# Airway & Breathing Danger Recognition

## Purpose

Help EMS recognize airway and breathing danger early.

## Airway Danger Signs

- stridor
- gurgling/secretions
- facial/neck swelling
- burns or inhalation concern
- reduced consciousness
- vomiting with poor airway protection
- severe facial trauma
- anaphylaxis concern

## Breathing Danger Signs

- severe work of breathing
- inability to speak
- cyanosis
- exhaustion
- silent chest
- chest trauma
- suspected pneumothorax
- pulmonary edema features
- severe asthma/COPD features
- toxic inhalation

## What To Communicate

- airway risk
- breathing pattern
- ability to speak
- mental status
- suspected cause
- deterioration trend
- interventions performed according to local protocol
- estimated arrival

## Common Prehospital Trap

Calling dyspnea “anxiety” before checking airway, breathing, and circulation danger.

## Arabic Pearl

ضيق النفس في الميدان يُقاس بمظهر المريض وقدرته على الكلام قبل اسم التشخيص.

# Circulation, Shock & Bleeding Recognition

## Purpose

Help EMS recognize shock and bleeding before late collapse.

## Shock Clues

- pale/clammy skin
- altered mental status
- weak pulse
- severe weakness
- syncope/collapse
- cool extremities
- major mechanism
- chest pain with diaphoresis
- abdominal/back pain with collapse
- sepsis appearance
- anaphylaxis concern

## Bleeding Clues

- obvious external bleeding
- hidden bleeding after trauma
- GI bleeding symptoms
- pregnancy/postpartum bleeding
- anticoagulant use
- pelvic/long-bone trauma
- penetrating injury
- expanding hematoma

## EMS Priorities

- recognize shock early
- control visible bleeding according to local training
- reduce scene delay
- notify receiving ED when unstable
- reassess frequently

## Common Prehospital Trap

Waiting for late vital-sign collapse before calling a patient unstable.

## Arabic Pearl

الصدمة تبدأ من مظهر المريض وترويته، وليس من رقم واحد فقط.

# Chest Pain & ACS Red Flags for EMS

## Purpose

Help EMS identify high-risk chest pain and communicate it early.

## High-Risk Features

- pressure/heaviness
- sweating
- shortness of breath
- syncope/near syncope
- radiation to arm/jaw/back/epigastrium
- ongoing or recurrent pain
- abnormal ECG concern
- known cardiac disease
- diabetes/elderly/atypical presentation
- stimulant use
- shock appearance

## Danger Diagnoses To Consider

- ACS/STEMI-equivalent concern
- aortic dissection
- pulmonary embolism
- pneumothorax
- pericardial tamponade concern
- severe arrhythmia

## What To Communicate

- onset time
- pain character/radiation
- associated symptoms
- ECG concern if available
- hemodynamic status
- risk factors
- trend during transport

## Common Prehospital Trap

Reassuring because pain improved before arrival.

## Arabic Pearl

ألم الصدر في الإسعاف يُنقل معه الزمن والأعراض والتخطيط إن وجد.

# Stroke & Neurologic Emergencies

## Purpose

Help EMS recognize time-sensitive neurologic danger.

## Red Flags

- facial droop
- arm/leg weakness
- speech disturbance
- visual loss
- severe sudden headache
- seizure
- reduced consciousness
- new confusion
- dizziness with neurologic signs
- anticoagulant use with head injury
- hypoglycemia mimic concern

## EMS Priorities

- identify last-known-well time
- check glucose if available and within scope
- note deficits clearly
- reduce delay
- notify receiving ED/stroke pathway according to local policy
- document symptom timeline

## What To Communicate

- last known well
- symptoms and deficits
- baseline status if known
- anticoagulant use
- seizure/head trauma
- glucose status if available
- trend during transport

## Common Prehospital Trap

Ignoring symptoms because they improved.

## Arabic Pearl

في الجلطة، الزمن جزء من التشخيص؛ انقله بدقة.

- airway/breathing concern
- shock appearance
- severe bleeding
- altered mental status
- chest pain/dyspnea after trauma
- abdominal pain/tenderness
- pelvic pain/instability concern
- neurologic deficit
- severe pain out of proportion
- burns/inhalation concern

## What To Communicate

- mechanism
- number of patients

- vitals/trends if available
- interventions under protocol
- estimated arrival
- need for trauma team according to local policy

# ECG Danger Recognition for EMS

## Purpose

Teach EMS-level ECG danger recognition, not advanced cardiology interpretation.

## ECG Concerns To Escalate

- ST elevation or STEMI-equivalent concern
- new ischemic changes with symptoms
- wide complex tachycardia
- severe bradycardia with symptoms
- complete heart block
- rapid irregular rhythm with instability
- long QT with syncope/seizure concern
- hyperkalemia pattern concern
- toxicology-related ECG abnormality concern
- paced rhythm with concerning symptoms
- syncope with abnormal ECG pattern

## EMS ECG Principles

- ECG must match symptoms.
- A normal-looking ECG does not automatically make chest pain safe.
- Serial changes matter if available.
- Transmit/communicate according to local policy.
- Do not delay urgent transport for perfect interpretation.

## What To Communicate

- ECG concern
- symptoms
- onset time
- vital stability
- rhythm changes
- transmission status if applicable
- estimated arrival

## Common Prehospital Trap

Calling a symptomatic wide-complex rhythm benign too quickly.

## Arabic Pearl

التخطيط قبل المستشفى قيمته في تسريع التعرف على الخطر والتواصل مع الطوارئ.

# Handover to ED: ATMIST / MIST

## Purpose

Teach concise prehospital-to-ED handover.

## MIST

- Mechanism / Medical complaint
- Injuries / Illness features
- Signs / vital trends
- Treatment / actions under protocol

## ATMIST

- Age
- Time of incident/onset
- Mechanism / Medical complaint
- Injuries / Illness features
- Signs / vital trends
- Treatment / actions under protocol

## Good Handover Example

“This is a [age] patient with [complaint/mechanism]. Onset/incident time was [time]. Main red flags are [features]. Current status is [appearance/vitals/trend]. We are concerned about [danger]. Actions performed under local protocol include [summary]. ETA is [time].”

## Include

- onset/incident time
- trend during transport
- red flags
- interventions under protocol
- response to interventions
- allergies/medications if relevant
- safety concerns

## Common Prehospital Trap

Giving a long story but missing onset time and current stability.

## Arabic Pearl

التسليم الجيد يعطي الطوارئ: من؟ ماذا حدث؟ متى؟ ما الخطر؟ وما تغيّر؟

# Infection Prevention & PPE for EMS

## Purpose

Teach infection prevention in prehospital environments.

## Core Behaviors

- hand hygiene when possible
- appropriate PPE
- gloves do not replace hand hygiene
- eye/face protection when splash risk exists
- safe sharps handling
- equipment cleaning
- ambulance surface cleaning according to policy
- respiratory precautions when relevant
- needle-stick/exposure reporting
- avoid contaminating radio/phone/steering wheel

## EMS Challenges

- uncontrolled environment
- limited space
- urgent care
- multiple patients
- unknown infectious risk
- weather and crowd pressure

## Common Prehospital Trap

Wearing gloves everywhere but contaminating equipment and surfaces.

## Arabic Pearl

في الإسعاف، العدوى تنتقل بسرعة لأن المكان صغير والضغط عالي.

# Mass Casualty / Disaster Basics

## Purpose

Give EMS learners a non-protocol overview of disaster thinking.

## Key Concepts

- scene safety first
- incident command according to local system
- communication discipline
- resource awareness
- triage according to local policy/training
- do not self-deploy outside system
- identify hazards
- protect responders
- distribute patients according to system direction
- documentation and accountability

## What EMS Should Learn

- one rescuer cannot manage the scene alone
- communication is treatment
- triage systems are local/protocol-driven
- personal safety and PPE matter
- transport decisions depend on system coordination

## Common Prehospital Trap

Trying to help without integrating into command structure.

## Arabic Pearl

في الكوارث، التنظيم ينقذ أكثر من الحماس الفردي.

# EMS-to-Intern Interface

## Purpose

Help EMS and ED interns understand each other’s needs.

## What EMS Brings

- scene story
- timeline
- first impression
- patient trend
- mechanism
- bystander information
- home medications/containers
- ECG or vitals if available
- response to protocol-based actions
- safety concerns

## What Interns Need

- onset/last known well
- instability signs
- red flags
- what changed
- what was done
- what was found at scene
- medications/allergies if known
- family/bystander contact information if available

## Best Interface

EMS gives a structured handover.  
Intern listens, clarifies critical missing data, and escalates if unstable.

## Common Trap

ED team ignores EMS timeline; EMS handover becomes too vague.

## Arabic Pearl

المسعف يرى بداية القصة؛ الطبيب في الطوارئ يحتاج هذه البداية ليكمل القرار.
