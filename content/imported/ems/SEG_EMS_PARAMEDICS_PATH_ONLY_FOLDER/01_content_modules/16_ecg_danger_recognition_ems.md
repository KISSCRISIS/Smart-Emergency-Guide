# ECG Danger Recognition for EMS

## Purpose

Teach EMS-level ECG danger recognition, not advanced cardiology interpretation.

## ECG Concerns To Escalate

- ST elevation or STEMI-equivalent concern
- new ischemic changes with symptoms
- wide complex tachycardia
- severe bradycardia with symptoms
- complete heart block
- rapid irregular rhythm with instability
- long QT with syncope/seizure concern
- hyperkalemia pattern concern
- toxicology-related ECG abnormality concern
- paced rhythm with concerning symptoms
- syncope with abnormal ECG pattern

## EMS ECG Principles

- ECG must match symptoms.
- A normal-looking ECG does not automatically make chest pain safe.
- Serial changes matter if available.
- Transmit/communicate according to local policy.
- Do not delay urgent transport for perfect interpretation.

## What To Communicate

- ECG concern
- symptoms
- onset time
- vital stability
- rhythm changes
- transmission status if applicable
- estimated arrival

## Common Prehospital Trap

Calling a symptomatic wide-complex rhythm benign too quickly.
