# Pediatric Prehospital Red Flags

## Purpose

Help EMS recognize sick children early.

## Red Flags

- poor feeding in infant
- lethargy or reduced interaction
- respiratory distress
- cyanosis
- apnea
- seizure
- non-blanching rash
- dehydration concern
- reduced urine
- bilious vomiting
- persistent vomiting with drowsiness
- fever in very young infant
- trauma/safeguarding concern
- caregiver says child is not normal

## What To Notice

- age
- behavior compared with baseline
- work of breathing
- color
- hydration
- crying/interaction
- caregiver concern
- rash
- trauma signs

## What To Communicate

- age/weight estimate if relevant to local protocol
- appearance
- feeding/urine
- breathing effort
- mental status
- caregiver concern
- red flags
- trend during transport

## Common Prehospital Trap

Reassuring because the child briefly looks better.
