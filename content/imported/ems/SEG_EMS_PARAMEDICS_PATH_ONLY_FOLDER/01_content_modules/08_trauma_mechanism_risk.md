# Trauma & Mechanism-Based Risk

## Purpose

Teach EMS to combine mechanism, physiology, and exam.

## High-Risk Mechanisms / Context

- high-speed collision
- ejection/rollover
- pedestrian/cyclist struck
- fall from height
- penetrating trauma
- blast/crush injury
- motorcycle crash
- elderly fall with anticoagulant use
- child injury with safeguarding concern
- multiple casualties

## Red Flags

- airway/breathing concern
- shock appearance
- severe bleeding
- altered mental status
- chest pain/dyspnea after trauma
- abdominal pain/tenderness
- pelvic pain/instability concern
- neurologic deficit
- severe pain out of proportion
- burns/inhalation concern

## What To Communicate

- mechanism
- number of patients
- injuries found
- vitals/trends if available
- interventions under protocol
- estimated arrival
- need for trauma team according to local policy

## Common Prehospital Trap

Judging severity by visible wounds only.
