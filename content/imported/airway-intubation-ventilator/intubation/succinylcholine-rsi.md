# Succinylcholine for RSI


## Definition
Succinylcholine is a depolarizing neuromuscular blocker used for rapid paralysis in RSI.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It acts rapidly but can cause dangerous hyperkalemia in specific conditions.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- burns after the vulnerable time window
- denervation syndromes
- myopathies/multiple sclerosis
- preexisting hyperkalemia
- malignant hyperthermia/masseter spasm concern

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Typical educational RSI paralysis draft: succinylcholine 1–1.5 mg/kg IV.
- Rosen-based concept: denervation syndromes carry severe hyperkalemia risk; burn/trauma/stroke/spinal cord injury risk becomes clinically important after several days, often taught around day 5.
- Avoid when contraindications exist; use local protocol alternative.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Screen contraindications before paralysis.
2. Have alternative neuromuscular blocker ready.
3. Do not repeat blindly if masseter spasm occurs; consider malignant hyperthermia pathway.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.
