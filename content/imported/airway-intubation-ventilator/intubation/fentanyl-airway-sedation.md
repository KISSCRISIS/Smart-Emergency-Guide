# Fentanyl in Airway and Sedation


## Definition
Fentanyl is a potent opioid analgesic used for pain control and as part of sedation strategies.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It reduces pain and sympathetic response but can cause respiratory depression, especially with rapid or excessive dosing.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- painful procedure before sedation
- respiratory depression after opioid
- chest wall rigidity with large rapid boluses
- elderly or comorbid patient

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Rosen-based procedural sedation draft: fentanyl 1–2 mcg/kg IV can provide analgesia before titrated sedative dosing.
- Rosen-based warning: rapid large IV boluses increase risk of respiratory depression/arrest; severe chest wall rigidity is associated with very large rapid boluses and often requires paralysis to ventilate.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Dose/titrate according to local protocol and patient risk.
2. Monitor ventilation with capnography when sedating.
3. Be prepared for airway support and opioid reversal when indicated.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.
