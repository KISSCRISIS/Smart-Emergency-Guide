# Cannot Intubate Cannot Oxygenate


## Definition
CICO is the emergency state where neither intubation nor noninvasive oxygenation/ventilation is maintaining oxygenation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
This is a time-critical pathway to front-of-neck access according to trained local practice.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- no chest rise with BVM/SAD
- rapid desaturation
- failed attempts with airway trauma/edema
- no effective oxygen delivery path

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Announce CICO clearly.
2. Stop ineffective attempts.
3. Activate local front-of-neck access protocol.
4. Continue best oxygenation attempt until access is established.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to cannot intubate cannot oxygenate?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
