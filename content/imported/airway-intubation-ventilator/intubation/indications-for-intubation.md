# Indications for Intubation


## Definition
Intubation is considered for oxygenation failure, ventilation failure, airway protection failure, or predicted deterioration/need for controlled airway.

## Why It Matters in the ED
The tube should solve a clear physiologic problem, not satisfy anxiety from an abnormal number alone.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- persistent hypoxemia
- hypercapnic fatigue
- shock requiring controlled airway for procedure/transport
- airway edema/burns/angioedema progression
- GCS alone is not the whole decision

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Name the indication before the attempt.
2. Decide whether NIV/HFNC/BVM bridge is safer first.
3. Prepare for post-intubation physiology.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to indications for intubation?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
