# Awake Intubation Concept


## Definition
A strategy to secure a predicted difficult airway while maintaining spontaneous breathing and airway tone.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Paralyzing a patient with a predicted impossible airway may remove the only thing keeping them alive.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- severe angioedema
- airway tumor or distortion
- critical obstruction but breathing spontaneously
- anticipated cannot intubate/cannot ventilate if asleep

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Call expert airway/anesthesia/ENT early.
2. Prepare topicalization/sedation according to local protocol.
3. Maintain spontaneous ventilation.
4. Have surgical airway backup.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to awake intubation concept?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
