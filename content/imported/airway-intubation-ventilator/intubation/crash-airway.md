# Crash Airway


## Definition
A situation where the patient is peri-arrest or unresponsive and immediate airway intervention is needed with minimal time for standard preparation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Delays can kill, but even crash airways require backup oxygenation and confirmation.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- cardiac arrest
- agonal respirations
- profound hypoxemia with loss of consciousness
- imminent airway loss

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Call for help and assign roles.
2. Ventilate/oxygenate immediately.
3. Use fastest available skilled approach.
4. Confirm tube and reassess circulation.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to crash airway?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
