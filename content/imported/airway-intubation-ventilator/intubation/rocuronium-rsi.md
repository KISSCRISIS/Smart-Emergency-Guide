# Rocuronium for RSI


## Definition
Rocuronium is a nondepolarizing neuromuscular blocker used as an RSI paralytic alternative to succinylcholine.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It avoids succinylcholine hyperkalemia risk but has longer paralysis, making post-intubation sedation essential.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- succinylcholine contraindication
- need for longer paralysis awareness
- must sedate after intubation
- pediatric masseter spasm rescue concept

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Typical educational RSI paralysis draft: rocuronium 1.0–1.2 mg/kg IV for RSI.
- Rosen-based concept: competitive neuromuscular blockers such as rocuronium can terminate succinylcholine-associated masseter spasm.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Plan sedation immediately after paralysis.
2. Do not mistake paralysis for comfort.
3. Use local reversal/monitoring policies where relevant.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.
