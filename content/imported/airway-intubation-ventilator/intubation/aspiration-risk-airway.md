# Aspiration Risk During Airway Management


## Definition
Risk that gastric contents, blood, or secretions enter the lungs during airway management.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Aspiration can worsen oxygenation, cause pneumonitis/pneumonia, and complicate ventilation.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- vomiting
- bowel obstruction
- pregnancy/obesity
- altered mental status
- trauma/bleeding airway

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Suction ready before induction.
2. Position appropriately when safe.
3. Use RSI strategy when indicated by local practice.
4. Do not let aspiration fear delay a life-saving airway when oxygenation is failing.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to aspiration risk during airway management?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
