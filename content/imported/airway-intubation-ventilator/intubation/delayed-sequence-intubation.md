# Delayed Sequence Intubation Concept


## Definition
DSI uses dissociative sedation to allow preoxygenation and preparation in an agitated patient before paralysis/intubation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Some patients cannot tolerate preoxygenation but will crash if paralyzed while severely hypoxemic.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- agitated hypoxemic patient pulling mask
- severe shunt physiology
- need to tolerate NIV/HFNC/preoxygenation
- ketamine-suitable physiology

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Educational draft: ketamine is commonly used for DSI-style dissociation; exact dosing must follow local protocol.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use only with full airway readiness.
2. Preoxygenate after dissociation.
3. Proceed to RSI only when oxygen reserve and team are ready.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to delayed sequence intubation concept?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
