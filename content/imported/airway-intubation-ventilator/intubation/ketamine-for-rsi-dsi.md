# Ketamine for RSI and DSI


## Definition
Ketamine is a dissociative induction/sedation agent with analgesic properties and relatively preserved respiratory drive at dissociative doses.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It is useful in selected ED airways, especially when analgesia and maintenance of blood pressure are desirable, but it is not risk-free.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- reactive airway disease
- shock where sympathetic tone may help
- agitated hypoxemic patient needing DSI-style preoxygenation
- secretions/emergence phenomena considerations

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Typical educational RSI induction draft: ketamine 1–2 mg/kg IV.
- Typical dissociative sedation draft for DSI/procedural sedation: institution-specific; final dosing must follow local policy.
- Rosen-based concept: ketamine supports blood pressure via catecholamine release and has bronchodilatory advantages; routine benzodiazepine coadministration to prevent emergence in children is discouraged.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use when physiology fits and local protocol supports it.
2. Have suction ready due to secretions.
3. Do not rely on it as a complete airway plan.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to ketamine for rsi and dsi?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
