# Massive Hemoptysis Airway


## Definition
Large-volume airway bleeding threatens oxygenation by flooding alveoli and obstructing visualization.

## Why It Matters in the ED
The immediate killer is asphyxiation, not blood loss alone.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- active coughing blood
- desaturation
- one-lung source suspected
- poor visualization
- hemodynamic instability

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Suction and position bleeding lung down if known and appropriate.
2. Call anesthesia/ICU/pulmonary/IR/surgery early.
3. Prepare large-bore tube strategy according to local expertise.
4. Protect the nonbleeding lung when possible.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to massive hemoptysis airway?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
