# Failed Airway Algorithm Draft


## Definition
A structured escalation pathway when the initial intubation attempt fails or oxygenation becomes unsafe.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Repeated attempts without changing strategy cause hypoxemia, trauma, edema, and death.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- falling SpO₂
- poor view despite attempt
- blood/secretions blocking view
- inability to ventilate between attempts
- operator fixation

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Rosen/DAS-style educational draft: after a failed attempt, stop, oxygenate, improve conditions, change device/operator/position, limit attempts, then move to supraglottic rescue or front-of-neck access if oxygenation fails.
- Final algorithm must match local difficult-airway policy.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Declare difficulty early.
2. Return to oxygenation between attempts.
3. Change something meaningful before another attempt.
4. Escalate to rescue airway/front-of-neck access if oxygenation cannot be maintained.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to failed airway algorithm draft?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
