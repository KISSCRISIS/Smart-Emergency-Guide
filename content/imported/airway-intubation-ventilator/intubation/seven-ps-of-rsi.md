# The 7 Ps of RSI


## Definition
A structured RSI sequence: preparation, preoxygenation, pretreatment/optimization, paralysis with induction, positioning/protection, placement, and post-intubation care.

## Why It Matters in the ED
RSI errors happen when clinicians focus on laryngoscopy and forget physiology, preparation, and aftercare.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- unprepared post-intubation hypotension
- no backup plan
- no suction ready
- no sedation plan after paralysis

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Prepare team/equipment/drugs/backup.
2. Optimize oxygenation and circulation.
3. Induce/paralyze only when plan and rescue are ready.
4. Confirm and manage immediately after intubation.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to the 7 ps of rsi?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
