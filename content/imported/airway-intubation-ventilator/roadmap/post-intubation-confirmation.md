# Post-Intubation Confirmation


## Definition
Verification that the endotracheal tube is in the trachea and remains there after movement and ventilation changes.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Unrecognized esophageal intubation is catastrophic and auscultation alone is not reliable enough.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- persistent waveform ETCO₂ after several breaths
- bilateral chest rise and breath sounds as adjuncts
- tube depth and chest x-ray for position after confirmation
- deterioration after movement suggests displacement

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Rosen-based educational point: ETCO₂ detection is the most reliable listed method for confirming tube placement; direct visualization with fiberoptic scope is gold standard but not routinely required.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use continuous waveform capnography whenever available.
2. Do not rely on chest auscultation alone.
3. Reconfirm after transport, transfer, or sudden deterioration.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to post-intubation confirmation?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
