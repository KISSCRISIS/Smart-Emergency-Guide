# BVM Thinking and Mask Seal


## Definition
Bag-valve-mask ventilation is a rescue skill for oxygenation and ventilation before, during, or after definitive airway attempts.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Failure to ventilate with BVM is often more dangerous than failure to intubate on the first attempt.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- poor chest rise
- large leak around mask
- facial hair or edentulous anatomy
- gastric insufflation from excessive pressure

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use two-person technique early.
2. Optimize mask seal and airway position.
3. Add oral/nasal airway adjuncts when appropriate.
4. Ventilate gently enough to see chest rise while avoiding overventilation.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to bvm thinking and mask seal?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
