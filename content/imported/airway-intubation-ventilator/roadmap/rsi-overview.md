# RSI Overview


## Definition
Rapid sequence intubation combines preparation, physiologic optimization, induction, paralysis, laryngoscopy, tube confirmation, and post-intubation care.

## Why It Matters in the ED
RSI is not just giving two drugs; it is a high-risk resuscitation sequence.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- full stomach/aspiration risk
- shock physiology
- need for paralysis for first-pass success
- backup plan already prepared

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Prepare equipment, people, plan A/B/C.
2. Optimize oxygenation, perfusion, position, and suction.
3. Choose drugs based on physiology and contraindications.
4. Confirm tube with waveform ETCO₂ and reassess hemodynamics.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to rsi overview?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
