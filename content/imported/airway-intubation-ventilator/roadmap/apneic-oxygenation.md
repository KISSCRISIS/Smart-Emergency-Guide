# Apneic Oxygenation


## Definition
Delivery of oxygen during apnea, often by nasal cannula or high-flow nasal oxygen, to slow desaturation during laryngoscopy.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It can buy time during intubation, but it does not ventilate or remove CO₂.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- high desaturation risk
- anticipated difficult laryngoscopy
- hypoxemic respiratory failure
- pediatric patients with rapid oxygen consumption

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use as an adjunct, not a substitute for ventilation.
2. Keep oxygen running during attempts when feasible.
3. Move to ventilation/rescue if oxygenation fails.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to apneic oxygenation?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
