# Basic Airway Maneuvers


## Definition
Noninvasive maneuvers that open the upper airway: repositioning, head tilt/chin lift when appropriate, jaw thrust, suction, and adjuncts.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Many crashing patients need immediate airway opening before medications, imaging, or definitive intubation.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- snoring respirations
- tongue obstruction
- pooling secretions
- trauma with need for jaw thrust rather than neck movement

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Reposition first when safe.
2. Suction aggressively when blood/vomit/secretions obscure the airway.
3. Use adjuncts if tolerated and appropriate.
4. Escalate if oxygenation or ventilation remains inadequate.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to basic airway maneuvers?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
