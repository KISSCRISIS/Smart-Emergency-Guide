# Airway Protection: Not Just the Gag Reflex


## Definition
Airway protection means maintaining patency and preventing aspiration by clearing secretions, swallowing, coughing, and sustaining adequate consciousness.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Misreading the gag reflex can lead to unnecessary intubation or false reassurance.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- swallowing on command
- cough strength
- voice quality and phonation
- secretion burden
- vomiting or active bleeding

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Assess function, not one reflex.
2. Watch trend: stable, improving, or losing airway skills.
3. Prepare suction and positioning early.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to airway protection: not just the gag reflex?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
