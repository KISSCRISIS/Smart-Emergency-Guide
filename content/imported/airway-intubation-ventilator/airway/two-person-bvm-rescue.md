# Two-Person BVM Rescue


## Definition
A rescue ventilation technique using one operator for mask seal/jaw lift and another for bagging.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It improves seal and ventilation in difficult mask situations and buys time before definitive airway.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- poor seal
- desaturation during attempts
- facial hair/edentulous patient
- obesity or airway obstruction

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Call for a second operator early.
2. Use two-handed mask seal with jaw thrust.
3. Add adjuncts and PEEP valve if appropriate.
4. Avoid excessive ventilation pressures.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to two-person bvm rescue?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
