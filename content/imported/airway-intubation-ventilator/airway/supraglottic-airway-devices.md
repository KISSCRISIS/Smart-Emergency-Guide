# Supraglottic Airway Devices


## Definition
Devices placed above the glottis to provide rescue oxygenation/ventilation when BVM or intubation is failing or as a bridge.

## Why It Matters in the ED
A supraglottic airway can rescue oxygenation while the team prepares the next step.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- failed direct/video laryngoscopy
- need for bridge ventilation
- cannot maintain mask seal
- cardiac arrest airway strategy

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use when local algorithm supports it.
2. Confirm chest rise and capnography when possible.
3. Do not let a functioning rescue device delay definitive plan if aspiration/anatomy requires more.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to supraglottic airway devices?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
