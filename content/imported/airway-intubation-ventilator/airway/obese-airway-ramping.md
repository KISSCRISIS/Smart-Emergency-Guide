# Obese Airway and Ramping


## Definition
Obesity increases aspiration risk, desaturation risk, positioning difficulty, and post-intubation ventilation challenges.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Poor positioning and under-preoxygenation in obesity can cause rapid desaturation and failed laryngoscopy.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- high BMI/large neck
- OSA/snoring history
- GERD/aspiration risk
- supine intolerance
- need for ramped position

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Rosen/Tintinalli-supported concept: ramping aligns the external auditory meatus with the sternal notch and improves laryngeal exposure in obese patients.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Ramped or head-up position when safe.
2. Use aggressive preoxygenation and apneic oxygenation.
3. Prepare difficult mask and backup airway equipment.
4. Plan post-intubation ventilation carefully.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.
