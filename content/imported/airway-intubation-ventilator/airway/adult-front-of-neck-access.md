# Adult Front-of-Neck Access Concept


## Definition
Emergency front-of-neck access is the rescue pathway when oxygenation cannot be maintained by mask, supraglottic device, or intubation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Delayed declaration of cannot intubate/cannot oxygenate is fatal.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- failed oxygenation despite optimized BVM and supraglottic attempts
- repeated failed intubation attempts
- rapidly falling saturation with no ventilation path
- airway obstruction not bypassed from above

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Declare CICO early according to local algorithm.
2. Stop repeated ineffective attempts.
3. Move to trained team/front-of-neck access pathway.
4. Continue oxygenation attempts while preparing.

## Common Trap
Waiting until profound hypoxic arrest before declaring failed oxygenation.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to adult front-of-neck access concept?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
