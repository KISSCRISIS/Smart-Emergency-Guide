# Extubation and Weaning Readiness Concept


## Definition
A conceptual readiness assessment for reducing ventilator support and considering extubation in appropriate monitored settings.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Premature extubation fails; delayed liberation exposes patients to ventilator harms.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- improved cause of respiratory failure
- adequate mental status and secretion control
- stable gas exchange
- acceptable work of breathing
- hemodynamic stability

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use local ICU/ED observation protocol.
2. Assess airway protection and respiratory mechanics.
3. Have reintubation risk plan.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to extubation and weaning readiness concept?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
