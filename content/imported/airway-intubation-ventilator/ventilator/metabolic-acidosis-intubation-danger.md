# Metabolic Acidosis and Intubation Danger


## Definition
Severe metabolic acidosis patients often maintain survival by very high minute ventilation; intubation can abruptly remove compensation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
If post-intubation ventilation does not match pre-intubation compensation, pH can fall and the patient can arrest.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- DKA, salicylate poisoning, renal failure, severe lactic acidosis
- Kussmaul breathing
- very low bicarbonate
- high respiratory rate before RSI

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Avoid intubation unless clearly necessary.
2. If necessary, plan to preserve high minute ventilation as locally guided.
3. Use blood gas/ETCO₂ trends and expert help.

## Common Trap
Paralyzing a patient with severe acidosis and then setting a normal minute ventilation.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to metabolic acidosis and intubation danger?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
