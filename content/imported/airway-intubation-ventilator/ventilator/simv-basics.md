# SIMV Basics


## Definition
Synchronized intermittent mandatory ventilation provides set mandatory breaths while allowing spontaneous breaths between them.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Learners often confuse it with full support; spontaneous breaths may need pressure support.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- mandatory breath rate
- spontaneous breaths between mandatory breaths
- possible increased work if support insufficient

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Know whether patient receives adequate support.
2. Monitor work of breathing and fatigue.
3. Use according to ICU/local ventilator strategy.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to simv basics?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
