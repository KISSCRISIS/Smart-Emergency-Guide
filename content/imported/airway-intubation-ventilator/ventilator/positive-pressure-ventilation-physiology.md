# Positive-Pressure Ventilation Physiology


## Definition
Positive-pressure ventilation pushes gas into the lungs and changes intrathoracic pressure, venous return, cardiac output, and lung mechanics.

## Why It Matters in the ED
The ventilator can save breathing but worsen circulation or cause barotrauma if misunderstood.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- hypotension after intubation
- RV failure/pulmonary hypertension
- air trapping
- high airway pressure
- asynchrony

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Check blood pressure after starting PPV.
2. Think preload/RV/auto-PEEP when shock appears.
3. Use lung-protective thinking when injury risk exists.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to positive-pressure ventilation physiology?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
