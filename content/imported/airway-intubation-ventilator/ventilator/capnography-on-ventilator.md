# Capnography on the Ventilator


## Definition
Continuous ETCO₂ monitoring helps confirm tube placement, trend ventilation, and detect abrupt airway/circulation changes.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Loss or change of waveform may signal displacement, obstruction, low perfusion, or ventilation change.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- persistent waveform after intubation
- sudden loss of ETCO₂
- rising ETCO₂ with hypoventilation
- low ETCO₂ with poor perfusion

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use waveform trend, not single number only.
2. Interpret with ventilation and perfusion context.
3. Reconfirm tube with changes.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to capnography on the ventilator?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
