# Post-Intubation Hypotension


## Definition
Hypotension after intubation can be caused by induction drugs, reduced venous return from PPV, auto-PEEP, tension pneumothorax, or uncorrected shock.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It is a common pathway to peri-intubation cardiac arrest.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- hypotension minutes after tube
- high intrathoracic pressure
- shock before induction
- obstructive lung disease
- sedative bolus

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Check tube/circuit and ventilator pressures.
2. Think preload, PPV, pneumothorax, auto-PEEP, drug effect.
3. Treat immediately reversible causes and support circulation by local protocol.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to post-intubation hypotension?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
