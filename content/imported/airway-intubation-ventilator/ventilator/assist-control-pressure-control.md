# Assist-Control Pressure Control


## Definition
A mode where breaths are delivered to a set inspiratory pressure, with tidal volume varying by compliance/resistance.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Pressure is controlled but ventilation may fall if compliance worsens.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- variable tidal volume
- pressure limit set
- compliance-dependent ventilation
- useful when pressure control is desired

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Watch delivered Vt and minute ventilation.
2. Reassess blood gas/ETCO₂.
3. Do not assume pressure control guarantees adequate ventilation.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to assist-control pressure control?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
