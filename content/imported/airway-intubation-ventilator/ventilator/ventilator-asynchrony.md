# Ventilator Asynchrony


## Definition
Mismatch between patient effort and ventilator breath delivery.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Asynchrony increases work of breathing, distress, oxygen consumption, and can worsen ventilation.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- patient fighting ventilator
- tachypnea despite support
- double triggering
- ineffective efforts
- agitation or pain

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Treat pain, anxiety, hypoxemia, hypercapnia, and tube issues.
2. Adjust ventilator settings with experienced support.
3. Avoid reflexively deepening sedation without diagnosing the mismatch.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to ventilator asynchrony?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
