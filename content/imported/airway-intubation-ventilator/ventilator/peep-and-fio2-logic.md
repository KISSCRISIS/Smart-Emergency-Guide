# PEEP and FiO2 Logic


## Definition
FiO₂ changes oxygen concentration; PEEP maintains alveolar recruitment and increases functional residual capacity.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Oxygenation failure often needs recruitment, but PEEP can worsen hemodynamics.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- persistent hypoxemia despite high FiO₂
- atelectasis/ARDS/pulmonary edema
- hypotension after PEEP increase
- RV failure sensitivity

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Rosen-based concept: PEEP primarily increases functional residual capacity by keeping injured/flooded alveoli open and can improve oxygenation; it may decrease cardiac output.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Titrate oxygen down after stabilization to avoid unnecessary hyperoxia.
2. Use PEEP thoughtfully in shock/RV failure.
3. Reassess after changes.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to peep and fio2 logic?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
