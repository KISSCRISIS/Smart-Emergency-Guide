# Sudden Desaturation on the Ventilator


## Definition
A rapid fall in oxygen saturation after intubation/ventilation requires immediate patient-tube-machine assessment.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Tube displacement, obstruction, pneumothorax, equipment failure, and disease progression can kill quickly.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- loss of ETCO₂ waveform
- high or low pressure alarm
- unequal breath sounds
- hypotension
- cyanosis/agitation

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Increase oxygen and assess patient immediately.
2. Check tube position and circuit.
3. Consider DOPE: displacement, obstruction, pneumothorax, equipment.
4. Hand ventilate if machine/circuit concern exists.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to sudden desaturation on the ventilator?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
