# ARDS Lung-Protective Ventilation


## Definition
A strategy to limit ventilator-induced lung injury in ARDS by using low tidal volumes and controlling pressures.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Overdistension and high pressures worsen lung injury and mortality.

## First 60-Second Impression
Look at mental status, work of breathing, oxygen saturation trend, capnography if available, hemodynamics, and whether the patient can protect and clear the airway.

## Key ED Clues
- bilateral infiltrates
- severe hypoxemia
- poor compliance
- rising plateau pressure
- high FiO₂/PEEP needs

## Rosen-Based Educational Draft
- Educational ARDS draft: tidal volume around 6 mL/kg predicted body weight and plateau pressure target ≤30 cm H₂O are classic lung-protective anchors.
- Adjust PEEP/FiO₂, oxygenation targets, permissive hypercapnia, proning, and paralysis only by protocol/specialist guidance.

**Final status:** Requires final local protocol/formulary verification before bedside or public clinical use.

## Conceptual First ED Actions
1. Use PBW-based low tidal volume.
2. Measure plateau pressure when appropriate.
3. Avoid chasing normal CO₂ at the expense of dangerous pressures.

## Common Trap
Treating the monitor number without asking whether the patient is oxygenating, ventilating, and perfusing.

## Assessment Question
**A patient requires emergency airway support. What is the safest first principle related to ards lung-protective ventilation?**

A. Delay airway planning until all laboratory tests return.
B. Identify the immediate physiologic threat, optimize oxygenation/ventilation, and use local protocol for definitive intervention.
C. Rely on one physical sign alone to decide all airway management.
D. Ignore hemodynamics once the tube is placed.

**Correct Answer:** B
