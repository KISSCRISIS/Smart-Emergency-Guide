# Intervals: PR, QRS, QT

## Core Definition
The PR interval reflects atrioventricular conduction time, the QRS duration reflects ventricular depolarization time, and the QT interval reflects total ventricular depolarization and repolarization time. QT should be interpreted as corrected QT (QTc) because it varies with heart rate.

## ECG Recognition Clues
PR > 200 ms suggests first-degree AV block. QRS ≥ 120 ms suggests bundle branch block, ventricular rhythm, paced rhythm, preexcitation or metabolic/drug conduction delay. Prolonged QTc increases risk of polymorphic VT and sudden death.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Intervals are the hidden danger zone. A patient may look stable while the ECG shows long QT, wide QRS toxicity, high-grade block or preexcitation.

## Common Trap
Do not trust automated QTc blindly. Confirm the end of the T wave manually when clinical risk is high or the tracing is noisy.

## Assessment Question
Which finding is most concerning for risk of malignant ventricular arrhythmia?
A) PR interval 160 ms
B) QTc markedly prolonged
C) Normal QRS duration
D) Upright P wave in lead II

**Answer:** B) QTc markedly prolonged
