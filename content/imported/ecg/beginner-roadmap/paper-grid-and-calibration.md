# Paper, Grid, and Calibration

## Core Definition
Standard ECG paper usually runs at 25 mm/s with standard voltage calibration of 10 mm/mV. Horizontally, 1 small box = 0.04 seconds and 1 large box = 0.20 seconds. Vertically, 1 small box = 0.1 mV and 1 large box = 0.5 mV.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Before measuring anything, confirm paper speed and calibration. A visually 'wide' QRS or 'tall' R wave can be misleading if the calibration or paper speed is non-standard.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Measurement errors lead to wrong diagnoses: false bundle branch block, false QT prolongation, false ventricular hypertrophy or inaccurate rate.

## Common Trap
Never calculate intervals by memory alone. Count boxes. The ECG grid is the ruler; your eye is only a screening tool.

## Assessment Question
At a paper speed of 25 mm/s, a QRS complex spans 3 small boxes. What is its duration?
A) 0.06 s
B) 0.08 s
C) 0.12 s
D) 0.20 s

**Answer:** C) 0.12 s

**Arabic Rationale:** كل مربع صغير = 0.04 ثانية. ثلاثة مربعات = 0.12 ثانية، وهذا هو الحد الذي يجعلك تفكر أن QRS أصبح عريضًا.
