# Rhythm Analysis

## Core Definition
Rhythm analysis asks where the impulse starts, whether atrial activity is organized, whether every P wave conducts to a QRS, and whether the ventricular response is regular or irregular.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Use four questions: Are P waves present? Are they upright in lead II and negative in aVR? Is there one P before every QRS? Is the R-R interval regular? If yes, sinus rhythm is likely.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Dangerous rhythms can present before blood pressure collapses. Recognizing atrial fibrillation, SVT, VT, complete heart block and junctional/escape rhythms changes immediate management.

## Common Trap
A narrow QRS tachycardia is not automatically sinus tachycardia. Look for P waves and the clinical reason for tachycardia.

## Assessment Question
Which ECG finding best supports sinus rhythm?
A) Irregularly irregular R-R intervals without P waves
B) Upright P waves in lead II before each QRS with a consistent PR interval
C) Wide QRS complexes at 180 bpm
D) ST elevation in V2-V4

**Answer:** B) Upright P waves in lead II before each QRS with a consistent PR interval
