# ST-T Changes

## Core Definition
ST-T changes include ST elevation, ST depression, T-wave inversion, hyperacute T waves and abnormal repolarization patterns. They may reflect acute coronary occlusion, demand ischemia, pericarditis, early repolarization, electrolyte abnormality, ventricular hypertrophy strain or conduction-related secondary changes.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Look for distribution. Contiguous lead changes suggest an anatomic territory. Reciprocal changes increase concern for acute coronary occlusion. Diffuse concave ST elevation with PR depression suggests pericarditis. Hyperacute broad T waves may precede obvious ST elevation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
ST-T interpretation is where beginners miss high-risk ACS patterns. A nondiagnostic first ECG does not exclude evolving occlusion when symptoms are typical.

## Common Trap
Do not wait for troponin to declare an obvious STEMI/OMI pattern. ECG plus clinical picture can demand immediate escalation.

## Assessment Question
ST elevation in leads II, III and aVF most strongly localizes injury to which wall?
A) Inferior wall
B) Anterior wall
C) High lateral wall
D) Septal wall only

**Answer:** A) Inferior wall
