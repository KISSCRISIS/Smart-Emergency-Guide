# Final 5-Step ECG Interpretation

## Core Definition
A reproducible ECG method prevents misses: (1) Measurements: rate, PR, QRS, QT, axis; (2) Rhythm analysis; (3) Conduction analysis; (4) Waveform analysis; (5) Final interpretation plus comparison with previous ECG.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Write a final interpretation in layers: rhythm, rate, axis, intervals, conduction, ischemia/ST-T, hypertrophy or other patterns, then clinical correlation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
The method protects you from being distracted by one obvious abnormality while missing a second life-threatening one.

## Common Trap
The biggest beginner error is jumping to diagnosis before finishing the checklist. A structured method is not slow; it becomes fast with repetition.

## Assessment Question
Which step should be added after giving the final ECG interpretation?
A) Ignore prior ECGs because they confuse beginners
B) Compare with previous ECG when available
C) Read only lead II
D) Skip intervals if rhythm is sinus

**Answer:** B) Compare with previous ECG when available
