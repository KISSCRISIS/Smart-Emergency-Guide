# What Is an ECG?

## Core Definition
A 12-lead ECG is a surface recording of the heart's electrical activity over time. It does not directly show mechanical contraction; it shows depolarization, repolarization and timing relationships between atria, AV node, His-Purkinje system and ventricles.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Read it as a timed electrical story: P wave = atrial depolarization; PR interval = atrial-to-ventricular conduction time; QRS complex = ventricular depolarization; ST segment and T wave = early and late ventricular repolarization.

## Why It Matters in the ED
For an intern or GP, ECG is the fastest bedside test that can reveal immediately dangerous rhythm, ischemia, electrolyte toxicity or conduction failure before labs or imaging return.

## Common Trap
Do not say 'normal ECG' after only seeing sinus rhythm. Rhythm is only one part of interpretation. A patient may have sinus rhythm and still have STEMI, hyperkalemia, Wellens syndrome or long QT.

## Assessment Question
A stable 55-year-old patient has a regular rhythm with visible P waves before each QRS. Which statement is most correct?
A) The ECG is definitely normal.
B) Rhythm is only one component of ECG interpretation.
C) ST elevation cannot occur in sinus rhythm.
D) Electrolyte disorders do not affect the ECG.

**Answer:** B) Rhythm is only one component of ECG interpretation.

**Arabic Rationale:** وجود P قبل كل QRS يطمئنك على النظم، لكنه لا ينهي قراءة التخطيط. لازم تكمل: rate, axis, intervals, QRS, ST-T, and comparison.
