# Heart Rate Calculation

## Core Definition
Heart rate can be estimated using the 300 rule for regular rhythms or a timed strip method for irregular rhythms. For regular rhythms: HR = 300 divided by the number of large boxes between consecutive R waves. For irregular rhythms: count QRS complexes in a 6-second strip and multiply by 10.

## ECG Recognition Clues
First decide if the rhythm is regular. Regular rhythm: R-R intervals nearly equal. Irregular rhythm: R-R intervals vary significantly, so use the longer sampling method.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Rate changes urgency. Severe bradycardia may cause shock or syncope; very rapid rhythms may collapse cardiac output or degenerate into unstable rhythms.

## Common Trap
Using the 300 rule in atrial fibrillation gives a false sense of precision. Irregular rhythms require averaging.

## Assessment Question
A regular rhythm has 4 large boxes between two consecutive R waves. What is the approximate ventricular rate?
A) 60 bpm
B) 75 bpm
C) 100 bpm
D) 150 bpm

**Answer:** B) 75 bpm

**Arabic Rationale:** قاعدة 300: 300 ÷ 4 = 75. أول قرار قبل الحساب: هل النظم منتظم؟ هنا نعم.
