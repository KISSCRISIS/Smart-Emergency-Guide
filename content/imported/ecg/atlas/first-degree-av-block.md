# First-Degree AV Block

## Core Definition
First-degree AV block is delayed AV conduction with a PR interval >200 ms and no dropped QRS complexes.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Every P wave conducts to a QRS; PR interval is prolonged but constant.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Usually benign, but may indicate conduction disease, medication effect, ischemia or electrolyte abnormality depending on context.

## Common Trap
It is a delay, not a true block causing dropped beats.

## Assessment Question
PR 280 ms, every P followed by QRS. Diagnosis?
A) First-degree AV block
B) Mobitz II
C) VF
D) WPW

**Answer:** A) First-degree AV block
