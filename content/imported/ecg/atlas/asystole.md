# Asystole

## Core Definition
Asystole is absence of detectable ventricular electrical activity on ECG, usually seen as a near-flat line after confirming the tracing is valid.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Flat or nearly flat line in multiple leads; no organized QRS complexes; confirm leads, gain and patient connection.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Asystole is a non-shockable cardiac arrest rhythm requiring immediate CPR and reversible-cause search per resuscitation protocols.

## Common Trap
Do not shock true asystole. Also do not mistake disconnected leads for asystole.

## Assessment Question
Which step is essential before declaring true asystole?
A) Confirm leads/gain and check another lead while resuscitation proceeds
B) Give a synchronized shock immediately
C) Diagnose AF
D) Ignore the patient

**Answer:** A) Confirm leads/gain and check another lead while resuscitation proceeds

**Arabic Rationale:** الخط المستقيم قد يكون سلكًا مفصولًا، لكن المريض بلا نبض لا ينتظر.
