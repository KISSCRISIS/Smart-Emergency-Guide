# Pacemaker Rhythm

## Core Definition
Pacemaker rhythm reflects electrical stimulation from an implanted device, usually seen as pacing spikes preceding atrial or ventricular depolarization.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Thin pacing spikes; ventricular paced beats often have wide QRS with LBBB-like morphology if RV paced; atrial pacing spike may precede P wave.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Failure to pace, failure to capture or failure to sense can cause bradycardia, syncope or instability.

## Common Trap
Do not mistake pacing spikes for artifact; zoom and inspect relation to QRS/P waves.

## Assessment Question
A pacing spike followed by no QRS complex suggests what?
A) Failure to capture
B) Normal capture
C) Atrial flutter only
D) Wellens

**Answer:** A) Failure to capture

**Arabic Rationale:** الشرارة موجودة لكن لا توجد ضربة بعدها = capture problem.
