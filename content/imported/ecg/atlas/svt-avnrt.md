# SVT / AVNRT

## Core Definition
Paroxysmal supraventricular tachycardia, commonly AVNRT, is a rapid regular narrow-complex tachycardia involving reentry above or within the AV node region.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Regular narrow QRS tachycardia, often 150-250 bpm; P waves absent, retrograde or hidden; abrupt onset/offset.

## Why It Matters in the ED
May cause palpitations, chest discomfort, dyspnea, hypotension or syncope depending on rate and patient reserve.

## Common Trap
Do not assume all narrow tachycardia is SVT; sinus tachycardia and atrial flutter are common mimics.

## Assessment Question
Abrupt-onset regular narrow tachycardia at 190 bpm with hidden P waves most suggests what?
A) AVNRT/SVT
B) Asystole
C) Complete heart block
D) STEMI

**Answer:** A) AVNRT/SVT
