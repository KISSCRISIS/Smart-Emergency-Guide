# Pulmonary Embolism ECG Clues

## Core Definition
ECG in pulmonary embolism is often nonspecific but may show right-heart strain patterns.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Sinus tachycardia is common. Possible signs: right axis deviation, RBBB, T-wave inversion V1-V4/inferior leads, S1Q3T3, atrial arrhythmias.

## Why It Matters in the ED
ECG cannot rule out PE, but right strain in the correct clinical picture can support urgent evaluation.

## Common Trap
A normal ECG does not exclude PE.
