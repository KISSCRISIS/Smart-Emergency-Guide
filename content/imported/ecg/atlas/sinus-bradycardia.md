# Sinus Bradycardia

## Core Definition
Sinus bradycardia is sinus rhythm with a ventricular rate below 60 bpm.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Sinus P-wave morphology with 1:1 AV conduction; regular rhythm; rate <60 bpm.

## Why It Matters in the ED
May be normal in athletes or sleep, but dangerous if associated with hypotension, syncope, ischemia or altered mental status.

## Common Trap
Do not treat the number alone; treat the patient and cause.

## Assessment Question
A patient has sinus P waves before every QRS at 45 bpm and feels well. What is the most accurate statement?
A) Always cardiac arrest
B) Sinus bradycardia, interpret clinically
C) VF
D) STEMI

**Answer:** B) Sinus bradycardia, interpret clinically
