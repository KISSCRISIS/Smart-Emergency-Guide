# Normal Sinus Rhythm

## Core Definition
Normal sinus rhythm is an organized rhythm generated by the sinus node with appropriate atrial activation and AV conduction.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Rate usually 60-100 bpm in adults, upright P in lead II, negative P in aVR, one P before each QRS, constant PR, narrow QRS unless baseline conduction abnormality exists.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It is the reference rhythm, but not a complete ECG interpretation.

## Common Trap
Sinus rhythm does not rule out ischemia, hyperkalemia, QT prolongation or structural ECG abnormalities.

## Assessment Question
Which feature supports sinus rhythm?
A) P before every QRS with consistent PR
B) No P waves and irregular R-R
C) Chaotic baseline
D) Wide-complex tachycardia

**Answer:** A) P before every QRS with consistent PR
