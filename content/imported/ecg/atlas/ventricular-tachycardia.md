# Ventricular Tachycardia

## Core Definition
Ventricular tachycardia is a ventricular-origin tachyarrhythmia with wide QRS complexes, often regular, and potentially unstable.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Wide QRS tachycardia ≥120 ms, AV dissociation, capture/fusion beats, concordance or extreme axis may support VT.

## Why It Matters in the ED
VT can cause shock, ischemia, syncope or cardiac arrest and may deteriorate into VF.

## Common Trap
Never delay emergency management in an unstable patient while trying to prove VT vs SVT.

## Assessment Question
Regular wide-complex tachycardia in a patient with prior MI should be considered what until proven otherwise?
A) VT
B) Sinus tachycardia
C) Normal ECG
D) First-degree AV block

**Answer:** A) VT
