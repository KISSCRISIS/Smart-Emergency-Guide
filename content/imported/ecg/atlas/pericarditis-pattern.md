# Pericarditis Pattern

## Core Definition
Acute pericarditis can produce diffuse inflammatory ST-T changes rather than territorial coronary occlusion.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Diffuse concave ST elevation, PR depression, reciprocal ST depression mainly in aVR/V1, symptoms often pleuritic/positional.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Can mimic STEMI but management and risk differ; clinical context is essential.

## Common Trap
Do not diagnose pericarditis when ST elevation is territorial with reciprocal depression in opposite leads.

## Assessment Question
Diffuse concave ST elevation with PR depression most suggests what?
A) Pericarditis
B) Inferior STEMI only
C) Complete heart block
D) WPW

**Answer:** A) Pericarditis
