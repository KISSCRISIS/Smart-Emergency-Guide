# Second-Degree AV Block: Mobitz II

## Core Definition
Mobitz II is intermittent non-conducted P waves with constant PR intervals in conducted beats, usually implying infranodal disease.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Constant PR before conducted QRS complexes; sudden dropped QRS without progressive PR prolongation; QRS may be wide.

## Why It Matters in the ED
High risk of progression to complete heart block and symptomatic bradycardia.

## Common Trap
Do not label every dropped beat as Wenckebach. Check whether PR prolongs before the drop.

## Assessment Question
Constant PR intervals with sudden dropped QRS complexes suggest what?
A) Mobitz II
B) First-degree AV block
C) AF
D) Sinus tachycardia

**Answer:** A) Mobitz II
