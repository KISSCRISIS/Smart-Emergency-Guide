# Benign Early Repolarization

## Core Definition
Early repolarization is a common benign ST-elevation pattern, especially in young healthy patients, but must be differentiated from ischemia and pericarditis.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Concave ST elevation, J-point notching/slurring, stable pattern over time, prominent T waves, often precordial/lateral distribution.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Recognizing benign variants prevents overdiagnosis, but chest pain with dynamic changes requires caution.

## Common Trap
Do not label early repolarization as benign without clinical context and old ECG comparison.

## Assessment Question
Which feature supports benign early repolarization more than STEMI?
A) Stable old pattern with J-point notching in asymptomatic young patient
B) New reciprocal ST depression
C) Shock and crushing pain
D) Rising ST elevation over serial ECGs

**Answer:** A) Stable old pattern with J-point notching in asymptomatic young patient
