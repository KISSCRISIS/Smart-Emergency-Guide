# Sinus Tachycardia

## Core Definition
Sinus tachycardia is sinus rhythm with a rate above 100 bpm, usually a physiologic response to stress, fever, pain, hypovolemia, hypoxia or other causes.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Upright sinus P waves before each QRS, constant PR, regular rhythm, usually gradual onset/offset.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It may be the first clue of shock, sepsis, PE, bleeding, pain or withdrawal.

## Common Trap
Do not simply suppress sinus tachycardia before searching for the cause.

## Assessment Question
What is the best first interpretation of sinus tachycardia in a febrile dehydrated patient?
A) Primary arrhythmia until proven otherwise
B) Physiologic response requiring cause search
C) Complete heart block
D) WPW

**Answer:** B) Physiologic response requiring cause search

**Arabic Rationale:** الحرارة والجفاف يفسران التسارع، لكن يجب تقييم الشدة والسبب.
