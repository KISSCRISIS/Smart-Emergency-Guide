# Complete Heart Block

## Core Definition
Complete heart block is total AV dissociation with atria and ventricles paced independently.

## ECG Recognition Clues
P waves and QRS complexes march independently; no fixed PR relationship; escape rhythm may be narrow or wide.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Can cause syncope, shock or arrest; may occur with MI or conduction system disease.

## Common Trap
If you cannot find P waves, increase attention to subtle atrial activity across multiple leads.

## Assessment Question
P waves march through with no relationship to QRS. Diagnosis?
A) Complete heart block
B) Sinus rhythm
C) WPW
D) Pericarditis

**Answer:** A) Complete heart block
