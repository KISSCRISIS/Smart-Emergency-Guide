# Anterior STEMI / OMI

## Core Definition
Anterior STEMI/OMI involves acute injury in anterior/septal territories, often related to LAD occlusion.

## ECG Recognition Clues
ST elevation or hyperacute T waves in V1-V4, possible reciprocal inferior depression, new Q waves or loss of R-wave progression in evolving cases.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Large anterior occlusion can rapidly cause LV failure, shock or malignant arrhythmia.

## Common Trap
Subtle hyperacute T waves may precede obvious ST elevation. Compare with old ECG and repeat.

## Assessment Question
ST elevation V2-V4 localizes to what territory?
A) Anterior/septal
B) Inferior
C) Posterior only
D) Right atrial

**Answer:** A) Anterior/septal
