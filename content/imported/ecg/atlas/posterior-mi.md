# Posterior MI

## Core Definition
Posterior MI is infarction of the posterior wall, often appearing as reciprocal anterior changes on standard 12-lead ECG.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Horizontal ST depression in V1-V3, tall R waves in V1-V3, upright T waves; posterior leads V7-V9 may show ST elevation.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It is easily missed because the standard ECG does not directly view the posterior wall unless posterior leads are placed.

## Common Trap
Do not call V1-V3 ST depression 'subendocardial ischemia only' when chest pain and tall R waves suggest posterior occlusion.

## Assessment Question
ST depression V1-V3 with tall R waves during chest pain suggests what?
A) Posterior MI
B) Normal ECG
C) First-degree AV block
D) Isolated sinus arrhythmia

**Answer:** A) Posterior MI
