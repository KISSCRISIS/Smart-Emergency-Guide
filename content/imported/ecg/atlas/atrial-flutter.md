# Atrial Flutter

## Core Definition
Atrial flutter is a macro-reentrant atrial tachyarrhythmia, often producing organized flutter waves with variable AV conduction.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Sawtooth flutter waves, classically best seen in inferior leads; atrial rate often around 300 bpm; ventricular response may be regular or variable.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Rapid flutter can cause hypotension, ischemia or heart failure, and may be mistaken for sinus tachycardia or SVT.

## Common Trap
2:1 flutter can hide flutter waves and present as a regular narrow tachycardia near 150 bpm.

## Assessment Question
A regular narrow tachycardia at 150 bpm with subtle sawtooth waves should raise suspicion for what?
A) Atrial flutter with 2:1 conduction
B) VF
C) Asystole
D) Hyperkalemia

**Answer:** A) Atrial flutter with 2:1 conduction
