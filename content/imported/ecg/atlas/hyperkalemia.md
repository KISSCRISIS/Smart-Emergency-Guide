# Hyperkalemia

## Core Definition
Hyperkalemia causes progressive ECG changes affecting repolarization and conduction.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Peaked T waves, PR prolongation, flattened/absent P waves, QRS widening and sine-wave morphology in severe cases.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Can progress to VF/asystole; ECG changes may be the first urgent clue.

## Common Trap
Normal ECG does not fully exclude hyperkalemia in the right clinical context.
