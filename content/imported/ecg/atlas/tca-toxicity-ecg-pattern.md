# TCA Toxicity ECG Pattern

## Core Definition
Tricyclic antidepressant toxicity blocks fast sodium channels, widening the QRS and producing characteristic terminal rightward forces.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Wide QRS, terminal R wave in aVR, right axis of terminal 40 ms, prolonged PR/QT may occur.

## Why It Matters in the ED
ECG can identify life-threatening sodium-channel blockade before collapse.

## Common Trap
Do not wait for a toxicology screen if overdose context and ECG show sodium-channel blockade.

## Assessment Question
Overdose patient with wide QRS and prominent terminal R in aVR suggests what?
A) Sodium-channel blocker/TCA toxicity
B) Normal ECG
C) Wellens
D) Isolated sinus bradycardia

**Answer:** A) Sodium-channel blocker/TCA toxicity
