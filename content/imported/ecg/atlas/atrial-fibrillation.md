# Atrial Fibrillation

## Core Definition
Atrial fibrillation is chaotic atrial electrical activity causing an irregularly irregular ventricular response when conducted through the AV node.

## ECG Recognition Clues
No discrete P waves, fibrillatory baseline, irregularly irregular R-R intervals; QRS usually narrow unless aberrancy or preexisting conduction disease.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Can cause rapid ventricular response, heart failure exacerbation, ischemia, syncope or embolic stroke risk.

## Common Trap
Artifact can mimic AF. Confirm irregular R-R and absence of true P waves in more than one lead.

## Assessment Question
Which ECG clue is most typical of atrial fibrillation?
A) Sawtooth flutter waves
B) Irregularly irregular R-R intervals without discrete P waves
C) PR gradually lengthens then drops
D) Coved ST elevation V1-V2

**Answer:** B) Irregularly irregular R-R intervals without discrete P waves

**Arabic Rationale:** غياب P مع عدم انتظام كامل هو مفتاح AF.
