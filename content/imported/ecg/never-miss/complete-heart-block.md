# Complete Heart Block

## Core Definition
Complete heart block is third-degree AV block: atrial activity and ventricular activity are electrically dissociated because no atrial impulses conduct through the AV node/His system to the ventricles.

## ECG Recognition Clues
P waves march through independently of QRS complexes. PR intervals vary with no consistent relationship. Ventricular escape rhythm may be narrow junctional or wide ventricular and often slow.

## ED First Action
Assess perfusion immediately and follow local bradycardia/pacing pathway if unstable.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Severe bradycardia from complete heart block can cause syncope, shock, ischemia or cardiac arrest. It may be caused by inferior MI, anterior MI, degeneration, drugs or metabolic disease.

## Common Trap
Do not call it 2:1 block if P waves and QRS complexes are completely independent. Use calipers or mark P waves across the strip.

## Assessment Question
Which finding defines complete heart block?
A) Constant long PR interval with every P conducted
B) Irregularly irregular rhythm without P waves
C) Complete AV dissociation between P waves and QRS complexes
D) Short PR with delta wave

**Answer:** C) Complete AV dissociation between P waves and QRS complexes

**Arabic Rationale:** الفكرة ليست بطء فقط؛ الفكرة أن P و QRS لا علاقة ثابتة بينهما.
