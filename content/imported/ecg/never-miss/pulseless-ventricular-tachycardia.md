# Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia

## Core Definition
Pulseless VT is a wide-complex tachycardia that produces no palpable pulse and no effective perfusion.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Regular or near-regular wide QRS tachycardia, usually >120 ms QRS duration; rate often very fast; P waves absent or dissociated.

## ED First Action
Begin CPR and defibrillate according to current ACLS/local protocol.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Pulseless VT is treated as a shockable arrest rhythm. It may degenerate into VF or produce immediate circulatory collapse.

## Common Trap
The ECG label 'VT' is not enough. The key emergency question is: does the patient have a pulse and signs of perfusion?

## Assessment Question
A pulseless patient has a regular wide-complex tachycardia at 180 bpm. What is the emergency category?
A) Stable SVT
B) Pulseless VT
C) Normal paced rhythm
D) Sinus tachycardia

**Answer:** B) Pulseless VT

**Arabic Rationale:** Wide tachycardia + no pulse = shockable arrest rhythm. لا تضيع الوقت في التفريق النظري إذا المريض بلا نبض.
