# Wide-Complex Tachycardia

## Core Definition
Wide-complex tachycardia is a rapid rhythm with QRS duration ≥120 ms. In adults, a regular wide-complex tachycardia should be treated as VT until proven otherwise, especially when the patient is unstable or has structural heart disease.

## ECG Recognition Clues
QRS ≥120 ms, tachycardic rate, regular or irregular pattern. Clues favoring VT include AV dissociation, capture/fusion beats, extreme axis, very wide QRS and concordance in precordial leads.

## ED First Action
Assess stability first; manage according to local tachycardia/ACLS pathway and call senior help.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Mislabeling VT as SVT can be dangerous. Management depends heavily on stability and likelihood of ventricular origin.

## Common Trap
Do not give yourself false confidence from age alone. Young patients can have VT; older patients with ischemic heart disease are VT until proven otherwise.

## Assessment Question
A 70-year-old with prior MI has a regular wide-complex tachycardia at 170 bpm. What is the safest working assumption?
A) Sinus tachycardia
B) VT until proven otherwise
C) Normal variant
D) First-degree AV block

**Answer:** B) VT until proven otherwise
