# Wellens Syndrome

## Core Definition
Wellens syndrome is a high-risk ECG pattern suggesting critical proximal LAD stenosis, typically seen after recent chest pain has improved or resolved.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Deep symmetric T-wave inversions or biphasic T waves in V2-V3, usually with minimal ST elevation, preserved R-wave progression and no large Q waves. Troponin may be normal or only mildly elevated.

## ED First Action
Escalate as high-risk ACS; avoid stress testing unless specialist pathway confirms safety.

## Why It Matters in the ED
It is a pre-infarction warning pattern. Stress testing can be dangerous because it may provoke a large anterior MI.

## Common Trap
Do not reassure yourself because the patient is pain-free or troponin is low. The ECG may be warning you before complete occlusion.

## Assessment Question
A pain-free patient after chest pain has deep symmetric T-wave inversions in V2-V3 with minimal ST elevation. What is the key concern?
A) Wellens syndrome and critical LAD stenosis
B) Benign early repolarization only
C) Limb-lead reversal
D) Isolated hypercalcemia

**Answer:** A) Wellens syndrome and critical LAD stenosis

**Arabic Rationale:** تحسن الألم لا يعني الأمان. V2-V3 مع T عميقة/ثنائية الطور بعد ألم صدري = Wellens حتى يثبت العكس.
