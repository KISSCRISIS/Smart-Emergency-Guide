# STEMI / Occlusion MI Pattern

## Core Definition
STEMI/OMI refers to ECG evidence of acute coronary artery occlusion causing transmural myocardial injury. Classic STEMI uses ST elevation in contiguous leads, but occlusion MI can also present with high-risk patterns that do not meet simple criteria.

## ECG Recognition Clues
ST elevation in anatomically contiguous leads, reciprocal ST depression, hyperacute T waves, new Q waves, posterior ST-depression pattern, or high-risk equivalents such as de Winter or left main/proximal LAD patterns.

## ED First Action
Escalate immediately for ACS/reperfusion pathway according to local policy; repeat ECGs if evolving or initially nondiagnostic.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Acute coronary occlusion is time-sensitive myocardial death. The ECG should trigger rapid escalation, repeat ECGs and reperfusion pathway assessment.

## Common Trap
Do not reduce ACS to '1 mm ST elevation only'. Early occlusion can show hyperacute T waves or subtle reciprocal changes before obvious ST elevation.

## Assessment Question
A patient with crushing chest pain has ST elevation in V2-V4 with reciprocal inferior ST depression. What is the safest interpretation?
A) Normal early repolarization until troponin returns
B) Acute anterior occlusion pattern requiring urgent escalation
C) Isolated first-degree AV block
D) Artifact only

**Answer:** B) Acute anterior occlusion pattern requiring urgent escalation
