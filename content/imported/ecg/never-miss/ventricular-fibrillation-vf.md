# Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)

## Core Definition
Ventricular fibrillation is a chaotic ventricular rhythm with no organized QRS complexes and no effective cardiac output.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Irregular, chaotic baseline; no consistent P waves; no organized QRS; no measurable regular ventricular rate.

## ED First Action
Start high-quality CPR and defibrillate according to current ACLS/local protocol. Call for help immediately.

## Why It Matters in the ED
VF is a shockable cardiac arrest rhythm. Delayed recognition means delayed defibrillation and lower survival.

## Common Trap
Do not confuse fine VF with asystole. Check gain, leads, patient connection and pulse, but do not delay resuscitation when the patient is pulseless.

## Assessment Question
In an unresponsive pulseless patient, the monitor shows chaotic irregular waves with no identifiable QRS complexes. What is the rhythm?
A) Atrial fibrillation
B) Ventricular fibrillation
C) Sinus tachycardia
D) First-degree AV block

**Answer:** B) Ventricular fibrillation

**Arabic Rationale:** عدم وجود QRS منظم مع مريض بلا نبض = VF حتى يثبت العكس. هذا نمط إنعاش وليس نقاشًا أكاديميًا طويلًا.
