# Brugada Type 1 Pattern

## Core Definition
A Brugada type 1 ECG pattern is characterized by coved ST-segment elevation with T-wave inversion in the right precordial leads, classically V1-V2, and is associated with risk of ventricular arrhythmias and sudden cardiac death in susceptible patients.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Coved ST elevation, high take-off/J-point elevation in V1-V2, downsloping ST segment and inverted T wave; may be spontaneous or fever/drug-provoked.

## ED First Action
Escalate for senior/cardiology review, treat fever, avoid provoking drugs and assess arrhythmic symptoms.

## Why It Matters in the ED
In syncope, nocturnal agonal breathing, family history of sudden death or fever, this pattern should not be dismissed as RBBB or benign ST elevation.

## Common Trap
Do not diagnose full Brugada syndrome from ECG alone without clinical context and specialist evaluation. But do not ignore a type 1 pattern in a symptomatic patient.

## Assessment Question
A febrile young man with syncope has coved ST elevation and T-wave inversion in V1-V2. What pattern must be considered?
A) Brugada type 1 pattern
B) Inferior STEMI
C) Atrial flutter
D) First-degree AV block

**Answer:** A) Brugada type 1 pattern
