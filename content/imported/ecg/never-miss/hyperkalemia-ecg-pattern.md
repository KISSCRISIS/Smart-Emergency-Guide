# Hyperkalemia ECG Pattern

## Core Definition
Hyperkalemia alters cardiac depolarization and repolarization. ECG manifestations can progress from peaked T waves to PR prolongation, P-wave flattening/loss, QRS widening and sine-wave morphology.

## ECG Recognition Clues
Tall narrow tented T waves, diffuse pattern, PR prolongation, small/absent P waves, widening QRS, bradyarrhythmia or sine-wave in severe cases.

## ED First Action
Treat as an emergency in clinical context and follow local hyperkalemia/resuscitation protocol.

## Why It Matters in the ED
Hyperkalemia can suddenly deteriorate into malignant ventricular arrhythmia or arrest. ECG changes may not perfectly correlate with potassium level, so treat the pattern and patient seriously.

## Common Trap
Do not wait for a lab value if the ECG and clinical context strongly suggest life-threatening hyperkalemia.

## Assessment Question
Which ECG pattern most strongly suggests dangerous hyperkalemia?
A) Tall narrow peaked T waves with widening QRS
B) Isolated short PR with delta wave
C) Sawtooth flutter waves only
D) Normal QT with sinus rhythm

**Answer:** A) Tall narrow peaked T waves with widening QRS
