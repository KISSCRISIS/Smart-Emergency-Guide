# ECG Mini Cases

Each case is written for Interns, GPs and junior residents. Use the same four questions every time: What is the first thing you notice? What pattern is this? What can kill the patient now? What should be escalated?

## Mini Case 1: Chest pain and anterior ST elevation
**Clinical Stem:** A 58-year-old man has crushing retrosternal chest pain. ECG shows ST elevation in V2-V4 and reciprocal inferior ST depression.

**What is the first thing you notice?** ST elevation in an anterior contiguous territory with reciprocal changes.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Acute LAD occlusion/anterior OMI.

**What can kill the patient now?** Malignant arrhythmia, shock, expanding infarction.

## Mini Case 2: Pain resolved but T waves are abnormal
**Clinical Stem:** A 49-year-old woman had chest pain two hours ago. Now pain-free. ECG shows deep symmetric T inversion in V2-V3.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Deep anterior T-wave inversion after resolved pain.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Wellens syndrome.

**What can kill the patient now?** Near-term large anterior MI if missed; avoid stress testing.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 3: Pulseless chaotic rhythm
**Clinical Stem:** An unresponsive patient has no pulse. Monitor shows chaotic waves with no organized QRS.

**What is the first thing you notice?** No QRS, no organized rhythm.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Ventricular fibrillation.

**What can kill the patient now?** Death without immediate CPR/defibrillation pathway.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 4: Regular wide tachycardia
**Clinical Stem:** A 72-year-old with prior MI is hypotensive. ECG shows regular wide-complex tachycardia at 180 bpm.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Wide regular tachycardia in structural heart disease.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Ventricular tachycardia until proven otherwise.

**What can kill the patient now?** Circulatory collapse or degeneration to VF.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 5: Slow independent P and QRS
**Clinical Stem:** A patient with syncope has P waves marching through independently from a slow wide QRS escape rhythm.

**What is the first thing you notice?** AV dissociation.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Complete heart block.

**What can kill the patient now?** Bradycardic shock or arrest.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 6: Renal failure and tented T waves
**Clinical Stem:** A dialysis patient missed sessions. ECG shows tall tented T waves and widening QRS.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Peaked T waves plus conduction widening.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Life-threatening hyperkalemia pattern.

**What can kill the patient now?** VF/asystole if progression continues.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 7: Febrile syncope with V1-V2 coved ST
**Clinical Stem:** A young man with fever faints. ECG shows coved ST elevation and T inversion in V1-V2.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Right precordial coved pattern.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Brugada type 1 pattern.

**What can kill the patient now?** Ventricular arrhythmia/sudden death risk in clinical context.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 8: Flatline after collapse
**Clinical Stem:** An elderly patient is pulseless. Monitor shows flatline. Leads and gain are checked while CPR begins.

**What is the first thing you notice?** No organized ventricular electrical activity.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Asystole.

**What can kill the patient now?** Non-shockable arrest requiring CPR/reversible cause search.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 9: Narrow regular 150
**Clinical Stem:** A patient has palpitations. ECG shows regular narrow tachycardia near 150 bpm. Inferior leads reveal sawtooth waves.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Sawtooth flutter waves, 2:1 conduction.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Atrial flutter.

**What can kill the patient now?** Rapid ventricular response causing ischemia or hypotension.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 10: Overdose and wide QRS
**Clinical Stem:** A young adult after intentional overdose has altered mental status. ECG shows wide QRS and terminal R wave in aVR.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Sodium-channel blockade pattern.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** TCA/sodium-channel blocker toxicity.

**What can kill the patient now?** Seizures, ventricular arrhythmia, shock.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 11: V1-V3 ST depression
**Clinical Stem:** A patient with chest pain has horizontal ST depression in V1-V3 and tall R waves.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Posterior mirror-image ischemia.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Posterior MI/OMI.

**What can kill the patient now?** Missed occlusion if posterior leads are not considered.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟

## Mini Case 12: Diffuse ST elevation
**Clinical Stem:** A patient has pleuritic chest pain better leaning forward. ECG shows diffuse concave ST elevation and PR depression.

**What is the first thing you notice?** Diffuse non-territorial ST changes with PR depression.

**Most likely ECG pattern:** Pericarditis pattern.

**What can kill the patient now?** Mistaking it for STEMI or missing myocarditis/effusion context.

**Arabic Teaching Point:** لا تبحث عن الاسم فقط؛ اسأل دائمًا: هل هذا النمط يقتل الآن؟ وهل يحتاج تصعيدًا فوريًا؟
