# Pyloric Stenosis

## Definition
POCUS can identify thickened, elongated pylorus in infants with projectile vomiting when performed by trained clinicians.

## Why it matters in the ED
Early recognition speeds definitive imaging/surgical pathway.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use high-frequency probe in epigastrium/RUQ; identify pylorus in long and short axes.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Confusing pylorospasm with stenosis.
