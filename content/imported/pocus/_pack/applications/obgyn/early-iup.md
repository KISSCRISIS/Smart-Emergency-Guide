# Early Intrauterine Pregnancy

## Definition
First-trimester pelvic POCUS looks for a definite intrauterine pregnancy in the right clinical context.

## Why it matters in the ED
Identifying a true IUP lowers concern for ectopic pregnancy in most, but not all, cases.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use transabdominal or transvaginal technique according to training and policy; identify gestational sac with yolk sac or fetal pole.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Calling a pseudosac a definite IUP.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What makes an intrauterine pregnancy more definitive than a simple fluid sac?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Yolk sac or fetal pole within an intrauterine gestational sac.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
