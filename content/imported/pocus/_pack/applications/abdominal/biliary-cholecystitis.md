# Biliary Colic and Cholecystitis

## Definition
Gallbladder POCUS evaluates stones, wall thickening, pericholecystic fluid, sonographic Murphy sign, and biliary dilation clues.

## Why it matters in the ED
It helps sort RUQ pain and expedite surgical or formal imaging pathways.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Scan longitudinal/transverse gallbladder with patient repositioning if needed.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Equating gallstones alone with acute cholecystitis.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** Which findings support acute cholecystitis rather than simple cholelithiasis?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Stones plus inflammatory signs such as wall thickening, pericholecystic fluid, or sonographic Murphy sign.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
