# Small Bowel Obstruction

## Definition
Bowel POCUS may show dilated fluid-filled loops and abnormal peristalsis in SBO.

## Why it matters in the ED
It can support earlier suspicion but does not replace CT when needed.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Scan abdomen systematically; look for dilated loops and to-and-fro movement.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Using bowel ultrasound alone to determine surgical disposition.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What POCUS pattern may support SBO?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Dilated fluid-filled bowel loops with abnormal/to-and-fro peristalsis.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
