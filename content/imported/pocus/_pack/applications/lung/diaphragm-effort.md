# Diaphragm and Effort Clues

## Definition
Bedside ultrasound can observe diaphragm motion and gross respiratory mechanics in selected patients.

## Why it matters in the ED
It may support evaluation of ventilator weaning, neuromuscular weakness, or unexplained dyspnea.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use subcostal or lower intercostal views; compare sides qualitatively.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Overinterpreting diaphragm motion without patient effort context.
