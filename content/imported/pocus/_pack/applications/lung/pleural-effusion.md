# Pleural Effusion

## Definition
Pleural effusion appears as an anechoic or complex fluid collection above the diaphragm in thoracic views.

## Why it matters in the ED
It helps distinguish fluid from consolidation and can guide thoracentesis planning.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Scan posterior/lateral bases; identify diaphragm, liver/spleen, and lung movement.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Mistaking ascites below the diaphragm for pleural fluid.
