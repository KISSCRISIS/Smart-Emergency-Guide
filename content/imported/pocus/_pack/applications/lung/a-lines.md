# A-Lines and Dry Lung Pattern

## Definition
A-lines are horizontal reverberation artifacts deep to the pleural line, often seen with air-filled lung.

## Why it matters in the ED
Diffuse A-lines may support obstructive lung disease or normal aerated lung when correlated clinically.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use lung zones bilaterally and identify pleural line and sliding.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Assuming A-lines exclude every dangerous cause of dyspnea.
