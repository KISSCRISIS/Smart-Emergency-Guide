# B-Lines and Interstitial Syndrome

## Definition
B-lines are vertical artifacts arising from the pleural line and extending to the bottom of the screen, moving with lung sliding.

## Why it matters in the ED
Diffuse bilateral B-lines suggest interstitial fluid or edema patterns but are not specific to one diagnosis.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Scan multiple zones; assess distribution, pleural appearance, effusions, and cardiac context.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Equating any B-lines with heart failure.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** Diffuse bilateral B-lines with dyspnea may suggest what pattern?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Interstitial syndrome such as pulmonary edema, depending on context.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
