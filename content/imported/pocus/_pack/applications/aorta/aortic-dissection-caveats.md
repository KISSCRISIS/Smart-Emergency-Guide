# Aortic Dissection Caveats

## Definition
POCUS may show indirect findings of dissection but cannot reliably exclude acute aortic syndrome.

## Why it matters in the ED
High-risk chest/back pain often needs definitive imaging when appropriate.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Look for pericardial effusion, aortic root dilation, flap if visible, and complications—but know the limits.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Using bedside ultrasound to rule out dissection.
