# Central Venous Access Guidance

## Definition
Ultrasound guidance improves anatomic identification and real-time needle placement for central venous access.

## Why it matters in the ED
It reduces mechanical complications when used correctly and with sterile technique.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Identify vein/artery, compressibility, depth, needle path, and guidewire location per local protocol.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Relying on color alone to identify artery versus vein.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What should be identified before central venous puncture?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Vein, artery, depth, compressibility, and safe needle path.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
