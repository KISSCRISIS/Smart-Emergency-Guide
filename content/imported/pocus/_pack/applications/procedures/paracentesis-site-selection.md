# Paracentesis Site Selection

## Definition
POCUS identifies ascites pockets and avoids bowel/vessels before paracentesis.

## Why it matters in the ED
It improves procedural planning and safety.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Scan both lower quadrants; evaluate depth and bowel movement; consider color Doppler if needed.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Assuming dullness to percussion equals safe pocket.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What is the POCUS goal before paracentesis?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Find a safe fluid pocket and avoid bowel/vessels.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
