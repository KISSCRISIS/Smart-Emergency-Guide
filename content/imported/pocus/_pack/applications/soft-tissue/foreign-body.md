# Foreign Body Ultrasound

## Definition
POCUS may help identify superficial foreign bodies and surrounding abscess or inflammation.

## Why it matters in the ED
Useful for radiolucent objects such as wood in selected cases.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use linear probe, water bath/standoff if superficial, and scan in two planes.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Assuming a negative scan excludes all foreign bodies.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What is a limitation of foreign body POCUS?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Operator dependence and false negatives, especially deep or tiny objects.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
