# DVT Femoral Compression

## Definition
Compression ultrasound evaluates vein compressibility in proximal lower-extremity DVT assessment.

## Why it matters in the ED
A noncompressible vein suggests thrombus in the correct clinical setting.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Identify common femoral vein, saphenofemoral junction, and femoral bifurcation region.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Compressing artery and thinking the vein is assessed.
