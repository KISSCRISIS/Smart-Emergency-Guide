# Negative FAST Limitations

## Definition
A negative FAST does not exclude injury, especially early bleeding, retroperitoneal injury, bowel injury, or solid-organ injury without free fluid.

## Why it matters in the ED
Stable patients may still require CT or observation depending on risk.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use serial exams and broader trauma assessment.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Using negative FAST as a discharge test.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** Which injury is less likely to be detected by FAST?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Retroperitoneal hemorrhage or isolated bowel injury.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
