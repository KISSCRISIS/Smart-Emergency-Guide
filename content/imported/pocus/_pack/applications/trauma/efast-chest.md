# eFAST Chest Views

## Definition
eFAST adds thoracic views for pneumothorax and hemothorax to the classic abdominal FAST.

## Why it matters in the ED
It rapidly searches for major chest threats after trauma.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Assess lung sliding anteriorly and pleural fluid at bases.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Letting a negative eFAST falsely reassure when physiology remains concerning.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What does eFAST add to FAST?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Thoracic assessment for pneumothorax and hemothorax.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
