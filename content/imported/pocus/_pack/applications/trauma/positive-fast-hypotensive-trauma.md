# Positive FAST in Hypotensive Trauma

## Definition
A positive FAST in a hypotensive trauma patient is a major danger signal for hemorrhage.

## Why it matters in the ED
It can accelerate surgical/trauma pathway decisions.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Interpret in context of trauma mechanism, vitals, and exam.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Delaying definitive hemorrhage control for unnecessary perfect imaging.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What does a positive FAST in hypotensive blunt trauma suggest?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Clinically significant internal bleeding until proven otherwise.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
