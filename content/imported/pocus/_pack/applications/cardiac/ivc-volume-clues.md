# IVC and Volume Clues

## Definition
IVC assessment may provide clues about right atrial pressure and volume responsiveness but is highly context dependent.

## Why it matters in the ED
It can support, but not determine alone, fluid or shock decisions.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Assess size and respiratory variation with clinical context and ventilation status.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Using IVC collapse as a standalone fluid order.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** Why should IVC not be used alone to decide fluids?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. It is affected by ventilation, right heart function, intrathoracic pressure, and patient effort.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
