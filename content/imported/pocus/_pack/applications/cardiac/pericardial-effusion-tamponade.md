# Pericardial Effusion and Tamponade

## Definition
Focused cardiac ultrasound can identify pericardial fluid and signs concerning for tamponade physiology.

## Why it matters in the ED
Tamponade is an obstructive shock diagnosis where minutes matter.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Subcostal and parasternal views; look for anechoic fluid around the heart, right-sided chamber collapse, plethoric IVC, and clinical shock.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Mistaking pleural effusion or epicardial fat for pericardial effusion.
