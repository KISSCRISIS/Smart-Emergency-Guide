# POCUS Mini Cases

## Case 1 — Dyspnea
A 67-year-old with severe dyspnea has diffuse bilateral B-lines, reduced LV function, and pleural effusions.
- First impression: cardiogenic pulmonary edema pattern.
- What can kill the patient now? Hypoxemic respiratory failure and shock.
- Trap: B-lines are not a diagnosis by themselves; integrate heart, vitals, ECG, and history.

## Case 2 — Trauma
A hypotensive MVC patient has free fluid in RUQ FAST.
- First impression: hemorrhage until proven otherwise.
- What can kill the patient now? Ongoing internal bleeding.
- Trap: Do not delay trauma escalation for perfect extra images.

## Case 3 — Early Pregnancy
A patient has positive pregnancy test, pelvic pain, no definite IUP, and free fluid.
- First impression: possible ruptured ectopic pregnancy.
- What can kill the patient now? Hemorrhagic shock.
- Trap: Pregnancy of unknown location is not a reassurance label.

## Case 4 — Ventilated Patient Desaturation
A ventilated asthma patient becomes hypotensive; lung sliding is present bilaterally, and there is concern for dynamic hyperinflation.
- First impression: ventilator physiology may be contributing.
- What can kill the patient now? Obstructive shock from air trapping or missed pneumothorax.
- Trap: Ultrasound is one piece; disconnect/bagging decisions require clinical protocol and senior help.

## Case 5 — Eye
A patient has sudden painless vision loss; ocular POCUS shows a tethered mobile membrane.
- First impression: retinal detachment pattern.
- What can kill the vision now? Delayed ophthalmology escalation.
- Trap: Do not scan if open globe is suspected.
