### 1. Shock POCUS
A hypotensive patient has a large pericardial effusion on subcostal view and right-sided chamber collapse. What is the most dangerous physiology?
A. COPD exacerbation
B. Cardiac tamponade
C. Simple pleural effusion
D. Uncomplicated dehydration

**Answer: B**

**Arabic Reasoning:** وجود سائل حول القلب مع علامات ضغط على الحجرات يعني أن القلب قد لا يمتلئ؛ هذه صدمة انسدادية قاتلة.

### 2. Lung Sliding
Absent lung sliding in one anterior chest zone after intubation most strongly requires which mindset?
A. Pneumothorax is possible, but confirm with context and other signs
B. Pneumothorax is impossible
C. Heart failure is confirmed
D. Retinal detachment is confirmed

**Answer: A**

### 3. Negative FAST
A stable trauma patient has persistent abdominal tenderness but a negative FAST. What is the safest interpretation?
A. No abdominal injury exists
B. FAST excludes retroperitoneal hemorrhage
C. Negative FAST does not exclude clinically important injury
D. Patient can always be discharged

**Answer: C**

### 4. Ocular POCUS
Which situation should make you avoid ocular ultrasound pressure?
A. Suspected retinal detachment
B. Suspected open globe
C. Painless visual floaters
D. Headache with intact eye exam

**Answer: B**

### 5. Ultrasound-guided needle procedures
What is the most important procedural safety principle?
A. Advance when the shaft is visible
B. Keep the needle tip identified before advancing
C. Use maximum depth always
D. Do not document images

**Answer: B**
