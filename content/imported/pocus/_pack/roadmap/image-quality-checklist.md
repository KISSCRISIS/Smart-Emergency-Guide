# Image Quality Checklist

## Definition
Good interpretation starts with good images: correct probe, correct preset, correct depth, centered target, adequate gain, identifiable landmarks, and saved clips.

## Why it matters in the ED
A technically poor scan should be labeled limited rather than forced into a confident diagnosis.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use a pre-interpretation checklist before making a call.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Interpreting a poor window with the confidence of a perfect image.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What should be documented when the target cannot be adequately visualized?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. A limited exam with the reason for limitation.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
