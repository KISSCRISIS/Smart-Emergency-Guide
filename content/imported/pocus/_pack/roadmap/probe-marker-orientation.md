# Probe Marker and Image Orientation

## Definition
The probe marker links the physical probe to the marker dot on the ultrasound screen; without orientation, right/left and cranial/caudal interpretation becomes unsafe.

## Why it matters in the ED
Orientation errors can invert anatomy and cause wrong conclusions, especially in vascular, FAST, and procedural guidance scans.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Confirm the institution’s convention, identify the marker on the probe, and perform a quick orientation check before scanning.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Assuming every machine or preset displays the marker in the same location.
