# Documentation, Storage, and QA

## Definition
Every clinically used POCUS exam should have saved images/clips, a focused interpretation, and QA review according to local policy.

## Why it matters in the ED
POCUS affects decisions; undocumented images are hard to audit, teach from, or defend clinically.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Document indication, limited scope, key findings, interpretation, and limitations.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Doing a scan, acting on it, then saving nothing.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What is the safest documentation principle for POCUS?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Save representative images/clips and document a focused interpretation with limitations.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
