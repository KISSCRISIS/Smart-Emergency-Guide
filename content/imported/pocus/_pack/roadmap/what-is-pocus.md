# What POCUS Is—and Is Not

## Definition
POCUS is a focused bedside ultrasound examination performed by the treating clinician to answer a time-sensitive clinical question.

## Why it matters in the ED
POCUS narrows uncertainty at the bedside; it does not replace a full radiology study when a comprehensive exam is needed.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use it when the answer changes immediate ED action: free fluid? tamponade? pneumothorax? AAA? IUP? abscess?

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Overcalling a limited scan as a definitive comprehensive diagnosis.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** Which statement is most accurate about ED POCUS?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. It is a focused bedside tool that answers specific clinical questions and must be integrated with the clinical picture.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
