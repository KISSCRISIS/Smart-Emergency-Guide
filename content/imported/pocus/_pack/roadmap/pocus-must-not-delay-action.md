# POCUS Should Not Delay Life-Saving Action

## Definition
POCUS is useful only if it speeds or clarifies care; it should not delay obvious life-saving intervention in unstable patients.

## Why it matters in the ED
A crashing patient with classic tension physiology, massive external hemorrhage, or obvious airway failure may need action before imaging.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use ultrasound when it can be obtained immediately and will change the next step.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Waiting for a perfect image while the patient deteriorates.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** A hypotensive trauma patient has obvious peritonitis and positive FAST. What is the principle?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Do not delay definitive trauma management for unnecessary additional imaging when the scan and physiology already support urgent action.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
