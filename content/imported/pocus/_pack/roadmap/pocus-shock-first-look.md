# POCUS in the Undifferentiated Shock Patient

## Definition
POCUS in shock is a structured bedside search for immediately reversible causes: pump failure, tamponade, hypovolemia clues, RV strain, pneumothorax, free fluid, or AAA.

## Why it matters in the ED
Shock is a race against time; ultrasound helps separate obstructive, cardiogenic, distributive, and hemorrhagic patterns.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use a repeatable sequence: heart, lungs, IVC/volume clues, abdomen/pelvis, aorta, legs when appropriate.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Scanning randomly while resuscitation is delayed.

**Question:** In undifferentiated shock, what is the main purpose of POCUS?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. To rapidly look for reversible physiologic patterns that change immediate management.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
