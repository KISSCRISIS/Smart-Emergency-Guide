# Machine, Probe, and Screen Basics

## Definition
Safe POCUS begins with understanding the probe, marker, depth, gain, focus, and the relationship between the patient, probe, and screen.

## Why it matters in the ED
Most beginner errors are not diagnostic errors; they are acquisition errors caused by wrong probe, wrong depth, poor contact, or wrong orientation.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Start with probe choice, orient the marker, adjust depth so the target fills the screen, then tune gain.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Changing multiple knobs before knowing what you are trying to see.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** A beginner cannot see the gallbladder because the image is too deep and dark. What is the best first concept?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Optimize acquisition first: choose the appropriate probe, reduce depth, improve contact, and adjust gain.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
