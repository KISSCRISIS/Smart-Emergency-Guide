# False Positives and False Negatives

## Definition
Every POCUS application has limitations: poor windows, body habitus, bowel gas, early disease, operator inexperience, and confounders can create false results.

## Why it matters in the ED
A negative FAST does not exclude solid organ injury; absent B-lines does not exclude all causes of dyspnea; no hydronephrosis does not exclude early stone.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Know what the scan can answer and what it cannot answer.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Using a negative limited scan to dismiss a dangerous clinical presentation.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** A stable trauma patient has a negative FAST but persistent abdominal tenderness. What is safest?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Do not use a negative FAST alone to exclude clinically important injury.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
