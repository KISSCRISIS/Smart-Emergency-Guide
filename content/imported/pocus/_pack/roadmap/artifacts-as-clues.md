# Ultrasound Artifacts as Clues

## Definition
Artifacts are image patterns created by physics rather than true anatomy, but in POCUS they often become diagnostic clues.

## Why it matters in the ED
A-lines, B-lines, acoustic shadowing, posterior enhancement, mirror artifacts, and reverberation can help identify lung, biliary, fluid, and foreign-body patterns.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Learn the artifact, its mechanism, and when it supports rather than confuses the diagnosis.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Calling every bright line or shadow a disease without asking whether it is artifact.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** Which lung artifact suggests air-filled lung when repeated horizontally below the pleural line?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. A-lines.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
