# Image Labeling and Side Confirmation

## Definition
Correct patient, side, view, and anatomic labeling prevent dangerous miscommunication.

## Why it matters in the ED
POCUS often guides urgent decisions, so wrong-side images are high-risk.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Say the side out loud and save labeled images when possible.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Saving an unlabeled clip and later guessing the side.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What should be confirmed before saving a procedural or diagnostic POCUS image?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Patient, side, view, and focused finding.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
