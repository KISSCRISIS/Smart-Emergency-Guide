# Color Doppler Basics

## Definition
Color Doppler displays movement of blood or fluid relative to the probe; it is useful but highly angle and settings dependent.

## Why it matters in the ED
Helps confirm vessel flow, differentiate vessels from ducts, and guide vascular assessment.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use as an adjunct after identifying anatomy in B-mode.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Assuming no color equals no flow without optimizing settings.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What is a major limitation of color Doppler?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. It is angle- and settings-dependent.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
