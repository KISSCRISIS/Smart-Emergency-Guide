# Phased Array Probe

## Definition
A small-footprint low-frequency probe designed to see between ribs, especially cardiac and thoracic windows.

## Why it matters in the ED
The small footprint helps obtain cardiac views through narrow acoustic windows.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use for echo, pericardial effusion, LV/RV assessment, and subcostal views.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Expecting it to show superficial fine detail like a linear probe.
