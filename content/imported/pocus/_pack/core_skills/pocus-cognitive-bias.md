# POCUS Cognitive Bias

## Definition
POCUS can create anchoring: once you see one abnormality, you may stop looking for a second problem.

## Why it matters in the ED
Critically ill patients can have multiple simultaneous pathologies.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Integrate POCUS with vital signs, exam, ECG, labs, and reassessment.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Letting one image close the differential too early.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** A dyspneic patient has B-lines but also hypotension. What should you avoid?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Anchoring on pulmonary edema without assessing other shock causes.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
