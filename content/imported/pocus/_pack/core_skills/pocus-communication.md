# POCUS Communication

## Definition
Good POCUS communication states the question, adequacy, finding, and limitation: “Limited cardiac POCUS shows…”.

## Why it matters in the ED
Clear language prevents overstatement and helps consultants understand the scope.

## Probe / Patient / Acquisition
Use focused wording: positive, negative, indeterminate, or limited.

## Key Findings to Look For
- Start with the focused clinical question.
- Confirm the relevant anatomy before interpreting pathology.
- Compare sides or views when appropriate.
- Save representative images or clips for QA when the scan is used clinically.

## Common ED Trap
Saying “normal ultrasound” after a limited POCUS exam.

## Assessment Question
**Question:** What is safer than documenting “normal ultrasound”?

A. It replaces the clinical examination
B. Documenting a focused limited interpretation with limitations.
C. It should be interpreted without clinical context
D. It is always definitive even when image quality is poor

**Correct Answer:** B
