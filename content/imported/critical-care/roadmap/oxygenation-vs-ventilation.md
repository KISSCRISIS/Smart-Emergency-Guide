# Oxygenation vs Ventilation

## Learning Goal
Separate oxygen failure from CO2 clearance failure so the correct respiratory support is chosen conceptually.

## Core Teaching Point
Critical care in the emergency department is not about memorizing every ICU detail. It is about recognizing **physiologic collapse early**, buying time safely, and choosing the next destination before the patient deteriorates.

## SEG Mental Model
1. **Find the immediate killer.**
2. **Support oxygen delivery.**
3. **Reverse the reversible cause.**
4. **Reassess after every intervention.**
5. **Communicate what is still dangerous.**

## Arabic Simplification

## Common Trap
Waiting for a complete diagnosis before acting on obvious physiology. A hypotensive, hypoxic, altered patient needs stabilization while the diagnostic workup is still running.

## Assessment Question
**A critically ill patient arrives confused, diaphoretic, hypotensive, and tachypneic. Which approach is safest?**

A. Wait for all laboratory results before acting.
B. Start a repeatable ABCDE assessment and treat immediate physiologic threats.
C. Focus first on the most likely final diagnosis only.
D. Delay reassessment until after imaging.

**Correct Answer:** B
