# Critical Care Mini Cases

> **SEG Safety Boundary:** This page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Medication doses, ventilator settings, procedure timing, transfusion ratios, and disposition rules must be verified against local policy and current specialty guidelines before publication or clinical use.



## Mini Case 1: Undifferentiated Shock

**Clinical Vignette:**
A patient arrives to the ED with hypotension or narrow pulse pressure, altered mental status, cool or mottled skin. The nurse says, “They looked better ten minutes ago.”

**Prompt:** What can kill the patient now?

**Expected Thinking:**
- Identify airway, breathing, circulation, and neurologic threats.
- Look for the reversible cause suggested by the clues.
- Use bedside reassessment rather than waiting passively.
- Escalate early if deterioration continues.

**Arabic Teaching Pearl:**
المريض الذي “كان أفضل قبل قليل” يعطيك رسالة خطيرة: الحالة تتحرك. لا تتعامل معه كصورة ثابتة، بل كفيلم يتدهور أمامك.
