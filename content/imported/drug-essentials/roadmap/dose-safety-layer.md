# Dose Safety Layer

## Purpose
Why SEG separates drug education from final numeric dosing until verification.

## Core Rule
Drug knowledge in SEG is divided into two layers:

1. **Clinical thinking layer:** what the drug is for, how it works, what to monitor, and what can go wrong.
2. **Verified dosing layer:** final dose, route, concentration, maximum dose, pediatric adjustment, renal/hepatic adjustment, and local formulary rules.

## Why This Matters
Emergency drugs can harm patients quickly when the indication, physiology, or route is wrong. The safest learner does not ask, “What is the dose?” first. The safest learner asks:

- What problem am I treating?
- Is this the right drug for this physiology?
- What can kill the patient now?
- What monitoring must start before or immediately after administration?
- What source verifies the final dose in this hospital?

## Assessment Question
Which practice is safest when building a drug page for SEG?

A. Publish memorized doses without local verification.
B. Separate educational drug logic from the final verified dosing layer.
C. Remove monitoring because it makes the page longer.
D. Treat adult and pediatric dosing as the same.

**Correct Answer: B**
