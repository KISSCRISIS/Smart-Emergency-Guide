# Dobutamine

## Definition
**Dobutamine** is an emergency-medicine drug/topic commonly encountered in the ED, board review, and acute-care decision making.

## Core ED Uses
Cardiogenic shock with poor contractility when blood pressure can tolerate inotropy.

## Mechanism in Simple Terms
Beta-1 predominant inotropy with vasodilatory tendency.

## Dose Layer Status
**Do not publish as a bedside dosing protocol yet.**

- **Recommended verification sources:** local ED protocol, hospital formulary, DailyMed/FDA labeling where applicable, and specialty guidelines for ACLS/toxicology/antimicrobials.

## What to Monitor
- Vital signs and clinical response.
- Relevant ECG/rhythm effects when applicable.
- Respiratory status and airway safety when sedating or using opioids/benzodiazepines.
- Renal/hepatic function when relevant.
- Bleeding, allergy, extravasation, or infusion-related harm when relevant.
- Reassessment after intervention: did the drug solve the problem, reveal a new problem, or cause harm?

## Dangerous Caution / Contraindication Logic
This drug should never be used by name alone. Confirm indication, patient physiology, allergy history, pregnancy/pediatric context, renal/hepatic status, drug interactions, and local policy.

## Common Exam Trap
It may worsen hypotension if used before adequate pressure support.
