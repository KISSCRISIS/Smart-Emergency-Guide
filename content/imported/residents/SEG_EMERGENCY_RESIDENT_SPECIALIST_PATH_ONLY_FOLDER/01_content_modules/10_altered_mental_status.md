# Altered Mental Status

## Purpose

Give residents a broad, safe approach to altered mental status.

## Why It Matters

AMS is a presentation, not a diagnosis.

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Start with:

- airway/breathing/circulation
- glucose concept
- trauma
- toxidrome/overdose
- sepsis
- stroke/seizure
- metabolic/endocrine
- hypoxia/hypercapnia
- medication effect
- psychiatric only after dangerous medical causes considered

## Dangerous Diagnoses Not To Miss

- hypoglycemia
- opioid/sedative toxicity
- sepsis/meningitis
- intracranial bleed
- stroke
- seizure/nonconvulsive seizure concern
- CO/toxic exposure
- DKA/HHS
- hepatic/uremic encephalopathy
- electrolyte abnormality
- hypoxia/hypercapnia

## Decision Points

- protect airway?
- need CT/labs/LP/EEG consideration?
- intoxication vs infection vs neuro?
- capacity and safety
- ICU vs ward vs psych after medical clearance framework

## Cognitive Traps

- labeling as psychiatric too early
- missing hypoxia/hypercapnia
- ignoring medication list
- not checking for trauma
- accepting “baseline confused” without collateral

## Exam Trap

AMS cases often hinge on the first immediately reversible danger.
