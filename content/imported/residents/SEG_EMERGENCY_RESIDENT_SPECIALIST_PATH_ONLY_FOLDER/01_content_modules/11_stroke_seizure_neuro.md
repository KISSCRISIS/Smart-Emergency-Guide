# Stroke, Seizure & Neuro Emergencies

## Purpose

Give residents a structured approach to time-sensitive neurologic emergencies.

## Why It Matters

Neurologic emergencies depend on timeline, deficits, contraindications, mimics, and rapid communication.

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- last known well?
- focal deficit?
- seizure or postictal state?
- glucose/metabolic mimic?
- anticoagulant use?
- trauma?
- headache pattern?
- infection/meningitis concern?
- pregnancy/postpartum?
- disposition pathway?

## Dangerous Diagnoses Not To Miss

- ischemic stroke
- intracranial hemorrhage
- subarachnoid hemorrhage
- status epilepticus
- meningitis/encephalitis
- cerebral venous thrombosis
- spinal cord compression
- cauda equina
- myasthenic/neuromuscular respiratory failure

## Decision Points

- stroke pathway under local protocol
- CT/CTA/MRI considerations according to institution
- neurologist/neurosurgeon consult
- airway/respiratory monitoring
- seizure management under local protocol
- ICU need

## Cognitive Traps

- symptoms improved = safe
- vertigo = benign
- headache response to analgesia = safe
- seizure explains everything
- missing spinal red flags
- missing pregnancy/postpartum neuro risk

## Exam Trap

Know the dangerous mimic and the timeline-critical decision.
