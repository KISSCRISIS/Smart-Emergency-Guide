# Sepsis & Severe Infection

## Purpose

Help residents manage suspected severe infection as a risk syndrome.

## Why It Matters

Sepsis is time-sensitive, but patients vary widely. Residents must identify danger, source, and disposition.

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- Is the patient toxic or in shock?
- Is organ dysfunction suspected?
- What is the likely source?
- Is source control needed?
- What comorbidity increases risk?
- Is the patient immunocompromised?
- What disposition is safest?
- Who needs early consultation?

## Dangerous Sources

- pneumonia
- pyelonephritis/urosepsis
- meningitis/encephalitis
- abdominal sepsis
- necrotizing infection
- septic arthritis
- line/device infection
- postpartum infection
- febrile neutropenia
- pediatric serious infection

## Decision Points

- resuscitation priority
- empiric therapy according to local protocol
- cultures/labs/imaging according to local pathway
- source control
- ICU need
- reassessment after interventions
- consultant involvement

## Cognitive Traps

- waiting for high fever
- missing sepsis in elderly
- missing immunocompromised risk
- focusing on labs over appearance
- delaying source control discussion
- underestimating normal-looking early sepsis

## Exam Trap

Questions often test early recognition of severe infection and escalation rather than naming the organism.
