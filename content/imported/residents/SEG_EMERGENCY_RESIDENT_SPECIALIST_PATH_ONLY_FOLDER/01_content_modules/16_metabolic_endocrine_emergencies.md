# Metabolic & Endocrine Emergencies

## Purpose

Help residents recognize metabolic emergencies that mimic many presentations.

## Dangerous Diagnoses Not To Miss

- DKA/HHS
- severe hypoglycemia
- adrenal crisis
- thyroid storm
- myxedema coma
- hyperkalemia
- severe hyponatremia/hypernatremia
- hypercalcemia/hypocalcemia
- hypothermia/hyperthermia-related metabolic failure
- lactic acidosis/sepsis/toxicology overlap

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- mental status?
- breathing pattern?
- volume status?
- glucose?
- potassium/ECG concern?
- acid-base pattern?
- infection trigger?
- medication trigger?
- renal failure?
- endocrine history?

## Decision Points

- resuscitation priority
- electrolyte/acid-base danger
- ICU need
- endocrine/renal/ICU consultation
- monitor and reassess frequently
- avoid correcting numbers without understanding patient physiology

## Cognitive Traps

- treating glucose number only
- missing potassium danger
- ignoring respiratory compensation
- correcting sodium too casually
- missing adrenal crisis in shock
- missing thyroid storm/myxedema in atypical presentations

## Exam Trap

Metabolic questions often test physiologic pattern recognition.
