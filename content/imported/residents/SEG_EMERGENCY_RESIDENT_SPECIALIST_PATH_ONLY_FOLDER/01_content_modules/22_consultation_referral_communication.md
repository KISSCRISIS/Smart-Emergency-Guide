# Consultation & Referral Communication

## Purpose

Help residents communicate effectively with consultants and admitting teams.

## Why It Matters

Poor consultation delays care and creates conflict.

## Consultation Structure

Use:

1. who you are
2. who the patient is
3. current instability/risk
4. working diagnosis/differential
5. key data
6. what has changed
7. what you are requesting
8. urgency
9. disposition question

## High-Value Language

- “I am concerned because...”
- “The patient is unstable due to...”
- “The decision point is...”
- “I need your input on...”
- “This cannot wait because...”
- “Here is what changed since arrival...”

## Avoid

- vague consult: “Please assess”
- no question
- no urgency
- hiding uncertainty
- dumping incomplete work
- confrontational tone
- not documenting advice

## Cognitive Trap

Calling consultation late because you want a perfect diagnosis first.

## Exam Trap

Communication questions test clarity, urgency, and patient safety.
