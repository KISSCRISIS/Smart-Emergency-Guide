# OB/GYN & Pregnancy Emergencies

## Purpose

Give residents a high-risk approach to pregnancy and gynecologic emergencies.

## Why It Matters

Pregnancy/postpartum changes risk, differential, and consultation threshold.

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- pregnancy possible?
- gestational age?
- postpartum timing?
- bleeding?
- pain?
- syncope?
- headache/visual symptoms?
- seizure?
- chest pain/dyspnea?
- fever?
- fetal movement when relevant?
- intimate partner violence/safeguarding?

## Dangerous Diagnoses Not To Miss

- ectopic pregnancy
- hemorrhage
- preeclampsia/eclampsia
- PE
- sepsis
- stroke/CVT
- placental complications
- ovarian torsion
- PID/TOA
- sexual assault
- postpartum hemorrhage/infection

## Decision Points

- OB/GYN consultation
- ultrasound/labs under local pathway
- Rh/pregnancy-related testing concept
- resuscitation with pregnancy considerations
- fetal viability/local pathway
- admission/transfer needs

## Cognitive Traps

- missing pregnancy possibility
- treating postpartum symptoms as normal recovery
- migraine label in severe pregnancy headache
- missing PE in postpartum dyspnea
- underestimating bleeding/syncope

## Exam Trap

Always consider pregnancy-related catastrophe in reproductive-age abdominal pain/syncope.
