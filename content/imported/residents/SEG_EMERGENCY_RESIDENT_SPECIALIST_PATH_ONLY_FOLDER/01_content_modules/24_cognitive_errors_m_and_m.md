# Cognitive Errors & M&M Thinking

## Purpose

Help residents recognize diagnostic and system errors.

## Common Cognitive Errors

- anchoring
- premature closure
- confirmation bias
- availability bias
- search satisfaction
- diagnostic momentum
- overconfidence
- framing effect
- normal test bias
- attribution bias

## ED-Specific Risk Points

- shift change
- crowding
- interruptions
- pending results
- handover gaps
- high-risk discharge
- abnormal vitals ignored
- consultant conflict
- fatigue
- repeated visits

## Resident Safety Habits

- ask “what else could this be?”
- explain abnormal vitals
- reassess after interventions
- review pending results
- document uncertainty
- invite nursing concern
- use checklists for high-risk presentations
- close the loop on consultations

## M&M Reflection Template

- What was the presenting complaint?
- What was the working diagnosis?
- What was missed or delayed?
- What cognitive/system factors contributed?
- What would change next time?
- What process improvement is needed?

## Exam Trap

Many exam stems are cognitive-error traps disguised as clinical cases.
