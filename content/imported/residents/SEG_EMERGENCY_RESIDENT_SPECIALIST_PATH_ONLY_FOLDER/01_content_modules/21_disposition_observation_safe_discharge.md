# Disposition, Observation & Safe Discharge Thinking

## Purpose

Help residents make safer disposition decisions.

## Why It Matters

Disposition is one of the highest-risk ED decisions.

## Disposition Options

- discharge with safety-net
- observation/reassessment
- ward admission
- ICU admission
- urgent specialist pathway
- OR/cath lab/stroke pathway
- transfer
- psychiatric/crisis pathway
- social admission/safeguarding pathway

## Resident Disposition Questions

Ask:

- what dangerous diagnosis remains?
- what changed during ED stay?
- were abnormal vitals explained?
- were pending results checked?
- does the patient have capacity/support?
- is follow-up realistic?
- were red flags explained?
- did consultant agree if high-risk?
- is documentation clear?

## High-Risk Discharge Categories

Be cautious with:

- chest pain
- syncope
- abdominal pain
- headache
- elderly/frail
- pediatrics
- pregnancy/postpartum
- anticoagulated patients
- immunocompromised patients
- mental health/self-harm
- intoxication
- abnormal vital signs
- unclear diagnosis with persistent symptoms

## Cognitive Traps

- normal test = safe
- improved symptoms = safe
- busy ED pressure
- discharge before pending result
- incomplete safety-net
- social reality ignored
- junior discharge without review

## Exam Trap

Disposition questions often test who is unsafe to discharge, even if initial tests seem reassuring.
