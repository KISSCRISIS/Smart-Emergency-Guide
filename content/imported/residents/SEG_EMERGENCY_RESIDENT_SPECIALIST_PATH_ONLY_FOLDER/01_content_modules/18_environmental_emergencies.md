# Environmental Emergencies

## Purpose

Give residents a high-yield framework for heat, cold, drowning, bites, stings, and exposure.

## Dangerous Diagnoses

- heat stroke
- severe hypothermia
- drowning/submersion injury
- carbon monoxide exposure
- electrical injury
- lightning injury
- venomous bites/stings
- altitude/diving pressure injuries depending on setting
- chemical exposure

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- exposure type?
- duration?
- environment?
- multiple patients?
- mental status?
- temperature?
- trauma?
- toxic inhalation?
- cardiac rhythm/ECG concern?
- need public health/toxicology/specialist involvement?

## Decision Points

- ABCDE first
- decontamination/scene safety according to local protocol
- temperature-related resuscitation concepts
- toxin/exposure consultation
- observation vs admission risk
- public health/occupational implications

## Cognitive Traps

- missing CO when multiple people have vague symptoms
- underestimating heat illness with altered mental status
- ignoring trauma in drowning/electrical injury
- treating bite/sting without systemic risk assessment

## Exam Trap

Environmental questions often test the exposure clue and the first safety priority.
