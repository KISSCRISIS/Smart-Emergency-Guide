# Procedures: Decision, Consent & Complications

## Purpose

Provide resident-level procedural decision framing without step-by-step instructions.

## Why It Matters

Procedures fail when indication, consent, equipment, supervision, and complications are not planned.

## Resident Procedure Framework

Before any procedure, clarify:

- indication
- urgency
- alternatives
- contraindications
- consent
- patient capacity
- anticoagulation/bleeding risk
- infection risk
- anatomy/ultrasound need
- equipment
- assistant/team roles
- analgesia/sedation plan under local protocol
- complication plan
- post-procedure confirmation/documentation

## High-Risk ED Procedures

- airway procedures
- central/arterial access
- chest decompression/chest tube
- procedural sedation
- cardioversion/defibrillation
- fracture/dislocation reduction
- lumbar puncture
- paracentesis/thoracentesis
- abscess drainage
- wound repair
- pelvic procedures
- pediatric procedures

## What This Page Should Not Include

- procedural step-by-step instructions
- medication doses
- local credentialing claims
- equipment-size tables
- sedation recipes

## Cognitive Traps

- doing procedure without clear indication
- poor consent/documentation
- not preparing for complications
- inadequate post-procedure reassessment
- overconfidence without supervision

## Exam Trap

Procedure questions often test indication, contraindication, or complication recognition.
