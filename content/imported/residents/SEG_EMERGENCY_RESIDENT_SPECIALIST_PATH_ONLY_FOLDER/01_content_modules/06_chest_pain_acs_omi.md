# Chest Pain, ACS & OMI Thinking

## Purpose

Give residents a risk-focused approach to chest pain and ischemia.

## Why It Matters

Chest pain decisions carry high risk: missed ACS, missed dissection, missed PE, unsafe discharge, delayed activation.

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- Is the patient unstable?
- Is this ischemic until proven otherwise?
- Are there STEMI or STEMI-equivalent concerns?
- Does the ECG match symptoms?
- What dangerous non-ACS causes fit?
- What serial data are needed?
- What disposition risk remains after initial tests?

## Dangerous Diagnoses Not To Miss

- ACS/OMI
- aortic dissection
- pulmonary embolism
- pneumothorax
- pericarditis/tamponade
- myocarditis
- esophageal rupture
- severe arrhythmia
- pneumonia/sepsis

## Decision Points

- ECG interpretation and repeat ECGs when appropriate
- symptom timeline
- risk factors and atypical presentations
- troponin concept and serial testing according to local protocol
- consultant/cath lab discussion when indicated
- observation vs admission vs discharge risk

## Disposition Implications

Disposition depends on:
- symptoms
- ECG evolution
- biomarkers under local protocol
- risk features
- comorbidities
- alternative dangerous diagnoses
- response and reassessment

## Cognitive Traps

- normal first ECG = safe
- young patient = safe
- pleuritic pain = not cardiac
- reproducible pain = no ACS
- one negative test = discharge
- anchoring on reflux/anxiety

## Exam Trap

Board-style chest pain questions often test the next action when initial ECG is nondiagnostic but suspicion remains high.
