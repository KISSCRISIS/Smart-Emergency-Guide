# Pediatric Emergency Decision-Making

## Purpose

Help residents approach pediatric emergencies safely.

## Why It Matters

Children compensate differently and may deteriorate quickly.

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- age?
- appearance?
- work of breathing?
- circulation/perfusion?
- mental status?
- hydration?
- caregiver concern?
- fever risk?
- trauma/safeguarding?
- medication/toxicology?
- weight-based care under verified protocol?

## Dangerous Diagnoses Not To Miss

- sepsis/meningitis
- respiratory failure
- dehydration/shock
- nonaccidental trauma
- seizure/status
- DKA
- toxic ingestion
- surgical abdomen
- testicular/ovarian torsion
- congenital/cardiac disease
- anaphylaxis

## Decision Points

- pediatric escalation early
- family-centered communication
- weight/age-specific risk
- safe disposition
- observation vs admission
- transfer to pediatric-capable facility depending on local resources

## Cognitive Traps

- child looks better briefly = safe
- parental concern ignored
- adult vital sign assumptions
- missing safeguarding
- underestimating dehydration
- delayed pediatric consultation

## Exam Trap

Pediatric emergency questions often test age-specific danger and subtle shock.
