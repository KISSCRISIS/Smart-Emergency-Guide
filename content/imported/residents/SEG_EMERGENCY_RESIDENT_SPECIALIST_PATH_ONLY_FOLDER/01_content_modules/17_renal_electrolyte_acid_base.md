# Renal / Electrolyte / Acid-Base Emergencies

## Purpose

Help residents connect renal function, electrolytes, ECG, and acid-base physiology.

## Dangerous Diagnoses

- hyperkalemia with ECG concern
- severe hyponatremia with neurologic symptoms
- severe hypernatremia/dehydration
- acute kidney injury with systemic illness
- uremic complications
- metabolic acidosis
- toxic alcohol/salicylate acid-base patterns
- rhabdomyolysis
- dialysis emergencies

## Resident First-Pass Thinking

Ask:

- is there ECG danger?
- mental status?
- seizure?
- renal failure?
- medication cause?
- acid-base pattern?
- volume status?
- need renal/ICU consultation?
- is dialysis consideration urgent under local practice?

## Decision Points

- cardiac monitoring
- ECG interpretation
- repeat labs when needed
- cause of derangement
- ICU/nephrology involvement
- avoid overcorrection
- disposition risk

## Cognitive Traps

- waiting for lab confirmation when ECG suggests danger
- treating potassium without reassessment
- ignoring medication list
- missing mixed acid-base disorders
- overlooking dialysis patient complications

## Exam Trap

Electrolyte questions often hinge on ECG/neuro symptoms and physiologic urgency.
