# Resident Tools — ED Leadership Mini-Templates

## 1. Resus Team Brief

“Team, this is a [age] patient with [presentation]. Immediate concern is [life threat]. Priorities are airway/breathing/circulation, monitoring, access, and identifying [danger differential]. I need [person] on airway, [person] on access/meds under protocol, [person] on documentation/time, and [person] to contact [consultant]. We will reassess in [short interval according to situation].”

## 3. Consultant Call

“I am calling about a [age] patient with [presentation]. I am concerned about [danger diagnosis]. Current instability/risk is [features]. Key findings are [data]. I need [specific request] because [urgency].”

## 4. High-Risk Disposition Pause

Before discharge/observation/admission decision:

- Are vitals explained?
- Are symptoms improved and why?
- Any dangerous diagnosis still plausible?
- Any pending result?
- Any consultant recommendation?
- Any social/support issue?
- Is follow-up realistic?
- Was safety-net documented?
- Would I be comfortable defending this decision?

## 5. Junior Supervision Check

Ask the intern/student:

- What is the main problem?
- What are the red flags?
- What is the worst diagnosis?
- What result is pending?
- What would make you call me immediately?
- What is the disposition concern?
