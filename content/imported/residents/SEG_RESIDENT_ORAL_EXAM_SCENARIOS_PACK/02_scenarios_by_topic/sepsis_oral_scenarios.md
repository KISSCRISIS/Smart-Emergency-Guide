# Sepsis Oral Scenarios

All scenarios are original SEG educational oral-exam cases, not official exam questions.

---

## Scenario 1: Elderly Confusion and Infection

**Original SEG oral-exam scenario — not an official exam question.**

### Examiner opening

“You are the emergency resident. An 82-year-old arrives with new confusion, weakness, and suspected urinary infection. Tell me your first steps.”

### Candidate first move

The candidate should begin with:
- ABCDE and stability assessment
- call for help / team organization if unstable
- monitor and reassess
- identify life threats before detailed diagnosis
- request focused vital signs, history, examination, and time-critical tests

### Dangerous differential

- sepsis
- stroke
- hypoglycemia
- medication/toxicologic cause
- dehydration/electrolyte abnormality

### Expected answer outline

- ABCDE, glucose, source search, organ dysfunction, cultures/labs/imaging per local protocol, early antibiotics concept without doses, disposition likely admission/ICU if unstable.

### Data the examiner may reveal if requested

- General appearance and mental status
- Full vital signs and trend
- Focused history
- Focused exam
- ECG when relevant
- Glucose when AMS/seizure/metabolic/toxicologic concern exists
- Labs/imaging according to the candidate’s reasoning
- POCUS findings only if the candidate asks a focused POCUS question
- Response to local-protocol interventions if candidate states them conceptually

### Case evolution prompt

“The patient’s condition changes / new data arrives. How does this change your differential, consultation, and disposition?”

### Disposition expectation

Candidate should state:
- likely safe location of care
- need for consultant/ICU/OR/cath/stroke/OB/peds/toxicology discussion when relevant
- what must be reassessed
- what would make discharge unsafe

### Critical failure / common trap

- Candidate waits for high fever or normalizes confusion as dementia.

### Arabic pearl

في الأورال، لا تبحث عن اسم التشخيص فقط؛ اشرح الخطر والقرار ومن يجب أن تتصل به.
