# Candidate Answer Framework

Use this structure for every oral scenario.

## 1. First Sentence

“I will first assess whether the patient is unstable and approach with ABCDE while calling for help if needed.”

## 2. Immediate Safety

State:
- airway risk
- breathing risk
- circulation/shock risk
- mental status
- monitoring/access/support
- need for senior/consultant/team activation

## 3. Focused Differential

Always split into:
- immediately life-threatening
- likely diagnosis
- dangerous mimics

## 4. Data Requests

Ask for:
- vital signs and trend
- focused history
- focused exam
- ECG when relevant
- glucose when AMS/seizure/metabolic possible
- labs/imaging only after stabilization priorities
- POCUS when it answers a focused question

## 5. Case Evolution

When new data arrives:
- reassess stability
- update differential
- state next decision
- call consultant/ICU/OR/cath/stroke/OB/peds/tox as needed
- choose disposition

## 6. Disposition Statement

End with:
- where patient goes
- why
- who was consulted
- what must be reassessed
- what cannot be missed

## 7. Critical Failure Avoidance

Never:
- ignore unstable vitals
- delay ABCDE for complete history
- reassure after one normal test
- miss pregnancy possibility
- miss toxicology/self-harm risk
- miss pediatric red flags
- discharge high-risk patient without clear reasoning
