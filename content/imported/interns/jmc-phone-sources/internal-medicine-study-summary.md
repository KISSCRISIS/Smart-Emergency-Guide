## Chapter 1: Endocrinology (علم الغدد الصماء)

### Pituitary Gland (الغدة النخامية)

- ** Anatomy**: Surrounded by sphenoid bone, covered by sellar diaphragm, lies in sella turcica under optic chiasm
- ** Two lobes **:
- ** Posterior (neurohypophysis)**: Stores ADH & oxytocin (produced in hypothalamus)
- ** Anterior (adenohypophysis)**: Produces GH, Prolactin, TSH, ACTH, LH, FSH
- Regulated by hypothalamic releasing/inhibiting hormones

#### Anterior Pituitary Diseases :

** Prolactinoma** (Most common pituitary mass)
- ** Microadenomas** (<1cm): more common in women
- ** Macroadenomas** (>1cm): more common in men, may cause visual defects
- ** Causes**: Physiologic (pregnancy, stress, nipple stimulation), drugs (phenothiazines, metoclopramide, SSRIs), hypothyroidism, renal/liver failure
- ** Symptoms in women**: Galactorrhea, amenorrhea, infertility, osteoporosis
- ** Symptoms in men**: Hypogonadism, erectile dysfunction, decreased libido
- ** Diagnosis**: Prolactin >100 ng/mL, MRI for tumor
- ** Treatment**: Dopamine agonists (bromocriptine, cabergoline) → surgery → radiation

** Acromegaly** (Gigantism in children)
- ** Cause**: GH-secreting macroadenoma (most common)
- ** Features**: Enlarged hands/feet, coarse facial features, prognathism, sweating, sleep apnea, organomegaly, HTN, DM
- ** Diagnosis**: IGF-1 (initial), GH suppression test with glucose (most accurate)
- ** Treatment**: Surgery (primary), octreotide (best medical), dopamine agonists, pegvisomant

** Hypopituitarism** (loss of anterior pituitary)
- Order of loss: **FSH/LH → GH → TSH → ACTH** (first lost, last lost)
- ** Causes**: Tumors, pituitary apoplexy (emergency!), Sheehan syndrome (postpartum necrosis → first sign: no lactation)
- ** Treatment**: Hormone replacement; start with **cortisol** first

** Empty Sella Syndrome **
- Herniation of subarachnoid space; most common in multiparous women
- Treatment: Reassurance only (benign)

#### Posterior Pituitary Diseases :

** Diabetes Insipidus (DI) **
- ** Central DI**: Deficiency of ADH (tumors, trauma, surgery, meningitis)
- ** Nephrogenic DI**: Kidney resistance to ADH (hypercalcemia, hypokalemia, lithium, amyloidosis)
- ** Features**: Polyuria (16-20L/day), polydipsia, nocturia
- ** Diagnosis**: Hypernatremia, high serum osmolarity, low urine osmolarity
- ** Treatment**: Desmopressin (central); HCTZ, amiloride (nephrogenic)

** SIADH** (Syndrome of Inappropriate ADH)
- ** Causes**: Small cell lung cancer, CNS disorders, drugs (carbamazepine, chlorpropamide)
- ** Features**: Water retention (no edema), hyponatremia (Na <130), low serum osmolarity, high urine Na
- ** Treatment**: Fluid restriction (800-1000mL/day), hypertonic saline, vaptans (tolvaptan)
- ** Correction rate**: 0.5-1 mmol/L/hour (max); faster → central pontine myelinolysis

### Thyroid Gland (الغدة الدرقية)

** Key Diagnostic Tests :**
- ** TSH**: Most sensitive for thyroid function
- ** Free T4/T3**: Reflect true functional status
- ** RAIU**: Increased in Graves, decreased in thyroiditis
- ** Antibodies**: Anti-TPO/anti-thyroglobulin → Hashimoto; TSI → Graves

#### Hyperthyroidism (فرط الدرقية)

** Causes :**
1. ** Graves' disease** (most common): Autoimmune (TSI antibodies), diffuse goiter, exophthalmos, pretibial myxedema
2. ** Toxic nodule** (Plummer disease): Common in elderly, associated with arrhythmia and CHF
3. ** Subacute thyroiditis**: Viral, painful goiter, high ESR, low RAIU
4. ** Factitious thyrotoxicosis**: Exogenous thyroid hormone

** Clinical Features :**
- Atrial fibrillation, tremor, heat intolerance, weight loss, sweating, palpitations
- Warm moist skin, palmar erythema, lid lag, infrequent blinking

** Treatment :**
- ** Methimazole**: Drug of choice (longer half-life, fewer side effects)
- ** Propylthiouracil (PTU)**: Use in **first trimester of pregnancy**, then switch to methimazole
- ** Both** can cause agranulocytosis !
- ** Radioactive iodine**: For large goiter, high T4, high TSI; causes hypothyroidism
- ** Surgery**: 2nd trimester pregnancy, children, compressive symptoms
- ** Beta-blockers** (propranolol): Symptom control

** Thyroid Storm** (Emergency!)
- ** Precipitants**: Infection, surgery, trauma, stress
- ** Features**: Hyperthermia, tachycardia, delirium, vomiting, diarrhea, hypotension
- ** Treatment**: Antithyroid drugs, iodine (Wolff-Chaikoff effect), beta-blockers, dexamethasone, cooling

---

#### Hypothyroidism (قصور الدرقية)

** Causes :**
1. ** Hashimoto's thyroiditis** (most common in developed countries)
2. Iodine deficiency (most common worldwide)
3. Post-ablative (surgery/RAI)
4. Drugs: lithium, amiodarone

** Clinical Features :**
- ** Cretinism (infants)**: Prolonged jaundice, hoarse cry, constipation, delayed milestones, coarse features, protruding tongue, umbilical hernia
- ** Adults**: Lethargy, cold intolerance, weight gain, dry skin, constipation, menorrhagia, slow reflexes, carpal tunnel syndrome, high cholesterol, periorbital puffiness

** Diagnosis :**
- Primary hypothyroidism: **↑ TSH, ↓ T4 **
- Secondary/tertiary: Normal or ↓ TSH, ↓ T4
- Associated with: ↑ Cholesterol, ↑ CPK, ↑ AST, hyponatremia, pernicious anemia (12%)

** Treatment :**
- ** Levothyroxine (T4)** : Monitor TSH (takes 6 weeks to stabilize)
- Take on empty stomach; avoid calcium/iron supplements
- In pregnancy: ↑ demand, monitor closely (q4-6 weeks until 20 weeks)

** Myxedema Coma** (Emergency!)
- Precipitated by: Cold, infection, trauma, CNS depressants
- Features: Hypothermia, stupor, respiratory depression
- Treatment: High-dose T4 + T3

---

#### Thyroiditis (التهاب الدرقية)

| Type | Cause | Features | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|------|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| ** Subacute (De Quervain)** | Viral | Painful goiter, fever, referred pain to jaw/ear | High ESR, ↓ RAIU, high T3/T4 | NSAIDs, prednisone, propranolol |
| ** Hashimoto** | Autoimmune | Painless, rubbery goiter, hypothyroidism | ↑ TSH, ↓ T4, anti-TPO antibodies | Levothyroxine |
| ** Lymphocytic (Silent)** | Unknown | Painless, firm, symmetrical goiter, hyperthyroidism | High T3/T4, ↓ RAIU, normal ESR | Propranolol (self-limited) |
| ** Riedel** | Fibrotic | Intense fibrosis of thyroid and surrounding structures | Clinical | Surgical |

---

#### Thyroid Neoplasia (أورام الدرقية)

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| ** Papillary** (70-80%) | Most common, women, lymphatic spread, psammoma bodies; treatment: surgery ± radiation |
| ** Follicular** (5-10%) | Elderly, women, hematogenous spread; NOT diagnosed by FNA (need capsular/vascular invasion) |
| ** Anaplastic** | Elderly, highly malignant, rapid painful enlargement, direct extension, death within 1 year |
| ** Medullary** | Parafollicular C-cells, produces calcitonin, MEN IIA/IIB; treatment: surgery |

** When to suspect thyroid cancer**: Recent growth, radiation history, solitary nodule, elevated calcitonin, calcification on X-ray, psammoma bodies

### Parathyroid Glands (الغدة جار الدرقية)

** Calcium Regulation**: 3 hormones (PTH, calcitonin, Vitamin D) × 3 tissues (bone, kidney, intestine)

#### Hypercalcemia (فرط الكالسيوم)

** Causes :**
1. ** Primary hyperparathyroidism** (most common)
2. Malignancy (PTH-related protein)
3. Granulomatous disease (sarcoidosis, TB) → ↑1,25-vitamin D
4. Vitamin D intoxication, thiazides, lithium
5. Paget's disease, hyperthyroidism, immobilization

** Features**: "Stones, bones, abdominal groans, and psychiatric moans "
- CNS: Confusion, coma, seizures
- GIT: Constipation, anorexia, nausea, pancreatitis
- Renal: Polyuria, polydipsia, nephrolithiasis
- CVS: HTN, short QT interval

** Treatment :**
- Normal saline + furosemide
- IV bisphosphonates (zoledronate, pamidronate)
- Calcitonin

** Primary Hyperparathyroidism **
- ** Causes**: Single adenoma (most common), 4-gland hyperplasia, carcinoma, MEN I/IIA
- ** Features**: Osteitis fibrosa cystica (bone cysts, brown tumors), hypercalcemia, hypophosphatemia
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ PTH, ↑ Ca (>10.5), ↓ PO4 (<2.5), sestamibi scan
- ** Surgery indications**: Symptomatic hypercalcemia, Ca >11.5, renal insufficiency, age <50, nephrolithiasis, osteoporosis

---

#### Hypocalcemia (نقص الكالسيوم)

** Causes :**
1. Hypoparathyroidism (most common: post-thyroidectomy)
2. Renal failure, hyperphosphatemia, hypomagnesemia
3. Drugs: loop diuretics, phenytoin, bisphosphonates
4. Pseudo-hypocalcemia (low albumin)

** Features :**
- Neuromuscular irritability: Tetany, seizures, paresthesias
- ** Chvostek sign**: Facial twitching on tapping facial nerve
- ** Trousseau sign**: Carpal spasm with BP cuff inflation
- Prolonged QT interval, cataracts

** Treatment**: IV calcium gluconate, oral calcium (2-4 g/day), vitamin D, phosphate binders

---

### Diabetes Mellitus (داء السكري)

** Definition**: Disorder of carbohydrate metabolism with absolute or relative insulin deficiency → hyperglycemia → end-organ damage

** Classification :**

| Feature | Type 1 DM | Type 2 DM |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Age | Young | >40 years |
| Body habitus | Lean | Obese |
| Family history | Uncommon | >20% first-degree |
| Ketosis | Prone | Not prone |
| Autoantibodies | Positive (islet cell, GAD) | Negative |
| HLA association | HLA-DR3, DR4 | None |
| Treatment | Insulin required | Oral agents, then insulin |

** Diagnostic Criteria :**
1. Random glucose >200 + symptoms
2. ** FBS ≥126 mg/dL** (twice)
3. **2- hour OGTT ≥200 mg/dL **
4. ** HbA1c ≥6.5% **

---

#### Pharmacological Treatment

** Oral Hypoglycemics (Type 2 DM) :**

| Drug Class | Examples | Mechanism | Key Points |
|------------|----------|-----------|------------|
| ** Metformin** | Glucophage | ↓ hepatic glucose production | **First-line**, no hypoglycemia, no weight gain, contraindicated in renal failure; side effects: GI upset (most common), lactic acidosis |
| ** Sulfonylureas** | Glyburide, glipizide | ↑ insulin secretion | Weight gain, hypoglycemia, sulfa allergy cross-reactivity |
| ** Thiazolidinediones** | Rosiglitazone, pioglitazone | ↓ insulin resistance | Worsens CHF, bladder cancer risk, hepatotoxicity |
| ** GLP-1 agonists** | Exenatide, liraglutide | ↑ insulin, ↓ glucagon, slows gastric emptying | Injectable, weight loss, GI side effects |
| ** DPP-4 inhibitors** | Sitagliptin, saxagliptin | ↑ GLP-1 | Oral, weight neutral |
| ** SGLT2 inhibitors** | - | ↑ urinary glucose excretion | Weight loss, UTI risk |

** Insulin Regimens :**
- ** Old regimen**: 0.5 U/kg; 2/3 morning (2/3 NPH + 1/3 Regular), 1/3 evening (1/2 NPH + 1/2 Regular)
- ** New regimen**: 0.5 U/kg; 50% basal (glargine) + 50% bolus (lispro/aspart) before meals

---

#### Acute Complications

** Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA) **
- ** Etiology**: Severe insulin deficiency (Type 1 DM)
- ** Precipitants**: Infection (most common), missed insulin, stress
- ** Features**: Nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing, fruity breath, dehydration, confusion
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ Glucose, ketones, high anion gap metabolic acidosis, hyperkalemia (with total body K depletion)
- ** Treatment **:
1. Normal saline
2. ** Insulin** (bolus 5-10 U then IV infusion)
3. Add dextrose when glucose 200-250
4. K+ replacement
5. Bicarbonate if pH <7.0

** Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HONK) **
- ** Etiology**: Type 2 DM with dehydration
- ** Features**: Glucose >700 mg/dL, high osmolarity, NO ketosis, NO acidosis (or mild)
- ** Treatment**: Aggressive IV fluids + insulin

---

#### Chronic Complications

** Cardiovascular** (#1 cause of death)
- Lipid targets: LDL <100 (or <70 with CVD), HDL >50, TG <150
- Most common dyslipidemia: **High TG + Low HDL **

** Renal **
- Screen annually for microalbuminuria
- ** Microalbuminuria**: 30-300 mg/24h → treat with ACEI/ARB

** Eye (Retinopathy) **
- Type 2: Screen at diagnosis, then annually
- Type 1: Screen after 5 years, then annually
- Proliferative retinopathy: Laser photocoagulation

** Neuropathy **
- Most common: Peripheral neuropathy (numbness, paresthesia, pain, loss of reflexes/vibration)
- Mononeuropathy: Foot drop, wrist drop, cranial nerve palsies (CN III, IV, VI)
- Autonomic neuropathy: Orthostatic hypotension, gastroparesis, ED, bladder dysfunction

** Foot Care**: Annual podiatric examination

** Special Concepts :**
- ** Honeymoon period**: Symptom-free interval after DKA (stress-induced epinephrine blocks insulin)
- ** Somogyi effect**: Midnight hypoglycemia → rebound morning hyperglycemia
- ** Dawn phenomenon**: Morning hyperglycemia from counter-regulatory hormones

---

### Hypoglycemia (نقص السكر)

** Clinical Features **:
- ** Adrenergic**: Sweating, tremor, tachycardia, anxiety, hunger
- ** Neuroglycopenic**: Dizziness, confusion, blurred vision, seizures, coma

** Classification :**
- ** Postprandial**: After gastrectomy, hyperinsulinism
- ** Fasting**: Hormone deficiencies (panhypopituitarism, adrenal insufficiency), liver disease, drugs

** Insulinoma** (pancreatic β-cell tumor)
- ** Features**: Blurred vision, headache, weakness, early morning/late afternoon symptoms, responds to feeding
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ Insulin, ↓ Glucose (<40), imaging (CT, US, angiography)
- ** Treatment**: Surgery, frequent meals

** Factitious Hyperinsulinism**: Self-administered insulin in healthcare workers

| Test | Insulinoma | Exogenous Insulin | Sulfonylureas |
|------|------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Plasma insulin | High | Very high | High |
| C-peptide | Increased | Low/normal | Increased |
| Insulin antibodies | Absent | +/- Present | Absent |
| Sulfonylurea level | Absent | Absent | Present |

** Ethanol-induced hypoglycemia**: Occurs after fasting + alcohol consumption

---

### Adrenal Gland (الغدة الكظرية)

#### Cushing Syndrome (متلازمة كوشينغ)

** Definition**: Prolonged exposure to excess cortisol

** Causes :**
1. ** Exogenous steroids** (most common cause overall)
2. ** Pituitary ACTH adenoma** (most common endogenous cause = Cushing's disease)
3. Ectopic ACTH (small cell lung cancer, thymus, pancreatic cancer)
4. Adrenal neoplasia

** Features:** "Moon face, buffalo hump, truncal obesity "
- HTN, muscle weakness, osteoporosis, cutaneous striae, easy bruising
- Acne, hirsutism, amenorrhea, emotional changes
- Glucose intolerance → DM, hypokalemia, leukocytosis, delayed wound healing

** Diagnosis :**
1. **1 mg overnight dexamethasone suppression test** (initial screening)
2. **24- hour urine free cortisol** (most accurate)
3. Midnight salivary cortisol
4. ACTH level, high-dose dexamethasone suppression test
5. CT/MRI, petrosal sinus sampling

** Treatment**: Surgery (first line), medical (ketoconazole, metyrapone)

---

#### Hyperaldosteronism (فرط الألدوستيرونية)

** Aldosterone effects**: Reabsorb Na⁺ + H₂O, excrete K⁺ + H ⁺

** Primary Hyperaldosteronism** (Conn syndrome)
- ** Most common cause**: Adrenal adenoma
- ** Features**: HTN, hypokalemia, muscle weakness, polyuria/polydipsia, metabolic alkalosis
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ Aldosterone, **↓ plasma renin activity** (key difference from secondary!)
- ** Treatment**: Surgery (adenoma), spironolactone (bilateral hyperplasia)

** Secondary Hyperaldosteronism **
- ** Causes**: Dehydration, hemorrhage, renovascular disease
- ** Features**: ↑ Aldosterone, **↑ plasma renin activity **
- ** Treatment**: Treat underlying cause

** Bartter Syndrome **
- Defect in Na-K-2Cl cotransporter in loop of Henle
- Features: Hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis, ↑ renin + aldosterone

---

#### Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia

**21- Hydroxylase Deficiency** (most common)
- ↓ Aldosterone → Hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, dehydration, hypotension
- ↑ Androgens → **Female: ambiguous genitalia**, male: precocious puberty

**11- Hydroxylase Deficiency **
- Early: ↓ Aldosterone → hypotension, hyperkalemia
- Later: ↑ Androgens → hypertension, hypokalemia

**17- Hydroxylase Deficiency**: Hypogonadism, hypokalemia, hypertension

** Treatment**: Glucocorticoid replacement

---

#### Adrenal Insufficiency

** Primary (Addison's Disease) **
- ** Most common cause**: Autoimmune adrenalitis
- ** Features**: Weakness, hyperpigmentation (↑ ACTH), weight loss, hypotension, hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, hypoglycemia, eosinophilia, lymphocytosis
- ** Diagnosis**: **Cosyntropin (ACTH) stimulation test** (most accurate)

** Secondary Adrenal Insufficiency **
- ** Causes**: Pituitary disease, sudden steroid withdrawal
- ** Features**: **NO hyperpigmentation**, normal electrolytes

** Treatment**: Glucocorticoid (hydrocortisone) + mineralocorticoid (fludrocortisone)

** Adrenal Crisis** (Emergency!)
- ** Precipitants**: Surgery, infection, trauma, abrupt steroid withdrawal
- ** Features**: Fever, abdominal pain, vomiting, altered mental status, collapse
- ** Treatment**: IV hydrocortisone, IV fluids

---

#### Pheochromocytoma (ورم القواتم)

** Definition**: Tumor of chromaffin cells (sympathetic nervous system)

** Rule of 10%**: 10% bilateral, 10% extra-adrenal, 10% malignant, 10% familial, 10% in children

** Familial**: MEN IIA, MEN IIB, von Recklinghausen NF, von Hippel-Lindau

** Catecholamine Secretion :**
- Norepinephrine: HTN (most common)
- Epinephrine: Tachycardia, sweating, flushing
- Dopamine: Familial syndromes, no HTN

** Features**: Paroxysmal headache, sweating, palpitations, HTN, anxiety, weight loss

** Diagnosis :**
1. **24- hour urinary VMA, metanephrines, catecholamines** (initial)
2. Clonidine suppression test
3. CT/MRI for localization, MIBG scan

** Treatment**: **Alpha-blockers FIRST** (phenoxybenzamine), then beta-blockers, then surgical removal

---

### Testicular/Hypogonadism (قصور الغدد التناسلية)

** Primary Hypogonadism** (Testicular failure)
- ** Causes**: Klinefelter syndrome, anorchia, mumps orchitis, chemotherapy, castration
- ** Labs**: **↑ LH, ↑ FSH**, ↓ Testosterone

** Secondary Hypogonadism** (Pituitary/Hypothalamic)
- ** Causes**: Hypopituitarism, Kallmann syndrome (hypogonadism + anosmia)
- ** Labs**: **↓ LH, ↓ FSH**, ↓ Testosterone

** Treatment**: Testosterone replacement

---

### Gynecomastia (التثدي)

** Causes :**
- Physiologic: Infants, puberty
- Liver cirrhosis, Klinefelter's, testicular cancer, chronic renal failure, hyperthyroidism
- Drugs: Spironolactone, CCBs, ketoconazole, methyldopa, cimetidine, digoxin, alcohol, heroin, marijuana

---

### Metabolic Syndrome (المتلازمة الأيضية)

** Criteria** (≥3 of 5) :
1. Abdominal obesity: Waist ≥94 cm (men), ≥80 cm (women)
2. TG ≥150 mg/dL
3. HDL <40 (men), <50 (women) mg/dL
4. BP ≥130/85
5. Fasting glucose ≥100 mg/dL

## Chapter 2: Rheumatology (أمراض الروماتيزم)

### Approach to Joint Disease

** Key Differentiating Factors :**
- ** Polyarticular**: RA, SLE, parvovirus, HBV
- ** Monoarticular**: Gout, pseudogout, septic arthritis
- ** Migratory**: Rheumatic fever, Lyme disease, gonococcal infection
- ** Oligoarticular**: Ankylosing spondylitis, osteoarthritis

** Joint Inflammation Signs :**
- Morning stiffness >1 hour
- Joint erythema, warmth
- ↑ ESR, ↑ CRP

---

### Joint Aspiration Analysis

| Disease | WBCs | Crystals |
|---------|------|----------|
| Osteoarthritis | <2,000 | None |
| Inflammatory | 5,000-50,000 | Gout: needle-shaped, negative birefringent |
| Pseudogout | 5,000-50,000 | CPPD: rhomboid-shaped, positive birefringent |
| Septic | >50,000 | Negative (culture may be negative if prior antibiotics) |

---

### Autoantibodies

| Antibody | Associated Disease |
|----------|-------------------|
| Anti-dsDNA | SLE only (activity, lupus nephritis) |
| Anti-Sm | SLE only (25-30%) |
| Anti-histone | Drug-induced lupus (95%) |
| Anti-Ro (SSA) | Neonatal lupus, Sjögren's, 3% ANA-negative lupus |
| Anti-La (SSB) | Sjögren's |
| Anti-centromere | CREST syndrome |
| Anti-RNP | Mixed connective tissue disease (100%) |
| c-ANCA | Wegener's (GPA) |
| p-ANCA | Polyarteritis nodosa, Churg-Strauss |

---

### Rheumatoid Arthritis (التهاب المفاصل الرثياني)

** Risk Factors**: Female, 35-50 years, smoking, infections

** Diagnostic Criteria** (≥4 of the following) :
1. Morning stiffness >1 hour for ≥6 weeks
2. Swelling of wrist, MCP, PIP for ≥6 weeks
3. Swelling of ≥3 joints for ≥6 weeks
4. Symmetric joint swelling for ≥6 weeks
5. RF positive or anti-CCP positive
6. ↑ CRP, ↑ ESR

** Joints NEVER involved**: DIP, lower back

** Extra-articular Features :**
- ** Rheumatoid nodules** (over pressure areas: olecranon, Achilles)
- ** Felty syndrome**: RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia
- ** Caplan syndrome**: RA + pneumoconiosis

** Deformities**: Boutonnière, Swan-neck, ulnar deviation, radial deviation of wrist

** Treatment :**
1. ** NSAIDs** (COX-2 inhibitors: avoid in CVD)
2. ** Glucocorticoids** (short course only)
3. ** DMARDs **:
- ** Methotrexate** (best initial)
- Hydroxychloroquine, Sulfasalazine, Gold
- ** TNF inhibitors** (infliximab, adalimumab): must screen for TB first !

** Complications **:
- ** Atlantoaxial subluxation**: Neurologic symptoms, check X-ray before intubation
- ** Ruptured Baker's cyst**: Swollen painful calf

---

### Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) (الذئبة الحمراء)

** Diagnostic Criteria** (≥4 of 11) :
1. Malar rash (butterfly)
2. Discoid rash (scarring)
3. Photosensitivity
4. Arthritis (non-erosive)
5. Serositis (pleuritis/pericarditis)
6. Renal involvement (lupus nephritis)
7. Neurologic (seizures, psychosis)
8. Hematologic (hemolytic anemia, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia)
9. Immunologic (anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm)
10. Antinuclear antibodies (ANA)
11. Other organ involvement

** Key Features :**
- ** Libman-Sacks endocarditis**: Non-infective endocarditis
- ** Most common cause of death**: Lupus nephropathy → ESRD
- UV light triggers flares

** Pregnancy in SLE**: Anti-Ro/SSA antibodies → neonatal lupus + heart block; treat with LMWH

** Treatment**: NSAIDs, corticosteroids, hydroxychloroquine, cyclophosphamide (for renal), belimumab (B-cell inhibitor)

---

### Drug-Induced Lupus

** Causes**: Hydralazine, isoniazid, procainamide, quinidine

** Features**: Fever, fatigue, arthritis, pleurisy; **NO skin or kidney involvement **

** Diagnosis**: Anti-histone antibodies (sensitive)

** Treatment**: Stop offending drug (symptoms resolve in 1-2 weeks)

---

### Scleroderma (Systemic Sclerosis)

** Features **:
- ** GIT**: Esophageal dysmotility, malabsorption
- ** Lung**: Pulmonary fibrosis (leading cause of death), cor pulmonale
- ** Renal**: Malignant HTN, acute renal failure → treat with **ACE inhibitors **
- ** Skin**: Thickening, sclerodactyly

** CREST Syndrome** (Limited scleroderma) :
- ** C**alcinosis
- ** R**aynaud's phenomenon
- ** E**sophageal dysfunction
- ** S**clerodactyly
- ** T**elangiectasias

** Treatment**: Penicillamine, CCBs (nifedipine), ACE inhibitors

---

### Sjögren's Syndrome

** Features **:
- Dry eyes (keratoconjunctivitis sicca)
- Dry mouth (xerostomia)
- Parotid enlargement

** Diagnosis **:
- Schirmer test (↓ tear production)
- Anti-Ro/SSA, Anti-La/SSB
- Salivary gland biopsy: Lymphocytic infiltrate

** Treatment**: Pilocarpine, cevimeline (↑ Ach → ↑ secretions)

---

### Seronegative Spondyloarthropathies

** Characteristics **:
- RF negative, ANA negative
- HLA-B27 positive
- Axial involvement (sacroiliitis, spine)

#### Ankylosing Spondylitis
- Young men (20-30 years)
- Lower back pain, morning stiffness (>1 hour), improves with exercise
- ** Anterior uveitis, aortic insufficiency** (3rd heart block)
- ** Diagnosis**: X-ray → sacroiliitis, bamboo spine; HLA-B27
- ** Treatment**: NSAIDs, physical therapy, TNF blockers

#### Reactive Arthritis (Reiter's Syndrome)
- ** Type 1**: After non-gonococcal urethritis (Chlamydia)
- ** Type 2**: After gastroenteritis (Campylobacter, Shigella, Salmonella)
- ** Features**: Arthritis, urethritis, conjunctivitis (can't see, can't pee, can't climb a tree)
- ** Treatment**: NSAIDs, tetracycline (3 weeks)

#### Psoriatic Arthritis
- Involves **DIP** joints
- Nail pitting, sausage digits
- Peripheral arthritis

#### Enteropathic Arthropathy
- Associated with IBD (UC > Crohn's)
- Pyoderma gangrenosum, erythema nodosum

---

### Osteoarthritis (الفصال العظمي)

** Risk Factors**: Age, female, obesity, joint trauma

** Features **:
- Pain with activity, relieved by rest
- Morning stiffness <20-30 minutes
- Crepitus, NO systemic symptoms
- ** Heberden's nodes**: DIP
- ** Bouchard's nodes**: PIP

** Diagnosis**: X-ray → osteophytes, narrowed joint space

** Treatment**: Acetaminophen, NSAIDs, weight loss, physical therapy, joint replacement

---

### Gout (النقرس)

** Features **:
- ** Podagra**: MTP joint of great toe (most common)
- Sudden onset, severe pain, redness, warmth
- Precipitated by: Alcohol, red meat, trauma, diuretics, thiazides

** Diagnosis **:
- ** Gold standard**: Synovial fluid → needle-shaped, **negative birefringent** crystals
- WBC: 5,000-50,000
- Serum uric acid may be normal during acute attack !

** Treatment :**
- ** Acute**: NSAIDs, colchicine (does NOT affect uric acid), steroids
- ** Chronic**: Allopurinol (for overproducers, renal failure), febuxostat, probenecid (for undersecretors)

---

### Temporal Arteritis (Giant Cell Arteritis)

** Key**: New onset headache in patient **>50 years **
- If untreated → **permanent vision loss **
- ** Features**: Headache, scalp tenderness, jaw claudication, visual changes, PMR
- ** Diagnosis**: **↑ ESR** (initial), temporal artery biopsy
- ** Treatment**: **Start corticosteroids immediately** (don't wait for biopsy!)

---

### Polymyositis/Dermatomyositis

** Features **:
- ** Proximal muscle weakness** (difficulty rising from chair, combing hair)
- ** Ocular muscles NEVER affected** (differentiates from MG)
- ** Dermatomyositis**: Heliotrope rash (purple eyelids), Gottron's papules (knuckles)

** Diagnosis **:
- ↑ CPK, aldolase
- Anti-Jo-1 antibodies
- EMG: Short duration, low amplitude
- Muscle biopsy: Confirms

** Treatment **:
- Polymyositis/Dermatomyositis: **Steroids **
- Inclusion body myositis: Immunosuppression (poor response to steroids)

---

### Erythema Nodosum

** Causes**: Strep infection, sarcoidosis, IBD, TB, syphilis, pregnancy, OCP, sulfonamides

** Features**: Painful red nodules on anterior shins; do NOT ulcerate; last ~6 weeks

** Diagnosis**: ASLO (if recent strep)

** Treatment**: Analgesics, NSAIDs, potassium iodide

---

## Chapter 3: Gastroenterology (أمراض الجهاز الهضمي)

### Esophageal Diseases

#### Achalasia (تعذر الارتخاء المريئي)

** Features **:
- Dysphagia to **both solids and liquids** (simultaneously)
- Regurgitation, weight loss
- ** No** relationship with alcohol/tobacco

** Diagnosis **:
- X-ray: Air-fluid level
- Barium: **Bird's beak sign **
- ** Esophageal manometry**: Most accurate

** Treatment **:
1. Pneumatic dilation (risk: perforation)
2. Botulinum toxin injection (needs reinjection)
3. Surgical myotomy (risk: reflux)

---

#### Esophageal Cancer

| Type | Location | Risk Factors |
|------|----------|--------------|
| ** Squamous cell** | Proximal 2/3 | Alcohol, tobacco |
| ** Adenocarcinoma** | Distal 1/3 | GERD, Barrett's esophagus |

** Features**: Dysphagia (solids then liquids), weight loss, halitosis, hoarseness, hypercalcemia

** Diagnosis**: Barium swallow (initial), CT (local spread), **Endoscopy with biopsy** (definitive)

** Treatment**: Surgery (localized), chemotherapy + radiation

---

#### Mallory-Weiss Syndrome

** Features **:
- ** Non-transmural tear** of distal esophagus from retching/vomiting
- Painless upper GI bleeding, hematemesis, melena
- ** No dysphagia or odynophagia **

** Diagnosis**: Upper endoscopy

** Treatment**: Spontaneous resolution, injection with epinephrine, cauterization

** Boerhaave Syndrome**: Full-thickness perforation; most common in alcoholics; emergency surgery

---

#### Esophagitis

** Infectious Causes**: Candida (most common in HIV), HSV, CMV

** Pill-induced**: Iron, alendronate, KCl, NSAIDs, doxycycline

** Features**: Odynophagia (painful swallowing)

** Treatment **:
- HIV: Trial fluconazole first; if no response → endoscopy
- Pill esophagitis: Drink plenty of water

---

#### Zenker's Diverticulum

** Features**: Bad breath, difficulty initiating swallowing, regurgitated food on pillow

** Diagnosis**: Barium swallow

** Treatment**: Surgical resection (**No NG tube or endoscopy**)

---

### Gastric Disorders

#### Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)

** Risk Factors**: Nicotine, alcohol, caffeine, chocolate, anticholinergics, CCBs, nitrates

** Features**: Heartburn, regurgitation, cough, hoarseness, metallic taste

** Diagnosis**: Clinical; **24-hour pH monitoring** (most accurate)

** Treatment **:
- Lifestyle modifications (elevate head of bed, avoid triggers)
- ** PPIs** (omeprazole, esomeprazole, pantoprazole)
- Nissen fundoplication
- H2 blockers (mild symptoms)

---

#### Barrett's Esophagus

** Definition**: Squamous epithelium → columnar epithelium (metaplasia) from chronic GERD

** Surveillance **:
- Barrett's: Endoscopy every 2-3 years
- Low-grade dysplasia: Endoscopy 3-6 months
- High-grade dysplasia: Esophagectomy or endoscopic resection

** Treatment**: PPIs

---

#### Peptic Ulcer Disease (مرض القرحة الهضمية)

** Causes **:
1. ** H. pylori** (most common)
2. ** NSAIDs** (decrease mucous barrier)
3. Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, gastric cancer, Crohn's, stress ulcers (burns, head trauma)

** Features **:
- ** Gastric ulcer**: Pain **with eating** (worse after meals)
- ** Duodenal ulcer**: Pain **relieved by eating** (pain on empty stomach, night pain)

** Diagnosis of H. pylori **:
- ** Serology**: Least expensive, least invasive
- ** Urea breath test**: Distinguishes old vs new infection
- ** Stool antigen**: Same as breath test
- ** Biopsy with histology/CLO test**: Most sensitive and specific

** Treatment**: Triple therapy (PPI + amoxicillin + clarithromycin) OR quadruple therapy

** Surgery Indications **:
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Perforation
- Refractory ulcers
- Gastric outlet obstruction

---

#### Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome

** Definition**: Gastrin-secreting tumor (gastrinoma)

** Location**: 50% duodenum, 25% pancreas, associated with MEN I

** Features **:
- Recurrent, multiple ulcers
- Diarrhea, steatorrhea (lipase inactivated by acid)

** Diagnosis **:
- **↑ Gastrin** (must rule out: pernicious anemia, chronic gastritis, renal failure, hyperthyroidism)
- Secretin stimulation test

** Treatment**: Surgery (localized), PPIs (metastatic)

---

#### Gastritis

** Types **:
- ** Type A**: Autoimmune → atrophy, achlorhydria, pernicious anemia (↓ B12)
- ** Type B**: H. pylori → increased acid production (most common)

** Treatment**: Treat underlying cause; B12 replacement for Type A

---

### Diarrhea

** Causes**: Infection, antibiotics, lactose intolerance, IBS, carcinoid

** Infectious Diarrhea **:

| Organism | Key Features |
|----------|--------------|
| ** Campylobacter** | Most common bacterial gastroenteritis, Guillain-Barré syndrome |
| ** E. coli O157:H7** | Contaminated hamburger, **Shiga toxin**, **HUS** → **DO NOT give antibiotics !* * |
| ** Giardia** | Unfiltered water, bloating, gas, **NO blood**, malabsorption |
| ** Salmonella** | Chicken/eggs/dairy |
| ** Shigella/Yersinia** | Mimics appendicitis; Yersinia in iron overload |
| ** Scombroid** | Contaminated fish (tuna, mackerel), histamine release → flushing, wheezing |
| ** Vibrio vulnificus** | Raw shellfish, liver disease, iron overload, **skin bullae ** |
| ** C. difficile** | Antibiotic-associated (clindamycin, fluoroquinolones, cephalosporins) |

** Treatment **:
- Hydration
- ** Blood in stool** + fever + abdominal pain → Antibiotics (ciprofloxacin + metronidazole)
- Giardia: Metronidazole or tinidazole

---

#### Clostridium difficile (Pseudomembranous Colitis)

** Etiology**: Antibiotics → ↓ normal flora → C. difficile overgrowth → toxins A & B

** Most common antibiotics**: Clindamycin, fluoroquinolones, cephalosporins

** Diagnosis**: ELISA for toxins A/B or PCR

** Treatment **:
- ** Oral metronidazole**: Drug of choice
- ** IV metronidazole**: If cannot take oral
- ** Oral vancomycin**: If no response to metronidazole

---

#### Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

** Rome Criteria** (≥3 months) :
1. Pain relieved by defecation or change in bowel habit
2. Fewer symptoms at night
3. Diarrhea alternating with constipation
4. ** No constitutional symptoms** (fever, weight loss, anemia)

** Treatment**: High-fiber diet, antidiarrheals (loperamide), antispasmodics, TCAs, SSRIs

---

#### Carcinoid Syndrome

** Features **:
- Diarrhea, flushing, tachycardia, hypotension
- Endocardial fibrosis → tricuspid insufficiency, pulmonic stenosis

** Diagnosis**: **Urinary 5-HIAA** (5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid)

** Treatment**: Octreotide (somatostatin analog), surgery

---

### Malabsorption Syndromes

** Causes **:
1. ** Celiac disease** (most common)
2. Chronic pancreatitis
3. Tropical sprue
4. Whipple's disease

** Features **:
- Steatorrhea (greasy, foul-smelling stools)
- Weight loss
- Fat-soluble vitamin deficiency (A, D, E, K)
- ** Dermatitis herpetiformis**: Vesicular rash on extensor surfaces (celiac disease)

** Diagnosis **:
- ** Celiac**: Anti-tissue transglutaminase, anti-endomysial antibodies; small bowel biopsy → villous atrophy
- ** Chronic pancreatitis**: Secretin test (most accurate), calcifications on CT
- ** Tropical sprue**: History of travel to tropics
- ** Whipple's disease**: PAS-positive macrophages, Tropheryma whippelii PCR

** Treatment **:
- Celiac: Gluten-free diet
- Chronic pancreatitis: Enzyme replacement
- Tropical sprue: TMP/SMX or doxycycline (6 months)
- Whipple's: TMP/SMX or doxycycline (1 year)

---

### Diverticular Disease

** Diverticulosis**: Outpocketings (most common in sigmoid colon)
- ** Causes**: Low-fiber diet → ↑ intracolonic pressure
- ** Features**: Left lower quadrant pain, bleeding (more common from right-sided diverticula)
- ** Treatment**: High-fiber diet

** Diverticulitis**: Infection of diverticulum
- ** Features**: Fever, LLQ tenderness, ↑ WBC
- ** Diagnosis**: CT scan (barium/endoscopy contraindicated)
- ** Treatment**: Antibiotics (ampicillin/sulbactam, piperacillin/tazobactam)

---

### Constipation

** Causes **:
1. Low fiber, insufficient fluids (most common)
2. Drugs: CCBs, iron, opiates, anticholinergics, TCAs
3. Hypothyroidism, hypercalcemia

** Treatment**: Stop offending drugs, hydration, bulking agents

---

### Gastrointestinal Bleeding

** Upper GI Bleeding** (Proximal to ligament of Treitz)
- ** Most common causes**: Duodenal ulcer (29%), gastric ulcer (16%), esophageal varices, gastritis, Mallory-Weiss
- ** Features**: Hematemesis, melena, orthostasis
- ** Diagnosis**: EGD (most accurate)

** Lower GI Bleeding **
- ** Most common causes**: Diverticulosis, angiodysplasia, hemorrhoids, cancer, IBD
- ** Features**: Hematochezia (red blood in stool)

** Treatment **:
- IV fluids (first line!)
- Maintain Hct >30% (elderly) or >20% (young)
- Platelets if <50,000
- ** PPIs** for ulcer/gastritis
- ** Octreotide** for varices; if fails → bands → TIPS
- ** Propranolol** for portal hypertension

**80% of GI bleeding stops spontaneously with resuscitation **

---

### Acute Pancreatitis (التهاب البنكرياس الحاد)

** Causes**: Alcohol, gallstones (most common)

** Features **:
- Midepigastric pain radiating to back
- Nausea, vomiting, fever, hypotension
- Cullen's sign (periumbilical), Turner's sign (flank)
- ARDS, pleural effusion, ascites

** Diagnosis **:
- ↑ Amylase, **↑ Lipase** (more specific)
- Hypocalcemia, hyperglycemia
- ** CT scan** (most accurate)
- ** Ranson's Criteria **

** Ranson's Criteria :**

| At Admission | At 48 Hours |
|--------------|-------------|
| Age >55 | Ca <8 |
| WBC >16,000 | Hct drop >10% |
| Glucose >200 | PO₂ <60 |
| AST >250 | BUN ↑ >5 |
| LDH >350 | Base deficit >4 mEq/L |
| | Fluid sequestration >6L |

| Criteria Met | Mortality |
|--------------|-----------|
| 0-2 | <2% |
| 3-4 | 15% |
| 5-6 | 40% |
| 7-8 | 100% |

** Treatment **:
- Supportive: IV fluids, bowel rest, pain control
- ERCP for stones/strictures
- Antibiotics (imipenem/meropenem) if >30% necrosis
- Surgical debridement for infected necrosis

** Complications **:
- ** Pseudocyst**: 2-4 weeks after; drain if pain, fistula, rupture, or expanding
- ** Abscess**: 4-6 weeks after

---

### Liver Cirrhosis (تليف الكبد)

** Causes **:
1. Alcohol (Western), Hepatitis B (Jordan)
2. Hepatitis C, Hemochromatosis, Wilson's disease
3. Primary biliary cirrhosis, Primary sclerosing cholangitis
4. Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency

** Features **:
- Portal hypertension: Ascites, esophageal varices, splenomegaly
- ↑ Estrogen: Palmar erythema, spider angiomata, gynecomastia
- Jaundice, asterixis, encephalopathy

** Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP) **
- ** Diagnosis**: Ascitic fluid WBC >500 or PMN >250 → start antibiotics (cefotaxime, ceftriaxone)
- ** SAAG **:
- >1.1: Portal hypertension (cirrhosis)
- <1.1: Cancer, infection

** Treatment**: Manage complications
- Ascites: Spironolactone
- Varices: Propranolol (primary prophylaxis)
- Encephalopathy: Neomycin + Lactulose

---

#### Primary Biliary Cirrhosis

- Middle-aged females
- ** Features**: Fatigue, pruritus
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ ALP, ↑ IgM, **Antimitochondrial antibodies** (specific)
- ** Treatment**: Ursodeoxycholic acid, cholestyramine, liver transplant

---

#### Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis

- Associated with **Ulcerative Colitis **
- ** Diagnosis**: Same as PBC **EXCEPT**: Anti-mitochondrial Ab is **negative**, **ERCP** is most specific
- ** Treatment**: Same as PBC

---

#### Hemochromatosis (داء ترسب الأصبغة الدموية)

** Features **:
- ** Cirrhosis, HCC **
- Restrictive cardiomyopathy
- DM (bronze diabetes)
- Hypogonadism, arthralgia
- Skin hyperpigmentation

** Diagnosis **:
- ↑ Iron, ↑ Ferritin
- ↓ TIBC
- ** C282Y gene**, liver biopsy (most accurate)

** Treatment**: Phlebotomy, deferoxamine, deferasirox

---

#### Wilson's Disease (داء ويلسون)

** Autosomal recessive** → copper accumulation

** Features **:
- ** Brain**: Movement disorders, psychiatric disturbance
- ** Eye**: Kayser-Fleischer rings
- ** Kidney**: Fanconi syndrome (type 2 RTA)
- ** Liver**: Cirrhosis

** Diagnosis **:
- ↓ Ceruloplasmin
- ↑ Urinary copper
- ** Liver biopsy**: Most accurate

** Treatment**: Penicillamine, trientine, zinc, liver transplant

---

#### Chronic Hepatitis B & C

| Feature | Hepatitis B | Hepatitis C |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Transmission | Blood, sexual, perinatal | Blood (most common) |
| Chronicity | 5-10% | 50-80% |
| Diagnosis | HBsAg >6 months | HCV Ab, PCR |
| Treatment | Interferon, entecavir, lamivudine, adefovir | Sofosbuvir/ledipasvir, simeprevir |

** Needle stick from HBV-positive patient**: HBIG + HBV vaccine

---

## Chapter 4: Cardiology (أمراض القلب)

### Approach to Chest Pain

** Key Features **:
- ** Stable angina/ACS**: Tightness, pressure, heaviness
- ** Inferior MI**: Nausea, vomiting, bradycardia, hypotension (vagal reflex)
- ** Sharp/knife-like pain**: Musculoskeletal
- ** MI pain**: >20-30 minutes
- ** Pain not responding to NTG**: GERD
- ** Women/elderly**: Atypical symptoms (dyspnea, SOB)

---

### Cardiac Biomarkers

| Marker | Appearance | Peak | Duration |
|--------|------------|------|----------|
| ** Troponin** | 4-6 hours | 12-24 hours | 14-21 days |
| ** CK-MB** | 4-6 hours | 12-24 hours | 2-3 days |
| ** Myoglobin** | 1-4 hours | - | - |
| ** AST** | 6-12 hours | 20-30 hours | 2-6 days |
| ** LDH** | 8-16 hours | 30-48 hours | 5-14 days |

** Troponin**: Gold standard, elevated in MI, HF, PE, renal failure, sepsis

---

### Ischemic Heart Disease

** Risk Factors :**
- ** Modifiable**: High cholesterol (↓ HDL, ↑ LDL), smoking, HTN, DM, obesity, sedentary
- ** Uncontrolled**: Age (>65), male, family history

** Stable Angina **
- ** Features**: Substernal pressure 5-15 min, exertion, relieved by rest/NTG
- ** Diagnosis **:
- ECG: ST depression during attack
- ** Exercise stress test**: Positive if ST depression >2mm or hypotension >10mmHg
- ** Contraindications**: Acute MI, unstable angina, severe CHF, aortic stenosis, HOCM, uncontrolled HTN
- ** Treatment**: NTG, beta-blockers, aspirin, statins
- ** Lipid targets**: LDL <100, HDL >40 (men)/50 (women), TG <150

** Statin Side Effects**: Myalgia (most common), ↑ AST/ALT, ↑ CPK, rhabdomyolysis

---

### Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)

** Types **:
- ** Unstable Angina**: No ST elevation, negative enzymes
- ** NSTEMI**: No ST elevation, positive enzymes
- ** STEMI**: ST elevation, positive enzymes

#### STEMI

** Features **:
- Substernal pressure >20 minutes
- Radiates to neck, jaw, shoulder, arm
- Nausea, vomiting, diaphoresis, SOB
- ** Elderly/female**: Atypical (nausea, dyspnea)

** ECG Changes **:

| Area | ECG | Artery |
|------|-----|--------|
| Inferior | II, III, aVF | Right coronary |
| Anterior | V2-V4 | LAD |
| Anteroseptal | V1-V3 | LAD |
| Lateral | I, aVL, V5-V6 | LAD or Circumflex |
| Posterior | ST depression V1-V3 | Posterior descending |

** ECG Evolution**: Hyperacute T waves → ST elevation → Q waves → T wave inversion

** Treatment: MONA **:
- ** M**orphine
- ** O**xygen
- ** N**itroglycerin
- ** A**spirin

** Reperfusion**: PCI (90 min) > Thrombolysis (180 min)
- ** Thrombolytics**: Streptokinase, alteplase, reteplase, tenecteplase
- ** Contraindications**: Intracranial hemorrhage, aortic dissection, recent head trauma/surgery, bleeding diathesis

** Discharge Medications **:
- Aspirin + Clopidogrel (dual antiplatelet)
- Beta-blocker
- ACE inhibitor (if HF)
- Statin
- Warfarin (if AF or mural thrombus)

---

#### MI Complications

1. ** Arrhythmias**: V-fib (most common cause of death), V-tach, bradycardia
2. ** Conduction blocks**: AV block, bundle branch block
3. ** Pump failure**: HF, MR, VSD
4. ** Pericarditis**: Dressler's syndrome → aspirin, NSAIDs, steroids
5. ** Thromboembolism**: Mural thrombus, DVT
6. ** Right ventricular infarction**: Give fluids

---

### Congestive Heart Failure

** Most common cause of left-sided HF**: IHD
** Most common cause of right-sided HF**: Left-sided HF

** Compensatory Mechanisms **:
- Cardiac: Frank-Starling, tachycardia, dilatation
- Neuronal: ↑ sympathetic activity
- Hormonal: RAAS activation, vasopressin, BNP

** Systolic HF**: EF <45% (loss of contractility)
** Diastolic HF**: EF normal (loss of relaxation)

** Symptoms **:
- ** Left-sided**: Dyspnea, orthopnea, PND, fatigue
- ** Right-sided**: Peripheral edema, JVD, ascites, hepatomegaly

** NYHA Classification **:
- Class I: No limitation
- Class II: Mild limitation
- Class III: Marked limitation
- Class IV: Confined to bed/chair

** Diagnosis**: Echo (best), BNP (for decompensation)

** Treatment (Systolic HF) **:
1. Diuretics + ACE inhibitors/ARBs
2. Beta-blockers (carvedilol, metoprolol)
3. Spironolactone
4. Digoxin, inotropes (dobutamine, dopamine)
5. Biventricular pacing, ICD, transplant

** Medications that reduce mortality**: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, Beta-blockers, Spironolactone, AICD

** Diastolic HF**: Treat underlying cause, avoid diuretics/vasodilators, use BB/CCB

---

#### Pulmonary Edema

** Features **:
- Pink frothy sputum, nocturnal dyspnea, crackles, wheezes
- CXR: Kerley B lines, prominent pulmonary vessels, enlarged cardiac silhouette

** Treatment **:
1. O₂
2. Furosemide
3. Morphine
4. Upright position
5. NTG, Digoxin, IV ACE inhibitors

---

### Valvular Heart Disease

#### Mitral Stenosis

** Causes**: Rheumatic fever (most common)

** Features **:
- Dyspnea, orthopnea, PND, fatigue, hemoptysis
- ** Hoarseness**: Enlarged left atrium compresses recurrent laryngeal nerve
- ** Malar flush, systemic embolism **

** Signs **:
- Loud S1, **Opening snap **
- Mid-diastolic rumbling murmur (mitral area)
- RVH, LAH

** Diagnosis**: Echo (thickening, LA enlargement), ECG (AF, RVH)

** Treatment**: Diuretics, anticoagulation (if AF), balloon valvulotomy, commissurotomy, valve replacement

---

#### Mitral Regurgitation

** Causes**: Ischemia (most common), LV dilation, rheumatic fever

** Signs **:
- Hyperdynamic displaced LV impulse (5th-6th ICS)
- ** Holosystolic murmur** (mitral area → axilla)
- S₃, soft S₁

** Diagnosis**: Echo (MVP, LV enlargement), catheterization (most accurate)

** Treatment**: ACE inhibitors, diuretics, digoxin; valve replacement if medical fails

---

#### Mitral Valve Prolapse

** Features**: Young women, palpitations, syncope, chest pain

** Signs**: Mid-to-late click, late systolic murmur; ↑ with Valsalva/standing, ↓ with squatting

** Treatment**: Beta-blockers (for chest pain), valve replacement

---

#### Aortic Stenosis

** Causes**: Calcific (aging), bicuspid valve, rheumatic fever

** Features **:
- ** Angina, syncope, dyspnea (CHF) **
- Pulsus tardus et parvus (slow, weak pulse)

** Signs **:
- Harsh systolic murmur (aortic area → carotids)
- S₄, **Paradoxically split S₂ **

** Diagnosis**: Echo (thick valves)

** Severe AS Criteria **:
- Valve area <1 cm²
- Mean gradient >40 mmHg
- Jet velocity >4 m/s

** Treatment**: **Valve replacement** (area <0.8 cm²), balloon valvuloplasty

---

#### Aortic Regurgitation

** Causes**: HTN, infective endocarditis, syphilis, ankylosing spondylitis, rheumatic fever, aortic dissection

** Features **:
- Dyspnea (most common)
- ** Wide pulse pressure **
- ** Corrigan's pulse**: Water-hammer pulse
- ** De Musset's sign**: Head bobbing
- ** Quincke's sign**: Capillary pulsations
- ** Hill's sign**: Popliteal BP exceeds brachial BP by ≥60 mmHg

** Signs **:
- ** Diastolic decrescendo murmur** (early)
- Austin Flint murmur (functional MS)
- Systolic flow murmur

** Diagnosis**: Echo (dilated LV and aorta)

** Treatment**: ACE inhibitors, diuretics; valve replacement if worsening symptoms or ↓ EF

---

### Cardiac Murmurs

| Systolic | Diastolic |
|----------|-----------|
| Mid-systolic: AS, PS, ASD, HOCM | Early diastolic: AR, PR, Austin Flint |
| Holosystolic: MR, TR, VSD | Mid/late diastolic: MS, TS, Graham-Steel |
| Late systolic: MVP | Continuous (machinery): PDA, AV fistula |

---

### Cardiomyopathies

#### Dilated Cardiomyopathy (Most common for transplant)

** Causes**: Idiopathic (most common), alcohol, peripartum, post-myocarditis, doxorubicin, toxins, metabolic

** Features**: Biventricular HF

** Diagnosis**: Echo (dilated ventricles, low EF)

** Treatment**: As systolic HF

---

#### Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy (HOCM)

** Autosomal dominant** (chromosome 14)

** Features **:
- Dyspnea, angina, syncope, palpitations
- ** Sudden death** (most common in young athletes)
- Large J wave, S₄, systolic murmur (↑ with Valsalva/standing, ↓ with squatting/handgrip)
- ** Systolic anterior motion (SAM)** of mitral valve

** Diagnosis**: Echo (septal hypertrophy, EF 80-90%)

** Treatment**: Beta-blockers, CCBs, disopyramide, ICD, septal myectomy

---

#### Restrictive Cardiomyopathy

** Causes**: Amyloidosis, sarcoidosis, hemochromatosis, scleroderma, radiation

** Features **:
- Biventricular HF
- ** Kussmaul's sign**: JVP rises with inspiration
- Low voltage ECG

** Diagnosis**: Echo (thickened walls), catheterization (square root sign)

** Treatment**: Treat underlying cause (hemochromatosis reversible); heart transplant

---

### Pericardial Diseases

#### Acute Pericarditis

** Causes**: Idiopathic (viral), SLE, uremia, neoplasm, trauma

** Features **:
- Chest pain worsened by lying down, coughing, inspiration
- Relieved by sitting up and leaning forward
- ** Pericardial friction rub** (diagnostic): 3 components (atrial systole, ventricular systole, early diastole)

** ECG**: Diffuse ST elevation, PR depression, upright T waves

** Treatment**: NSAIDs, colchicine (↓ recurrence)

---

#### Cardiac Tamponade (Emergency!)

** Features **:
- Dyspnea, fatigue, orthopnea
- ** Pulsus paradoxus**: ↓ systolic BP ≥10 mmHg with inspiration
- ** Beck's triad**: Low BP, distended neck veins, **muffled heart sounds **
- Neck vein distension with **clear lungs **

** Diagnosis**: Echo, equal pressures in all chambers

** Treatment**: **Pericardiocentesis**, subxiphoid drainage

---

### Arrhythmias

#### Sinus Bradycardia

** Causes**: MI (inferior), vagal stimulation, drugs (BB, CCB), hypothyroidism, athletes

** Treatment**: Atropine (acute), pacemaker (if symptomatic)

---

#### AV Blocks

**1 st Degree**: PR >0.20s → No treatment

**2 nd Degree **:
- ** Mobitz I (Wenckebach)**: Progressive PR prolongation, then dropped beat → Benign, no treatment
- ** Mobitz II**: Fixed PR interval, dropped beats → Requires pacemaker

**3 rd Degree (Complete Heart Block) **:
- Atria and ventricles beat independently
- ** Causes**: Fibrosis (most common), inferior MI, AS, ankylosing spondylitis
- ** Features**: Adams-Stokes attacks, CHF
- ** Treatment**: Pacemaker

---

#### Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)

** Features**: Sudden onset/termination, regular, rate 130-220

** Treatment **:
1. Carotid sinus massage (vagal maneuvers)
2. ** A**denosine (half-life 6 seconds)
3. ** B**eta-blockers
4. ** C**alcium channel blockers
5. ** D**igitalis
6. Unstable → Synchronized cardioversion

---

#### Atrial Fibrillation

** Causes**: Rheumatic mitral disease, CAD, CHF, HTN, hyperthyroidism, alcohol, hypoxemia

** ECG**: Irregularly irregular, no P waves, fibrillatory waves

** Treatment **:
- ** Rate control**: BB, CCB, digoxin (target HR <100-110)
- ** Rhythm control**: Cardioversion (unstable), amiodarone, flecainide
- ** Anticoagulation**: Based on CHA₂DS₂-VASc score

** CHA₂DS₂-VASc Score **:
| C | CHF | 1 |
| H | HTN | 1 |
| A₂ | Age ≥75 | 2 |
| D | DM | 1 |
| S₂ | Stroke/TIA | 2 |
| V | Vascular disease | 1 |
| A | Age 65-74 | 1 |
| Sc | Female sex | 1 |

- Score 0: No treatment
- Score 1: Aspirin or anticoagulant
- Score ≥2: **Anticoagulation** (warfarin, dabigatran, rivaroxaban)

#### Wolff-Parkinson-White (WPW) Syndrome

** ECG**: Short PR, **Delta wave**, wide QRS

** Treatment **:
- Stable: **Procainamide **
- Unstable: Cardioversion
- ** Avoid**: BB, CCB, Digoxin (can cause VF!)
- Definitive: Ablation

## Chapter 5: Hematology (أمراض الدم)

### Anemia (فقر الدم)

** Definition**: Hct <41% (men) or <36% (women); Hb <13.5 (men) or <12 (women)

** Classification by MCV :**

| Type | MCV | Causes |
|------|-----|--------|
| ** Microcytic** | <80 | Iron deficiency, thalassemia, sideroblastic, lead poisoning, anemia of chronic disease |
| ** Macrocytic** | >100 | B12 deficiency, folate deficiency, alcohol, liver disease, drugs |
| ** Normocytic** | 80-100 | Hemolytic anemia, acute blood loss, anemia of chronic disease |

---

### Iron Deficiency Anemia

** Causes **:
- Blood loss (most common): GI, menstruation
- Increased demand: Pregnancy, puberty
- Malabsorption, poor intake

** Daily requirements**: Men 1 mg, Women 2-3 mg, Pregnancy 5-6 mg

** Features **:
- ** Brittle nails, spoon nails (koilonychia) **
- Glossitis, pica

** Diagnosis **:
- ↓ Serum ferritin, ↓ MCV, ↓ serum iron
- **↑ TIBC** (key difference from anemia of chronic disease)
- ↑ RDW, ↑ Platelets, ↓ Reticulocytes

** Treatment**: Ferrous sulfate (until Hb normal + 2-3 months for stores); IV iron if malabsorption

---

### Anemia of Chronic Disease

** Pathophysiology**: Hepcidin ↑ → iron trapped in macrophages

** Diagnosis **:
- Normal or ↑ Ferritin
- ↓ Serum iron, ↓ TIBC
- ↓ Reticulocytes

** Treatment**: Correct underlying disease, erythropoietin (for renal failure)

---

### Sideroblastic Anemia

** Defect**: Hemoglobin synthesis → iron trapped in mitochondria → ringed sideroblasts

** Causes**: Alcohol, isoniazid, lead poisoning, myelodysplasia

** Diagnosis **:
- ↑ Serum iron, ↑ Ferritin, ↓ TIBC
- ** Prussian blue stain**: Ringed sideroblasts

** Treatment**: Stop offending drugs, pyridoxine (B6)

---

### Thalassemia

| Type | Inheritance | Features |
|------|-------------|----------|
| ** Alpha** | Chromosome 16 | 1 gene deleted: Normal; 2 genes: Mild anemia; 3 genes: Severe anemia; 4 genes: Death in utero |
| ** Beta** | Chromosome 11 | **Cooley's anemia**: Starts at 6 months, growth failure, hepatosplenomegaly, bony deformities |

** Diagnosis**: Microcytic anemia, **normal iron studies**, hemoglobin electrophoresis, **normal RDW **

** Treatment**: Transfusions, deferasirox/deferoxamine (iron chelation), splenectomy, bone marrow transplant

---

### Macrocytic Anemias

#### Vitamin B12 Deficiency

** Causes **:
1. ** Pernicious anemia** (↓ intrinsic factor)
2. Gastrectomy, malabsorption, pancreatic insufficiency
3. Fish tapeworm (Diphyllobothrium latum)

** Features **:
- Hematologic: Macrocytic anemia, pancytopenia, hypersegmented neutrophils
- ** Neurologic**: Peripheral neuropathy, **subacute combined degeneration** (loss of vibration/proprioception, spasticity, ataxia)
- Glossitis, diarrhea

** Diagnosis **:
- ↓ B12 level
- ↑ Methylmalonic acid, ↑ homocysteine
- Anti-intrinsic factor/anti-parietal cell antibodies

** Treatment**: B12 replacement (oral if adequate absorption, IM if not)

---

#### Folate Deficiency

** Causes **:
- ↓ Dietary intake, pregnancy, alcoholism
- ** Antifolate drugs**: Phenytoin, valproic acid, methotrexate, trimethoprim

** Features**: Hematologic only (NO neurologic symptoms)

** Treatment**: Oral folic acid

---

### Hemolytic Anemias

** General Features **:
- Normal MCV, ↑ Reticulocytes
- ↑ LDH, ↑ Indirect bilirubin
- ↓ Haptoglobin

#### Sickle Cell Disease

** Pathophysiology**: Valine substituted for glutamic acid at position 6 of β-globin → HbS

** Features **:
- ** Painful crises**: Bone, chest, abdomen
- ** Acute chest syndrome**: Chest pain, fever, infiltrates
- Autosplenectomy, aseptic necrosis, stroke, priapism
- ** Sickle cell trait**: Asymptomatic, isosthenuria, hematuria

** Diagnosis**: Hemoglobin electrophoresis (most specific), Sickledex (screening)

** Treatment **:
- Acute crisis: Fluids, analgesics, O₂, antibiotics (ceftriaxone), exchange transfusion
- Chronic: Folic acid, vaccinations (pneumococcus, influenza), hydroxyurea, BMT

---

#### G6PD Deficiency

** X-linked recessive **

** Triggered by**: Infection, fava beans, drugs (sulfonamides, primaquine, dapsone, nitrofurantoin)

** Features**: Acute hemolysis, Heinz bodies, bite cells

** Treatment**: Hydration, transfusion, avoid triggers

---

#### Hereditary Spherocytosis

** Autosomal dominant** → ↓ Spectrin

** Features**: Spherocytes, **↑ MCHC**, negative Coombs test

** Diagnosis**: Osmotic fragility test

** Treatment**: Splenectomy (if severe), folate

---

#### Autoimmune Hemolytic Anemia

- ** Warm antibody**: IgG, associated with SLE, lymphoma, CLL, drugs (methyldopa)
- ** Cold agglutinin**: IgM, associated with Mycoplasma, EBV

** Diagnosis**: **Positive Coombs test **

** Treatment**: Steroids → splenectomy, rituximab; **steroids NOT effective in cold agglutinin **

---

### Aplastic Anemia

** Pancytopenia** (RBCs, WBCs, platelets)

** Causes**: Idiopathic (most common), drugs (NSAIDs, chloramphenicol, chemotherapy), radiation, benzene, infections (hepatitis, HIV, parvovirus B19)

** Diagnosis**: Pancytopenia, bone marrow biopsy → **empty/hypoplastic, fat-filled **

** Treatment**: BMT; ATG + cyclosporine if BMT not possible

---

### Acute Leukemia

** AML Markers**: Auer rods, myeloperoxidase, esterase
** ALL Markers**: CALLA, TdT

** M3 (APL)**: Associated with DIC
** M4/M5**: CNS involvement

** Features**: Pancytopenia, organomegaly, DIC (M3), leukostasis (high WBC → headache, dyspnea)

** Treatment **:
- AML: Cytarabine + daunorubicin/idarubicin
- ALL: Daunorubicin + vincristine + prednisone
- ALL: **Intrathecal methotrexate** for CNS prophylaxis

---

### Chronic Leukemia

** CML**: Philadelphia chromosome (t(9;22)), ↓ LAP, high B12, massive splenomegaly
- ** Treatment**: Imatinib → BMT if fails

** CLL**: Smudge cells, CD5+, CD20+, CD23 +
- ** Staging**: 0 (lymphocytosis) → 1 (lymphadenopathy) → 2 (splenomegaly) → 3 (anemia) → 4 (thrombocytopenia)

---

### Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS)

** Pre-AML**, 5q- deletion

** Features**: Pancytopenia, Pelger-Huet cells (bilobed neutrophils)

** Treatment**: Lenalidomide, azacitidine, BMT

---

### Polycythemia Vera

** Features**: ↑ Hct, splenomegaly, thrombosis, plethora

** Diagnosis**: JAK-2 mutation (most specific)

** Treatment**: Phlebotomy, hydroxyurea, aspirin

---

### Multiple Myeloma

** Features **:
- ** Bone pain** (most common): Pathologic fractures, lytic lesions
- Anemia, hypercalcemia, renal failure
- Infections (encapsulated organisms: pneumococcus, Haemophilus)

** Diagnosis **:
- Monoclonal IgG (protein electrophoresis)
- ** Bence Jones protein** (urine)
- Bone marrow >10% plasma cells
- **↑↑ ESR **

### Bleeding Disorders

#### Platelet Disorders

** ITP** (Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura)
- ** Features**: Mucocutaneous bleeding, petechiae, purpura, epistaxis
- ** NO splenomegaly **
- ** Diagnosis**: Thrombocytopenia, ↑ megakaryocytes (marrow), antiplatelet antibodies
- ** Treatment**: Prednisone, IVIG, splenectomy, romiplostim/eltrombopag

** Von Willebrand Disease** (most common inherited bleeding disorder)
- ** Autosomal dominant **
- ** Features**: Mucocutaneous bleeding, epistaxis, menorrhagia
- ** Diagnosis**: Normal platelet, ↑ bleeding time, **↓ vWF**, ↑ PTT
- ** Treatment**: Desmopressin (DDAVP), factor VIII replacement

---

#### Coagulation Disorders

** Hemophilia A** (Factor VIII deficiency) & **Hemophilia B** (Factor IX deficiency)
- ** X-linked recessive **
- ** Features**: **Deep bleeding**: hemarthrosis, hematomas, GI/urinary bleeding
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ PTT, normal PT; mixing study corrects
- ** Treatment**: Desmopressin (mild), factor replacement (severe)

** Vitamin K Deficiency **
- Factors affected: **II, VII, IX, X, Protein C, Protein S ** (1972)
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ PT, ↑ PTT (corrects with vitamin K)
- ** Treatment**: Vitamin K + FFP (severe bleeding)

** Liver Disease **
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ PT, ↑ PTT (does NOT correct with vitamin K), thrombocytopenia
- ** Treatment**: FFP

---

#### DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation)

** Causes**: Sepsis (gram-negative), trauma, obstetrical disasters, M3 (APL), malignancy

** Features**: Bleeding, hemolysis, renal failure, jaundice, confusion

** Diagnosis **:
- ↑ PT, ↑ PTT, ↓ Platelets, ↓ Fibrinogen
- **↑ D-dimer, ↑ FSP (fibrin split products) **
- Schistocytes on smear

** Treatment**: FFP, platelet transfusions (severe bleeding); treat underlying cause

---

#### TTP/HUS

** TTP**: ADAMTS13 deficiency → ↑ vWF → platelet thrombi
- ** Features**: Thrombocytopenia, MAHA, fever, renal failure, neurologic changes
- ** Treatment**: Plasmapheresis (do NOT transfuse platelets)

** HUS**: Shiga toxin (E. coli O157:H7)
- ** Features**: Thrombocytopenia, MAHA, renal failure
- ** Treatment**: Supportive; **do NOT give antibiotics **

## Chapter 6: Infectious Disease (الأمراض المعدية)

### Antibiotics Overview

** Penicillins **:
- ** Penicillin G/V**: Streptococci (not staph)
- ** Ampicillin/Amoxicillin**: Streptococci + Enterococci + Listeria
- ** Penicillinase-resistant (Oxacillin, Nafcillin)**: Staph
- ** MRSA**: Vancomycin, linezolid, daptomycin, ceftaroline
- ** Penicillin + β-lactamase inhibitor (Amoxicillin-clavulanate)**: Staph

** Cephalosporins **:
- **1 st gen (Cefazolin, Cephalexin)**: Staph, Strep
- **2 nd gen (Cefuroxime, Cefoxitin)**: + Haemophilus, Klebsiella
- **3 rd gen (Ceftriaxone, Cefotaxime)**: Gram-negative (NO staph)
- **3 rd gen with Pseudomonas coverage**: Ceftazidime
- **4 th gen (Cefepime)**: Broad-spectrum (Gram-positive + Gram-negative)
- ** Ceftaroline**: Only cephalosporin with MRSA coverage

** Macrolides (Azithromycin, Clarithromycin)**: Atypical pneumonia, Strep

** Fluoroquinolones **:
- ** Ciprofloxacin**: Gram-negative, Pseudomonas
- ** Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin**: Atypical pneumonia (Strep pneumoniae)
- Side effects: Cartilage damage, tendon rupture

** Aminoglycosides (Gentamicin, Tobramycin, Amikacin)**: Gram-negative only
- ** Side effects**: Nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity

** Carbapenems (Imipenem, Meropenem)**: Broad-spectrum, Pseudomonas (except ertapenem)
- Side effects: Seizures

** Vancomycin**: MRSA, covers both Gram-positive and negative
- Side effects: Red man syndrome, nephrotoxicity

** Linezolid**: MRSA, VRSA, oral option
- Side effects: Myelosuppression, serotonin syndrome

** Metronidazole**: Anaerobes (below diaphragm)
** Clindamycin**: Anaerobes (above diaphragm), aspiration pneumonia

### CNS Infections

#### Meningitis

** Bacterial Causes by Age **:
- ** Neonates**: Group B Strep, E. coli, Listeria

- ** Adults**: **Streptococcus pneumoniae** (most common)
- ** Elderly/Immunosuppressed**: Listeria monocytogenes

** Features **:
- Fever, headache, photophobia, **nuchal rigidity **

- Empiric: **Vancomycin + 3rd generation cephalosporin** + ampicillin (Listeria)

- S. pneumoniae: Vancomycin

** Acute**: Viruses, Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, B. pertussis

#### Pneumonia

** Typical Pneumonia**: Strep pneumoniae, H. influenzae, Moraxella
** Atypical**: Mycoplasma, Legionella, Chlamydia, Viral

** Features **:
- Fever, productive cough, chest pain
- Rusty sputum: Strep pneumoniae
- ** Currant jelly sputum**: Klebsiella
- ** Dry cough + bullous myringitis + hemolytic anemia**: Mycoplasma
- ** CNS + GI symptoms**: Legionella
- ** PCP**: Dyspnea on exertion, HIV with CD4 <200

** CURB-65 Score** (for hospitalization) :
- ** C**onfusion: 1
- ** U**remia (BUN >7 mmol/L): 1
- ** R**espiratory rate >30: 1
- ** B**P (SBP <90 or DBP ≤60): 1
- **65** age ≥65: 1

#### Tuberculosis

** Features**: Fever, cough, night sweats, weight loss, hemoptysis
- Most common extra-pulmonary site: **Lymph nodes **
- ** Pott's disease**: Vertebral TB

** Diagnosis **:
- ** CXR**: Apical infiltrates, cavities, Ghon complex
- Sputum AFB stain, culture (most specific, 4-6 weeks)
- PPD: Screening (NOT diagnostic)

** PPD Interpretation **:
- ≥5 mm: HIV+, close contacts, abnormal CXR
- ≥10 mm: High-risk groups (HCWs, immigrants, immunocompromised)
- ≥15 mm: Low-risk population

** Treatment**: 4 drugs (INH, RIF, PZA, EMB) for 2 months → 2 drugs (INH, RIF) for 4 months

** Side Effects **:
- All TB drugs: Hepatotoxicity (except streptomycin)
- ** INH**: Peripheral neuropathy → give **B6 (pyridoxine) **
- ** Rifampin**: Orange/red body fluids
- ** Ethambutol**: Optic neuritis
- ** Pyrazinamide**: Hyperuricemia

** Latent TB**: INH + B6 for 9 months

### Infective Endocarditis

- ** Minor**: Predisposition, fever, vascular phenomena, immunologic phenomena

- MRSA: Vancomycin

### HIV/AIDS

## Chapter 7: Nephrology (أمراض الكلى)

### Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

** Definition**: Rapid rise in BUN/Cr over hours to days

** Classification **:
1. ** Prerenal**: ↓ Perfusion to kidney (hypovolemia, shock, CHF)
2. ** Intrarenal**: Kidney defect (ATN, glomerular)
3. ** Postrenal**: ↓ Drainage (obstruction)

** Diagnosis **:
- ** BUN/Cr ratio **:
- Prerenal: **20:1 **
- Intrinsic/Postrenal: **10:1 **
- ** Fractional Excretion of Na (FENa) ** :
- Prerenal: <1%
- Intrinsic: >2%

---

### Prerenal Azotemia

** Causes**: Hypovolemia, hypotension, CHF, renal artery stenosis, hepatorenal syndrome

** Diagnosis **:
- BUN/Cr = 20:1
- ↓ Urine Na (<10-20 mEq/L)
- ↓ FENa (<1%)
- ↑ Urine osmolality (>500)
- ↑ Specific gravity (>1.020)

** Hepatorenal Syndrome **:
- Hepatic failure → intense vasoconstriction of afferent arteriole
- ** Diagnosis**: No improvement after 1.5L colloid
- ** Treatment**: Midodrine + octreotide, liver transplant

---

### Postrenal Azotemia

** Causes**: Stones, tumor, BPH, neurogenic bladder

** Diagnosis **:
- BUN/Cr = 10:1 (or less)
- Distended bladder
- ** Hydronephrosis** on US/CT
- Residual volume >50 mL

---

### Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN)

** Causes**: Ischemia, nephrotoxins

** Phases **:
1. Prodromal
2. ** Oliguric** (<400 mL/24h) or Anuric (<100 mL/24h)
3. Postoliguric (diuretic phase)

** Diagnosis **:
- BUN/Cr = 10:1
- ↑ Urine Na (>40 mEq/L)
- ↑ FENa (>2%)
- ↓ Urine osmolality (<350)
- ** Muddy brown casts **

** Treatment**: Supportive, hydration, treat cause

---

### Allergic Interstitial Nephritis

** Causes**: Penicillins, cephalosporins, allopurinol, rifampin, quinolones, sulfonamides

** Features**: Fever, rash, joint pain

** Diagnosis **:
- ↑ Eosinophils, ↑ IgE
- ** Eosinophiluria** (Hansel stain)
- Kidney biopsy (most accurate)

** Treatment**: Stop offending drug, short course steroids

---

### Pigment-Induced Nephropathy

** Rhabdomyolysis** (myoglobin release) :
- ** Causes**: Crush injury, seizures, exertion, statins
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ CPK (>10,000-100,000), acidosis, hyperphosphatemia, hypocalcemia, hyperuricemia
- ** Treatment**: Hydration, mannitol, **alkalinize urine with HCO₃**; calcium chloride (if ECG changes)

---

### Contrast-Induced Nephropathy

** Timing**: 12-24 hours after contrast

** Mechanism**: Vasospasm of afferent arteriole

** Prevention**: N/S 1-2L before procedure, HCO₃, N-acetylcysteine

---

### Glomerulonephritis (Nephritic Syndrome)

** Features **:
- ** Edema** (periorbital), hematuria, **RBC casts **
- HTN, proteinuria <2 g/24h

| Disease | Key Features | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|---------|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| ** Wegener's (GPA)** | Lung, kidney, upper respiratory tract | **c-ANCA** | Cyclophosphamide + Steroids |
| ** Churg-Strauss** | Asthma, eosinophilia | **p-ANCA** | Cyclophosphamide + Steroids |
| ** Goodpasture** | Lung (hemoptysis) + Kidney | **Anti-GBM Ab** (type IV collagen) | Plasmapheresis + Steroids |
| ** Polyarteritis nodosa** | All organs except lung, **HBV-associated** | p-ANCA, sural nerve biopsy | Cyclophosphamide + Steroids |
| ** HSP (Henoch-Schönlein)** | Palpable purpura, abdominal pain, arthritis | IgA deposition; biopsy | Supportive (spontaneous resolution) |
| ** IgA Nephropathy (Berger's)** | Hematuria 1-2 days after viral illness | IgA high, normal C3, biopsy | ACE inhibitors (proteinuria); steroids (massive) |
| ** Post-streptococcal** | 1-2 weeks post pharyngitis (or 3-6 weeks post skin) | ↓ C3, **↑ ASLO**, IgG+C3 deposits | Diuretics, antibiotics |

---

### Nephrotic Syndrome

** Definition**: Proteinuria >3.5 g/24h, hypoalbuminemia, edema, hyperlipidemia

** Features **:
- Edema (salt/water retention + low oncotic pressure)
- Hyperlipidemia, hyperlipiduria
- ** Hypercoagulable state** (loss of antithrombin, protein C/S)

** Types **:

| Type | Most common in | Characteristics | Treatment |
|------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| ** Minimal Change** | Children | Effacement of foot processes (EM) | Steroids |
| ** FSGS** | Adults | Associated with HIV, heroin | Limited steroid response |
| ** Membranous** | Adults | Associated with HBV, HCV, SLE, cancer, NSAIDs | Steroids + Cyclophosphamide |
| ** MPGN** | - | Associated with hepatitis, **↓ C3** | Treat underlying |

---

### Electrolyte Disorders

#### Hyponatremia (Na <135)

** Types **:
- ** Hypovolemic**: GI loss, diuretics, adrenal insufficiency
- ** Hypervolemic**: CHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, renal failure
- ** Euvolemic**: SIADH, hypothyroidism, psychogenic polydipsia

** Treatment **:
- Mild: Fluid restriction
- Moderate: N/S + diuretics
- Severe: 3% hypertonic saline; **Correction rate: 0.5-1 mEq/L/hour** (faster → central pontine myelinolysis)

---

#### Hypernatremia (Na >145)

** Causes**: Dehydration, DI, osmotic diuresis

** Treatment**: Isotonic IV fluids; **Correction: 1 mEq/L every 2 hours** (faster → cerebral edema)

---

#### Hypokalemia (K <3.5)

** Causes**: GI loss, diuretics, ↑ aldosterone, alkalosis, insulin, β-agonists

** ECG**: Flat T wave, U wave, prolonged QT

** Treatment**: Oral/IV K⁺ (max IV 10-20 mEq/h); avoid dextrose

---

#### Hyperkalemia (K >5.5)

** ECG**: Peaked T waves, wide QRS, prolonged PR, short QT

** Treatment** (in order) :
1. ** Calcium gluconate**: Protects heart
2. Insulin + Glucose: Works in 30-60 min
3. Sodium bicarbonate (do NOT give with calcium)
4. Albuterol (β-agonist)
5. Kayexalate: Removes K⁺ via GI
6. Dialysis

---

### Acid-Base Disorders

** Anion Gap = (Na⁺) - (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻)** (Normal: 8-12)

** High Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (GOLDMARK) **:
- ** G**: Glycols (ethylene glycol)
- ** O**: Oxoproline (paracetamol)
- ** L**: L-Lactate
- ** D**: D-Lactate
- ** M**: Methanol
- ** A**: Aspirin (salicylates)
- ** R**: Renal failure
- ** K**: Ketoacidosis (DKA, alcoholic)

** Normal Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (HARDUP) **:
- ** H**: Hyperalimentation
- ** A**: Acetazolamide
- ** R**: RTA (type 1, 2, 4)
- ** D**: Diarrhea
- ** U**: Ureterosigmoidostomy
- ** P**: Pancreatic fistula

---

### Renal Tubular Acidosis (RTA)

| Type | Site | Features | Treatment |
|------|------|----------|-----------|
| ** Type 1 (Distal)** | Distal tubule | **HypoK**, nephrocalcinosis, stones, urine pH >5.5 | Oral HCO₃, K citrate |
| ** Type 2 (Proximal)** | Proximal tubule | **HypoK**, Fanconi syndrome, urine acidic | Thiazides + HCO₃ |
| ** Type 4** | Distal tubule | **HyperK**, mild renal insufficiency, hypoaldosteronism | Fludrocortisone |

---

### Nephrolithiasis

** Most common**: Calcium oxalate

** Features**: Flank pain radiating to groin, hematuria

** Diagnosis**: Spiral CT (most accurate)

** Treatment **:
- Analgesia, hydration, bed rest
- Shock wave lithotripsy (<2 cm)
- Ureteroscopy (5-7 mm stones: nifedipine + tamsulosin)

---

### Hypertension

** Definition**: SBP ≥140 or DBP ≥90

** Risk Factors**: Age, male, Black, obesity, smoking, DM

** Secondary HTN**: Consider if age <25 or >55, refractory HTN, or specific features

** Causes **:
1. ** Renal artery stenosis**: Atherosclerosis (elderly), fibromuscular dysplasia (young women)
- ** Diagnosis**: Captopril renogram, duplex, arteriogram
2. ** Primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn syndrome)**: HTN + hypokalemia
- ** Diagnosis**: ↑ Aldosterone, ↓ Renin
3. ** Pheochromocytoma**: Episodic HTN, headache, sweating, palpitations
4. ** Cushing's syndrome**: HTN + cushingoid features
5. ** Coarctation of aorta**: HTN upper extremities, radiofemoral delay

** Hypertensive Emergency **:
- SBP ≥180 or DBP ≥120 with **target organ damage **
- Treatment: IV (nitroprusside, labetalol); **↓ BP by ≤25% in first hour **

---

### Antihypertensive Medications

| Class | Examples | Side Effects |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| ** Thiazides** | HCTZ | Hyperglycemia, hyperuricemia, hypercalcemia, hypokalemia |
| ** Loop** | Furosemide | Hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, ototoxicity |
| ** K-sparing** | Spironolactone | Hyperkalemia, gynecomastia |
| ** Beta-blockers** | Propranolol, metoprolol | Bradycardia, bronchoconstriction, masks hypoglycemia |
| ** ACE inhibitors** | Enalapril, lisinopril | Cough, angioedema, hyperkalemia; **contraindicated in pregnancy, bilateral renal artery stenosis ** |
| ** ARBs** | Losartan, valsartan | Same as ACE but no cough |
| ** CCBs** | Amlodipine (dihydropyridine), Verapamil (non-dihydropyridine) | Ankle edema (most common), gingival hypertrophy; Verapamil → bradycardia, AV block |
| ** Central** | Methyldopa | Hemolytic anemia, hepatitis |

## Chapter 8: Pulmonology (أمراض الرئة)

### Pulmonary Function Tests

| Parameter | Obstructive | Restrictive |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| ** FEV1/FVC** | ↓↓ (<70%) | Normal or ↑ |
| ** TLC** | ↑ (hyperinflation) | ↓ |
| ** RV ** | ↑↑ | ↓ |
| ** DLCO** | ↓ (emphysema); Normal (bronchitis) | ↓ |

** Bronchodilator Reversibility**: ↑ FEV1 ≥12% and ≥200 mL → **Asthma** (not COPD)

** Flow-Volume Loops **:
- Restrictive: Shift right
- Obstructive: Scalloped (saw-tooth)
- ** Fixed obstruction**: Flattened at top and bottom

---

### Obstructive Diseases

#### Asthma

** Features **:
- Reversible airway obstruction, inflammation, hyperreactivity
- ** Extrinsic**: Allergic (IgE-mediated), childhood onset
- ** Intrinsic**: Non-allergic (infections, exercise, cold air)

** Features**: Wheezing, dyspnea, cough, chest tightness
- ** Poor prognostic signs**: Pulsus paradoxus, silent chest, cyanosis, fatigue

** Diagnosis**: PFTs (reversibility), Methacholine challenge (↓ FEV1 ≥20%)

** Treatment **:
- ** Acute**: O₂, β-agonists (albuterol), anticholinergics, systemic steroids
- ** Chronic**: ICS (cornerstone) + long-acting β-agonists; leukotriene modifiers, mast cell stabilizers (children)

** Allergic Bronchopulmonary Aspergillosis (ABPA) **
- Asthma + Aspergillus → eosinophilia, ↑ IgE, migrating infiltrates
- ** Treatment**: Corticosteroids

---

#### COPD

** Definition**: Chronic bronchitis (cough 3 months for 2 years) + Emphysema (permanent dilation of air spaces)

** Cause**: Smoking (20 pack-years), Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency

** Emphysema **:
- Distant breath sounds, hyperinflation, ↓ DLCO
- ** Centrilobular**: Smokers; **Panacinar**: AAT deficiency

** Chronic Bronchitis **:
- Rhonchi/wheezes, cyanosis, normal DLCO
- ** Blue bloater** vs **Pink puffer **

** Treatment (Stable) **:
- ** Anticholinergics** (tiotropium) - First line
- β-agonists, theophylline (watch toxicity)
- Home O₂ (if PaO₂ <55 or SaO₂ <88%)
- Smoking cessation, vaccines (pneumococcal, influenza)

** Exacerbation Treatment**: O₂ (Venturi mask), bronchodilators, steroids, antibiotics, NIV if needed

** Prognosis**: FEV1 best predictor of survival

---

#### Bronchiectasis

** Definition**: Permanent dilation of bronchi

** Causes**: TB, CF, immotile cilia, Kartagener's syndrome

** Features**: Copious purulent sputum, crackles, recurrent pneumonia, clubbing

** Diagnosis**: High-resolution CT (best)

** Treatment**: Bronchodilators, chest physiotherapy, antibiotics (TMP/SMX, amoxicillin)

** Complications**: Hemoptysis, amyloidosis, cor pulmonale

---

### Interstitial Lung Disease

** Features**: Dyspnea, nonproductive cough, crackles, restrictive PFTs

#### Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis (IPF)
- ** Features**: Ground-glass appearance (HRCT), honeycombing (late)
- ** Treatment**: Pirfenidone, lung transplant (worsens prognosis)

#### Sarcoidosis
- ** Features**: Noncaseating granulomas, bilateral hilar adenopathy
- ** Lofgren's syndrome**: Erythema nodosum, arthritis, hilar adenopathy
- ** Heerfordt's**: Fever, parotid enlargement, uveitis, facial palsy
- ** Diagnosis**: Biopsy, ↑ ACE
- ** Treatment**: Steroids (mandatory for uveitis, CNS, cardiac, hypercalcemia)

#### Pneumoconiosis
- ** Asbestosis**: Lower lobes, pleural plaques, ↑ risk of bronchogenic cancer, mesothelioma
- ** Silicosis**: Upper lobes, eggshell calcification, ↑ risk of TB
- ** Coal worker's**: Upper lobes, Caplan syndrome (RA + pneumoconiosis)

---

### Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

** Risk Factors** (Virchow's triad): Stasis, hypercoagulability, endothelial damage
- Recent surgery (orthopedic), cancer, immobility, OCP, pregnancy, thrombophilia

** Features **:
- ** PE**: Sudden dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, hemoptysis
- ** DVT**: Swelling, pain in calf/thigh

** Diagnosis **:
- ** CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA)**: Initial test of choice
- ** V/Q scan**: Alternative
- ** D-dimer**: Most sensitive (but not specific)
- ** CXR**: Normal, Hampton hump, Westermark sign
- ** ECG**: Sinus tachycardia (most common), S1Q3T3 (specific)

** Treatment **:
- ** Heparin** (LMWH/unfractionated) + **Warfarin** (6 months)
- Thrombolytics: For hemodynamically unstable patients
- ** Warfarin contraindicated in pregnancy** → LMWH

** HIT (Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia) **:
- Platelets ↓ 4-10 days after heparin start
- ** Treatment**: Stop heparin; use argatroban (direct thrombin inhibitor)

---

### ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome)

** Features**: Acute lung injury, increased permeability, pulmonary edema

** Causes**: Sepsis, trauma, DIC, pneumonia, aspiration

** Treatment**: Mechanical ventilation with **PEEP** (positive end-expiratory pressure); treat underlying cause

---

### Respiratory Failure

| Type | PO₂ | PCO₂ | Causes |
|------|-----|------|--------|
| ** Type 1 (Hypoxemic)** | <60 | Normal or ↓ | Pneumonia, PE, asthma, emphysema |
| ** Type 2 (Hypercapnic)** | <60 | ↑ | COPD, narcotics, hypoventilation, chest wall abnormalities |

---

## Chapter 9: Emergency Medicine (طب الطوارئ)

### Basic Life Support (CPR)

** Steps**: CAB (not ABC for adults)
- ** C**hest compressions: At least 2 inches (5 cm), 100-120/min
- ** A**irway: Open
- ** B**reathing: 2 breaths

** Ratio**: 30:2 (single rescuer)

---

### Shockable Rhythms

#### Ventricular Fibrillation (VF) / Pulseless VT

** Treatment **:
1. ** Defibrillation** (non-synchronized) - First !
2. CPR for 2 minutes
3. Epinephrine 1 mg q3-5 min
4. Amiodarone 300 mg after 3rd shock
5. Consider reversible causes (5Hs, 5Ts)

** Post-resuscitation**: Hypothermia protocol (32-34°C for 12-24 hours)

---

### Non-Shockable Rhythms

#### Asystole / PEA

**5 Hs **:
1. Hypovolemia
2. Hypoxia
3. Hydrogen ion (acidosis)
4. Hypo-/hyperkalemia
5. Hypothermia

**5 Ts **:
1. Tension pneumothorax
2. Tamponade (cardiac)
3. Toxins
4. Thrombosis (PE)
5. Thrombosis (MI)

** Treatment**: CPR, IV epinephrine q3-5 min; no shock

---

### Toxicology

#### Acetaminophen (Paracetamol) Poisoning

** Toxic dose**: 7-10 grams (fatal >12-15 g)

** Stages **:
- I (0-24h): Nausea, vomiting
- II (24-72h): ↑ LFTs, jaundice, hepatic failure
- III (72-96h): Peak liver failure
- IV (>5 days): Resolution or death

** Diagnosis**: Acetaminophen level at 4 hours, **↑ AST > ALT **

** Treatment **:
- Activated charcoal (within 1-2 hours)
- ** N-acetylcysteine (NAC)**: Within 8 hours
- Liver transplant if severe

---

#### Salicylate (Aspirin) Poisoning

** Features**: Nausea, vomiting, tinnitus, hyperthermia, confusion, metabolic acidosis (high anion gap)

** Diagnosis**: Aspirin level, ↑ anion gap

** Treatment **:
- Activated charcoal
- ** Alkalinize urine** (IV bicarbonate)
- Hemodialysis (severe)

---

#### Methanol & Ethylene Glycol Poisoning

** Features **:
- Methanol: Visual disturbances, blindness
- Ethylene glycol: Renal failure, oxalate crystals

** Diagnosis**: ↑ Osmolar gap, high anion gap metabolic acidosis

** Treatment**: **Fomepizole** (alcohol dehydrogenase inhibitor), dialysis

---

#### Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

** Features**: Headache (most common), nausea, confusion, cherry-red skin (late)

** Diagnosis**: Carboxyhemoglobin level (pulse oximetry is normal)

** Treatment**: 100% O₂, hyperbaric O₂

---

#### Opioid Poisoning

** Features**: Miosis, respiratory depression, constipation, bradycardia, hypotension

** Treatment**: **Naloxone **

---

#### Tricyclic Antidepressant (TCA) Poisoning

** Features**: Anticholinergic (dry mouth, tachycardia, mydriasis), **QRS widening**, confusion, seizures

** Treatment**: Activated charcoal, **IV bicarbonate** (protects heart)

---

#### Anticholinergic Poisoning

** Features**: "Red as a beet, dry as a bone, hot as a hair, blind as a bat, mad as a hatter, full as a flask "

** Treatment**: Supportive, BZD (seizures), bicarbonate (cardiac)

---

#### Organophosphate Poisoning

** Features**: DUMBELSS (Defecation, Urination, Miosis, Bradycardia/Bronchospasm, Emesis, Lacrimation, Salivation, Seizures)

** Treatment**: **Atropine** + **Pralidoxime (2-PAM) **

---

### Subarachnoid Hemorrhage (SAH)

** Etiology**: Saccular aneurysms (Circle of Willis)
- Anterior communicating (most common), Middle cerebral, Posterior communicating

** Features**: "Worst headache of my life," sudden onset, loss of consciousness, nuchal rigidity, photophobia, **3rd nerve palsy** (pupil-sparing)

** Diagnosis **:
- ** CT scan**: Initial test
- ** LP**: Xanthochromia (after 4-6 hours)
- ** Angiography**: Locate aneurysm

** Treatment**: Nimodipine (CCB, prevents vasospasm), surgery (clipping/coiling)

---

## Chapter 10: Neurology (أمراض الأعصاب)

### Stroke (Cerebrovascular Accident)

** Types **:
1. Ischemic (thrombotic, embolic)
2. Hemorrhagic (ICH, SAH)

** Risk factors**: HTN (most significant), smoking, DM, hyperlipidemia

** Diagnosis **:
- ** Non-contrast CT**: Initial test
- ** Diffusion-weighted MRI**: Most accurate for ischemia

---

### Middle Cerebral Artery (MCA) Stroke

** Features **:
- Contralateral hemiplegia (face + arm > leg)
- Contralateral hemisensory loss
- Homonymous hemianopia
- Aphasia (dominant hemisphere)

---

### Anterior Cerebral Artery (ACA) Stroke

** Features **:
- Contralateral weakness in **leg > arm **
- Urinary incontinence
- Confusion, behavioral changes

---

### Posterior Cerebral Artery (PCA) Stroke

** Features **:
- Contralateral homonymous hemianopia (with macula sparing)
- Visual hallucinations, agnosia

---

### Basilar Artery Occlusion

** Locked-in syndrome**: Quadriplegia, preserved consciousness, vertical eye movements

---

### Wallenberg Syndrome (Lateral Medullary)

** Occlusion**: PICA

** Features** (Ipsilateral) :
- Facial sensory loss
- Horner's syndrome
- Dysphagia, hoarseness
- Ataxia

** Contralateral**: Body sensory loss

---

### Seizures

** Classification **:
- ** Partial**: Simple (no LOC) vs Complex (with LOC)
- ** Generalized**: Absence (petit mal), Tonic-clonic (grand mal), Atonic, Myoclonic

** Absence Seizures**: Sudden cessation of activity (5-10 seconds), 3 Hz spike-and-wave; treatment: Ethosuximide (first line)

** Status Epilepticus**: Seizure >5-30 minutes
- ** Treatment**: IV lorazepam/diazepam → phenytoin/fosphenytoin → phenobarbital → anesthetics

** Antiepileptic Drugs **:
- ** Phenytoin**: Gum hypertrophy, hirsutism, ataxia
- ** Carbamazepine**: Hyponatremia (SIADH)
- ** Valproic acid**: Weight gain, hepatotoxicity
- ** Lamotrigine**: Stevens-Johnson syndrome
- ** Ethosuximide**: GI upset

---

### Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)

** Acute inflammatory demyelinating polyradiculoneuropathy **

** Etiology**: Post-infectious (Campylobacter jejuni, Mycoplasma)

** Features**: Ascending symmetrical weakness, areflexia, sensory disturbances (paresthesia), cranial nerve involvement

** Miller-Fisher variant**: Ataxia, areflexia, ophthalmoplegia

** Diagnosis**: LP → ↑ protein (no cells), EMG

** Treatment**: IV immunoglobulin, plasmapheresis

---

### Myasthenia Gravis

** Antibodies**: Acetylcholine receptor antibodies

** Features**: Fatigable muscle weakness, ptosis, diplopia, dysphagia, **ocular muscles affected** (unlike polymyositis)

** Diagnosis**: AChR antibodies, Tensilon test, EMG (decremental response)

** Treatment**: Pyridostigmine, thymectomy, steroids, immunosuppressants

** Eaton-Lambert**: Antibodies against Ca²⁺ channels, associated with small cell lung cancer; **improves with activity** (unlike MG)

---

### Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)

** Upper + Lower motor neuron disease **

** Features **:
- ** UMN**: Spasticity, hyperreflexia, Babinski
- ** LMN**: Weakness, atrophy, fasciculations

** Treatment**: Riluzole (inhibits glutamate), baclofen (spasticity)

---

### Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

** Autoimmune demyelinating disease **

** Features **:
- Weakness, numbness, tingling
- Blurry/double vision (optic neuritis)
- Urinary urgency/retention
- ** Relapsing-remitting** (most common)

** Diagnosis**: MRI (T2 hyperintensities, T1 hypointensities), evoked potentials, CSF oligoclonal bands

** Treatment **:
- Relapsing-remitting: Interferon-β, glatiramer acetate
- Secondary progressive: IFN-β1b, mitoxantrone
- Acute exacerbation: IV steroids

---

### Parkinson's Disease

** Dopamine deficiency** (substantia nigra)

** Features** (TRAP) :
- ** T**remor (resting, pill-rolling)
- ** R**igidity (cogwheel)
- ** A**kinesia/Bradykinesia
- ** P**ostural instability

** Treatment **:
- **<60 years, intact function**: Anticholinergics (benztropine) or amantadine
- **<60 years, compromised**: Pramipexole, ropinirole
- **>60 years**: **Carbidopa/levodopa** (most effective)

** Side effects**: Dyskinesia, akathisia, on-off phenomenon

---

### Huntington's Disease

** Autosomal dominant**, chromosome 4 (CAG repeat)

** Features**: Chorea, dementia, psychiatric disturbances

** Treatment**: Tetrabenazine (chorea), haloperidol

---

### Dementia

** Alzheimer's Disease** (most common cause)
- ** Features**: Cortical atrophy, senile plaques (amyloid), neurofibrillary tangles
- ** Chromosome 21** (APP gene)
- ** Treatment**: Cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine), memantine (NMDA antagonist)

** Vascular Dementia**: Stepwise deterioration, focal deficits

** Lewy Body Dementia**: Parkinsonism, hallucinations, fluctuating cognition

** Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus**: Dementia, urinary incontinence, gait apraxia → treat with shunt

---

### Cranial Nerves

| Nerve | Name | Function | Palsy |
|-------|------|----------|-------|
| ** CN III** | Oculomotor | Eye movement, pupil | Down & out, ptosis, mydriasis (surgical cause) |
| ** CN VI** | Abducens | Lateral rectus | Loss of lateral gaze (most common in ↑ICP) |
| ** CN VII** | Facial | Facial muscles, taste | Bell's palsy (forehead involvement) |
| ** CN VIII** | Vestibulocochlear | Hearing, balance | Deafness, nystagmus |

**3 rd Nerve Palsy **:
- Medical (DM, HTN): Pupil-sparing
- Surgical (aneurysm, tumor): **Pupil-involved (mydriasis) **

1. Endocrinology
Core Topics: Pituitary disorders, Thyroid/Parathyroid disease, Adrenal disorders, and Diabetes Mellitus.
Detailed Explanations:
Pituitary: Prolactinoma is the most common pituitary mass. It presents with galactorrhea/amenorrhea in women and erectile dysfunction in men. Acromegaly is diagnosed by measuring IGF-1 (initial) and a GH suppression test with glucose (most accurate).
Thyroid: Graves' Disease is the most common cause of hyperthyroidism (autoimmune), characterized by exophthalmos and pretibial myxedema. Hashimoto's Thyroiditis is the leading cause of hypothyroidism in developed countries, presenting with high TSH and low T4.
Diabetes Mellitus: Type 1 is an absolute insulin deficiency (autoimmune), while Type 2 involves insulin resistance. Diagnostic criteria include FBS ≥126 mg/dL (twice) or HbA1c ≥6.5%. Metformin is the first-line treatment but is contraindicated in renal failure.
High-Yield Exam Points:
Hypopituitarism: Hormones are lost in order: FSH/LH → GH → TSH → ACTH.
Diabetes Insipidus (DI): Central DI (ADH deficiency) is treated with Desmopressin .
SIADH: Features water retention (no edema) and hyponatremia; correction must be slow to prevent central pontine myelinolysis .
Hypercalcemia: "Stones, bones, abdominal groans, and psychiatric moans".

2. Cardiology
Core Topics: Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD), Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS), Heart Failure (HF), and Valvular disease .
Clinical Presentation & Management:
ACS: Includes Unstable Angina, NSTEMI, and STEMI. STEMI shows ST elevation and positive enzymes. Initial management (MONA) : Morphine, Oxygen, Nitroglycerin, Aspirin. PCI within 90 minutes is the best treatment.
Heart Failure: Systolic HF (EF <45%) vs. Diastolic HF (normal EF). Survival-improving drugs : ACE inhibitors, ARBs, Beta-blockers, and Spironolactone [5, 744; 8, 1582].
Valvular Disease: Mitral Stenosis is mostly caused by rheumatic fever; hallmark is an "Opening snap" and mid-diastolic rumble. Aortic Stenosis triad: Angina, Syncope, Dyspnea.
Red Flags:
Cardiac Tamponade: Beck’s triad (Low BP, JVD, muffled heart sounds) and pulsus paradoxus .
HOCM: Most common cause of sudden death in young athletes.
Exam Traps:
Digoxin, furosemide, and thiazides improve symptoms but do NOT decrease mortality [5, 744; 8, 1582].
Inferior MI (leads II, III, aVF ) often presents with nausea and bradycardia due to vagal reflex.
Arabic Explanation | شرح مبسط الذبحة الصدرية والجلطة القلبية تعالج ببروتوكول MONA (مورفين، أكسجين، نيترو، أسبرين)، والقسطرة (PCI) هي الحل الأفضل. في فشل القلب، الأدوية التي تطيل العمر هي مثبطات ACE وحاصرات بيتا والسبيرونولاكتون، بينما الديجوكسين والمدرات تحسن الأعراض فقط. تضيق الصمام الأبهري يتميز بثلاثية: ألم صدر، إغماء، وضيق تنفس. احذر من "نباتات القلب " (Tamponade) التي تسبب كتمان أصوات القلب وانخفاض الضغط، وهي حالة طارئة [5، 744-748؛ 8، 1578-1582] .

3. Gastroenterology & Hepatology
Clinical Presentation & Investigations:
Achalasia: Dysphagia to both solids and liquids; "Bird’s beak" on barium swallow; Manometry is the most accurate test.
PUD: H. pylori is the #1 cause. Urea breath test and stool antigen distinguish old vs. new infection.
Liver Cirrhosis: Complications include portal hypertension, esophageal varices, and Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP) . SAAG >1.1 indicates portal hypertension.
Management:
SBP: Ascitic fluid PMN >250; treat with Cefotaxime .
Hepatic Encephalopathy: Treat with Lactulose and Neomycin.
Arabic Explanation | شرح مبسط في الجهاز الهضمي، يتميز "تعذر الارتخاء " (Achalasia) بصعوبة بلع السوائل والجوامد معاً، ويظهر بشكل "منقار الطير" في الأشعة. جرثومة المعدة هي السبب الأول للقرحة، وفحص النفس أو البراز هو الأفضل للتشخيص. في تليف الكبد، ارتفاع ضغط الوريد البابي يسبب دوالي المريء والاستسقاء. إذا حدث التهاب في سائل البطن (SBP) ، يُعالج بالسيفوتاكسيم. لعلاج غيبوبة الكبد، نستخدم اللاكتولوز لتقليل الأمونيا [8 ، 1552 -1571].

4. Hematology
Anemia Classification:
Microcytic (MCV <80): Iron deficiency (High TIBC), Thalassemia (Normal iron studies), Sideroblastic (Lead poisoning).
Macrocytic (MCV >100): B12 deficiency (neurologic symptoms + high methylmalonic acid) vs. Folate deficiency (no neurologic symptoms).
Bleeding Disorders:
Hemophilia A (VIII) & B (IX): X-linked recessive; Deep bleeding into joints (hemarthrosis); High PTT.
Von Willebrand Disease: Most common inherited bleeding disorder; Mucocutaneous bleeding (epistaxis, menorrhagia).
ITP: Low platelets after viral infection; no splenomegaly; treat with steroids or IVIG.

6. Pulmonology
Pneumonia: Typical (Strep. pneumoniae - rusty sputum) vs. Atypical (Mycoplasma - dry cough, bullous myringitis). HAP is acquired >48h after admission.
Asthma: Reversible; diagnosed by ↑ FEV1 ≥12% after bronchodilator. Red Flag : PCO2 >60 indicates exhaustion and life-threatening status [5, 777; 8, 1653].
PE: Virchow’s triad; sudden dyspnea and pleuritic pain. CTPA is the initial test of choice.
Arabic Explanation | شرح مبسط التهاب الرئة قد يكون نموذجياً (بلغم صدئي) أو غير نموذجي (سعال جاف). الربو يتميز بتحسن وظائف الرئة بعد الموسع القصبي. علامة الخطر الكبرى في الربو هي ارتفاع ثاني أكسيد الكربون (PCO2 > 60) ، مما يعني إجهاد المريض وفشل التنفس. الجلطة الرئوية تسبب ضيق تنفس مفاجئ، وأفضل فحص لها هو الأشعة المقطعية للشرايين الرئوية (CTPA) [5 ، 777-786؛ 8، 1620-1659 ].

7. Neurology
Stroke: Ischemic (most common) vs. Hemorrhagic . MCA Stroke : Contralateral face/arm weakness > leg. ACA Stroke : Leg > arm.
Parkinson’s: TRAP triad (Tremor at rest, Rigidity, Akinesia, Postural instability).
Multiple Sclerosis: Relapsing-remitting is most common; Oligoclonal bands in CSF.
