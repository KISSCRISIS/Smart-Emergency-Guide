# OB/GYN Intern/JMC Reviewed Source

## Status

This is an unpublished reviewed source draft.

It is not a public page.  
It must not be linked from public navigation.  
It is intended to guide later Intern/JMC content creation.

## Intern Scope

OB/GYN content for Intern/JMC should focus on:

- recognizing pregnancy and gynecologic red flags
- early escalation
- safe communication
- exam-pattern thinking
- avoiding missed ectopic pregnancy, hemorrhage, sepsis, hypertensive emergency, and fetal/maternal risk

It should not include:

- medication amount tables
- delivery procedure instructions
- independent obstetric management pathways
- independent discharge decisions
- local protocol replacement
- specialist-level operative planning

## Core Rule

In reproductive-age patients, always consider pregnancy-related risk when clinically relevant.  
The intern role is to recognize red flags, check stability, communicate clearly, and escalate early.

## Universal OB/GYN First Look

Ask:

1. Is the patient pregnant or possibly pregnant?
2. Is there bleeding, pain, collapse, fever, hypertension symptoms, or reduced fetal movement?
3. Is the patient unstable?
4. Is there severe pain, peritonism, shoulder-tip pain, syncope, or heavy bleeding?
5. Is this early pregnancy, late pregnancy, postpartum, or gynecologic?
6. Does this need urgent senior or OB/GYN review?

## High-Yield OB/GYN Syndromes

### Early Pregnancy Bleeding and Pain

Intern focus:

- consider ectopic pregnancy until excluded by appropriate senior-led assessment
- assess hemodynamic stability
- ask about pain, bleeding, syncope, shoulder-tip pain, and last menstrual period when appropriate
- escalate concerning symptoms early

Red flags:

- collapse or syncope
- severe unilateral pelvic pain
- shoulder-tip pain
- hypotension
- heavy bleeding
- peritonism
- positive pregnancy context with pain or bleeding

Common trap:

- reassuring based on mild bleeding without considering ectopic pregnancy

Safe handover phrase:

"Early pregnancy pain or bleeding with concerning features. Ectopic risk needs senior review."

### Heavy Vaginal Bleeding

Intern focus:

- assess stability, amount of bleeding, pregnancy possibility, postpartum status, anticoagulant use, and symptoms of shock
- escalate heavy bleeding or instability immediately
- avoid independent disposition decisions

Red flags:

- hypotension
- syncope
- ongoing heavy bleeding
- postpartum bleeding
- pregnancy context
- severe abdominal pain
- pallor or weakness
- altered mental status

Common trap:

- underestimating bleeding severity from history alone

Safe handover phrase:

"Heavy vaginal bleeding or instability concern. Needs urgent senior and OB/GYN review."

### Hypertensive Symptoms in Pregnancy or Postpartum

Intern focus:

- recognize headache, visual symptoms, epigastric pain, neurological symptoms, severe swelling, or seizure risk
- escalate possible preeclampsia/eclampsia concern
- do not treat as simple headache when pregnancy/postpartum context exists

Red flags:

- severe headache
- visual symptoms
- epigastric or right upper quadrant pain
- seizure
- confusion
- severe hypertension context
- breathlessness
- reduced fetal movement

Common trap:

- missing postpartum hypertensive disease

Safe handover phrase:

"Pregnancy or postpartum hypertension symptoms present. Needs urgent senior OB/GYN review."

### Postpartum Fever or Deterioration

Intern focus:

- consider sepsis, hemorrhage, thromboembolism, wound infection, urinary infection, and breast infection patterns
- escalate fever with systemic symptoms or instability
- assess bleeding, pain, wound, urinary symptoms, breathing, and mental state

Red flags:

- fever with tachycardia or hypotension
- confusion
- severe pelvic or abdominal pain
- foul discharge
- wound concern with systemic illness
- breathlessness or chest pain
- heavy bleeding

Common trap:

- calling postpartum fever benign without systemic assessment

Safe handover phrase:

"Postpartum patient with fever or deterioration. Needs urgent senior review."

### Gynecologic Pelvic Pain

Intern focus:

- consider torsion, ectopic pregnancy, pelvic infection, hemorrhagic cyst, appendicitis, urinary causes, and bowel causes
- escalate sudden severe pain, peritonism, vomiting, fever, pregnancy possibility, or instability

Red flags:

- sudden severe unilateral pain
- vomiting with pelvic pain
- peritonism
- fever with pelvic pain
- positive or possible pregnancy
- syncope
- hemodynamic concern

Common trap:

- assuming pelvic pain is simple menstrual pain without red flag review

Safe handover phrase:

"Pelvic pain has red flags. Needs senior OB/GYN or surgical review."

### Breast Symptoms

Intern focus:

- distinguish common symptoms from red flags
- escalate systemic illness, abscess concern, suspicious mass, skin changes, or severe infection patterns
- avoid reassurance when red flags exist

Red flags:

- fever with severe breast pain
- abscess concern
- rapidly progressive redness
- suspicious mass
- nipple changes or bloody discharge
- skin dimpling or peau d'orange appearance

Common trap:

- treating all breast pain as benign without examining red flags

Safe handover phrase:

"Breast complaint has red flags or systemic features. Needs senior review."

### Labor, Delivery, and Late Pregnancy Presentations

Intern focus:

- recognize when pregnancy presentation is urgent
- escalate bleeding, severe abdominal pain, reduced fetal movement, ruptured membranes with concern, hypertension symptoms, fever, or trauma
- do not independently manage delivery decisions

Red flags:

- bleeding
- severe abdominal pain
- reduced fetal movement
- seizure or severe headache
- fever
- trauma
- breathlessness or chest pain
- collapse
- cord or fetal concern if suspected

Common trap:

- delaying OB/GYN review in late pregnancy red flags

Safe handover phrase:

"Late pregnancy presentation with red flags. Needs urgent OB/GYN review."

## JMC Exam Pattern

Common OB/GYN exam themes:

- ectopic pregnancy must not be missed
- pregnancy/postpartum hypertension symptoms are high-risk
- heavy bleeding needs urgent escalation
- postpartum deterioration can be sepsis or hemorrhage
- pelvic pain may be gynecologic, surgical, or pregnancy-related
- safe next step is escalation when red flags exist

## SBAR Handover Template

Situation:
- pregnancy or gynecologic context
- main problem
- stability concern

Background:
- pregnancy status or possibility
- gestational/postpartum context if known
- bleeding, pain, fever, hypertension symptoms
- relevant risk factors

Assessment:
- vitals
- bleeding severity
- abdominal or pelvic red flags
- mental state
- systemic illness signs

Recommendation:
- urgent senior or OB/GYN review
- specific dangerous diagnosis to exclude
