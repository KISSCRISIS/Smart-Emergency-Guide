# Emergency Cases MCQ Bank

### MCQ 1: STEMI / OMI Chest Pain
A patient has classic ischemic chest pain and ECG findings concerning for acute coronary occlusion. What is the most important ED mindset?

A. Wait for troponin confirmation before escalation.
B. Treat the ECG and patient as a time-sensitive occlusion syndrome while following local reperfusion protocol.
C. Discharge if pain improves after analgesia.
D. Repeat ECG only the next day.

**Correct Answer: B**
**Arabic reasoning:** الألم النموذجي مع نمط انسداد لا ينتظر إنزيمات. القرار القاتل هو التأخير، وليس تفعيل المسار مبكراً عند الاشتباه العالي.

### MCQ 2: Aortic Dissection Mimicking ACS
Which clue most strongly pushes chest pain toward aortic dissection rather than uncomplicated ACS?

A. Gradual burning pain after meals.
B. Abrupt maximal pain with neurologic deficit or pulse asymmetry.
C. Pain that improves with rest only.
D. Isolated low-grade fever.

**Correct Answer: B**
**Arabic reasoning:** البداية المفاجئة الشديدة مع علامات وعائية أو عصبية تفتح باب التسلخ وتمنع التفكير الآلي بـ ACS فقط.

### MCQ 3: Pulmonary Embolism Chest Pain
