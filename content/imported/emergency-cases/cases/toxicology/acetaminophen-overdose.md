# Acetaminophen Overdose

## Clinical Vignette
A patient has intentional or accidental acetaminophen ingestion, often initially asymptomatic.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Delayed hepatic failure if risk is missed early.**

## Key ED Clues
History may be unreliable, early symptoms mild, co-ingestion, repeated supratherapeutic use, delayed presentation.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Obtain level at appropriate timing per protocol, assess liver injury risk, start NAC pathway when indicated, and consult poison center for complex timing.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Acetaminophen level, AST/ALT, INR, creatinine, pregnancy test when relevant, co-ingestion screen.

## Common Trap
**Reassurance because the patient looks well early.**
