# Cannot Intubate Cannot Oxygenate

## Clinical Vignette
Airway attempts fail and bag-mask/supraglottic ventilation cannot oxygenate the patient.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Hypoxic arrest without immediate rescue oxygenation.**

## Key ED Clues
Falling saturation, absent effective ventilation, failed intubation attempts, airway obstruction, trauma/edema.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Stop repeated failed attempts, declare emergency, proceed to local front-of-neck access/surgical airway pathway with team roles clear.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Clinical oxygenation/ventilation monitoring; capnography if ventilation achieved.

## Common Trap
**Repeated laryngoscopy attempts while oxygenation worsens.**
