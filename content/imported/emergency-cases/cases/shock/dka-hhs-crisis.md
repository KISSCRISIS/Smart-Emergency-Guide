# DKA / HHS Crisis

## Clinical Vignette
A patient with diabetes or new hyperglycemia has vomiting, dehydration, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing, confusion, or infection trigger.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Shock, cerebral edema risk, potassium shifts, and coma.**

## Key ED Clues
Polyuria/polydipsia, dehydration, ketones/acidosis in DKA, profound hyperosmolarity in HHS, infection/MI trigger.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Assess volume, potassium safety, insulin pathway only after required checks, search for trigger, monitor mental status and electrolytes.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Glucose, ketones, blood gas, electrolytes, anion gap, osmolality when HHS, infection/ischemia evaluation.

## Common Trap
**Starting insulin without considering potassium and volume status.**
