# Preeclampsia / Eclampsia

## Clinical Vignette
A pregnant or postpartum patient has severe headache, visual symptoms, epigastric pain, hypertension, seizure, dyspnea, or edema.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Seizure, stroke, pulmonary edema, HELLP, maternal/fetal death.**

## Key ED Clues
Pregnancy/postpartum context, severe BP, neurologic symptoms, RUQ/epigastric pain, proteinuria or end-organ signs.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Stabilize, prevent/treat seizure per local magnesium pathway, control severe hypertension per protocol, involve OB urgently.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Urine protein assessment, CBC/platelets, CMP/LFTs/creatinine, fetal assessment per OB, CT head if concerning neurologic picture.

## Common Trap
**Forgetting postpartum preeclampsia after delivery.**
