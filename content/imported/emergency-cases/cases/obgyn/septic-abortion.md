# Septic Abortion

## Clinical Vignette
A patient after miscarriage/abortion presents with fever, pelvic pain, bleeding, foul discharge, hypotension, or peritonitis.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Septic shock, uterine perforation, pelvic infection.**

## Key ED Clues
Recent pregnancy event, fever, uterine tenderness, foul discharge, bleeding, shock.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Resuscitate, start broad antimicrobial pathway per local protocol, urgent OB/GYN involvement for source control.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Pregnancy test/hCG, CBC/CMP/lactate, cultures if feasible, pelvic ultrasound, coagulation if bleeding.

## Common Trap
**Treating as simple UTI while ignoring pregnancy-related source.**

A. Localized rash only.
