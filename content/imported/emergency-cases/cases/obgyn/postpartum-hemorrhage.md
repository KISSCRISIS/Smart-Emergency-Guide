# Postpartum Hemorrhage

## Clinical Vignette
A postpartum patient has heavy bleeding, dizziness, hypotension, tachycardia, uterine atony, retained tissue, trauma, or coagulopathy.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Exsanguination and shock.**

## Key ED Clues
Bleeding volume may be underestimated, boggy uterus, pallor, tachycardia, hypotension, ongoing clots.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Call OB/help early, resuscitate, uterine massage/uterotonic pathway per local protocol, identify tone/tissue/trauma/thrombin causes.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
CBC/coagulation, type and cross, bedside ultrasound for retained products when useful.

## Common Trap
**Underestimating blood loss because postpartum bleeding is expected.**
