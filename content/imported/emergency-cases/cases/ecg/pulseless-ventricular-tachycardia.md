# Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia

## Clinical Vignette
A pulseless patient has a regular wide-complex tachycardia.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Cardiac arrest with shockable rhythm.**

## Key ED Clues
No pulse, unresponsiveness, wide regular tachycardia, possible ischemia/electrolyte/toxin trigger.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Treat as cardiac arrest with defibrillation/CPR pathway per local ACLS; do not delay for antiarrhythmic decision first.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Rhythm strip, post-ROSC ECG, electrolytes, tox/ischemia workup.

## Common Trap
**Calling it stable VT because the monitor rhythm looks organized despite no pulse.**
