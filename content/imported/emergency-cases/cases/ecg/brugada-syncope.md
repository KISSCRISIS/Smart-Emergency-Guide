# Brugada Syncope

## Clinical Vignette
A young or middle-aged patient, often male, presents with syncope or nocturnal agonal respirations and ECG shows coved ST elevation in V1–V2.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Sudden ventricular arrhythmic death.**

## Key ED Clues
Coved ST elevation in right precordial leads, syncope without prodrome, fever trigger, family sudden death.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Recognize high-risk ECG pattern, treat fever, avoid culprit drugs, monitor, and involve cardiology/electrophysiology.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
ECG with high right precordial leads if needed, electrolytes, fever/infection evaluation, family history.

## Common Trap
**Discharging syncope because vitals normalized while ECG is high risk.**
