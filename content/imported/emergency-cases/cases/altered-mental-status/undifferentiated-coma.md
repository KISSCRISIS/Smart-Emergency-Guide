# Undifferentiated Coma

## Clinical Vignette
A patient is found unresponsive with unclear history.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Airway loss, hypoxia, hypoglycemia, opioid toxicity, trauma, sepsis, or intracranial catastrophe.**

## Key ED Clues
GCS depressed, abnormal respirations, trauma signs, pupils, temperature, glucose, toxidrome clues.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Protect airway/oxygenation, check glucose, consider naloxone when respiratory depression suggests opioid, search for trauma/sepsis/stroke/toxin.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Glucose, ECG, blood gas, electrolytes, tox/co-ingestion tests, CT head when indicated, temperature.

## Common Trap
**Jumping to CT before fixing hypoxia or hypoglycemia.**
