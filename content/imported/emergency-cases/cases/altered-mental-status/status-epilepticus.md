# Status Epilepticus

## Clinical Vignette
A patient has ongoing seizure activity, recurrent seizures without recovery, or persistent coma after convulsions.

## First 60-Second Impression
Ask immediately:
- Is the patient unstable?
- Is airway, breathing, circulation, disability, or exposure currently threatened?
- Does this case require a resuscitation bay, monitor, senior help, or specialty activation?

## What Can Kill the Patient Now?
**Neuronal injury, hypoxia, acidosis, aspiration, hyperthermia, and refractory seizures.**

## Key ED Clues
Continuous convulsion, repeated seizures, no return to baseline, subtle eye deviation or twitching in nonconvulsive status.

## First ED Actions — Conceptual, Not a Fixed Protocol
Protect airway/oxygenation, check glucose, terminate seizure per local protocol, search for cause, escalate if not responding.

> SEG safety note: this page is for education and board-style clinical reasoning. It is not a bedside protocol. Use local hospital policy, senior supervision, and current guideline pathways for final treatment decisions.

## Essential Tests / Bedside Tools
Glucose, electrolytes, tox screen when relevant, pregnancy test, neuroimaging if new focal signs/trauma, temperature/infection workup.

## Common Trap
**Assuming the seizure ended because motor activity stopped while coma persists.**
