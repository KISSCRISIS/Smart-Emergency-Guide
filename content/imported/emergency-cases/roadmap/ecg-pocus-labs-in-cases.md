# ECG, POCUS, and Labs in ED Cases

## Purpose
How to use ECG, bedside ultrasound, and critical labs as decision accelerators, not decorative tests.

## Core Principle
Emergency cases should be taught as **pattern recognition + physiology + first-danger thinking**, not as memorized diagnosis lists.

## SEG Teaching Rule
Each case page must answer four questions:
1. What is the patient showing?
2. What can kill the patient now?
3. What should the ED team do first conceptually?
4. What is the common trap?

## Arabic Simplification
في الطوارئ، لا تبدأ بالسؤال: "ما التشخيص النهائي؟" فقط. ابدأ بسؤال أخطر: "ما الشيء الذي قد يقتل المريض خلال دقائق إذا لم أنتبه له؟" ثم ابحث عن الاسم الكامل للمرض.

## Assessment Question
A resident is overwhelmed by a long ED case stem. What is the safest first mental step?

A. Memorize every rare diagnosis before touching the patient.
B. Identify instability and the immediate life threat, then refine the diagnosis.
C. Wait for all imaging before addressing ABCs.
D. Ignore vital signs if the history is interesting.

**Correct Answer: B**

**Arabic reasoning:** التفكير الطارئ يبدأ بالخطر الحالي، ثم التفاصيل. التشخيص مهم، لكن إنقاذ الهواء والتنفس والدورة أهم أولاً.
