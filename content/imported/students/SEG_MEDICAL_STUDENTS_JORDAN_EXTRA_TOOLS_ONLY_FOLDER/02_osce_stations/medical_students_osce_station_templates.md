# Medical Students Jordan Path — OSCE Station Templates

**Label:** Original SEG educational OSCE stations. Not official university stations.

Rules:
- No medication doses.
- No independent treatment.
- Student role must remain supervised.
- Use local university/hospital rules for examination, chaperone, and documentation.

---

## Station 1 — Chest Pain History

**Task:** Take a focused history from a patient with chest pain.

**Student must cover:**
- onset and duration
- character/radiation
- dyspnea/sweating/syncope/nausea
- cardiac risk factors
- PE/dissection clues
- medications/allergies
- red flags
- summary to examiner

**Fail-danger item:** Missing red flags or failing to say senior/ED review is needed for high-risk features.

---

## Station 2 — Shortness of Breath Assessment

**Task:** Assess a patient with dyspnea at student level.

**Student must cover:**
- ability to speak
- work of breathing
- chest pain/syncope
- wheeze/cough/fever
- asthma/COPD/heart history
- allergen exposure
- PE risk
- report urgent red flags

---

## Station 3 — Headache Red Flags

**Task:** Take history for a severe headache.

**Must ask:**
- thunderclap onset
- worst headache
- fever/neck stiffness
- neurologic symptoms
- seizure
- trauma
- pregnancy/postpartum
- cancer/immunosuppression
- visual symptoms

---

## Station 4 — Abdominal Pain History

**Task:** Take focused abdominal pain history.

**Must ask:**
- onset/progression/location
- vomiting/diarrhea/constipation
- GI bleeding
- urinary symptoms
- pregnancy possibility
- surgical history
- severe pain out of proportion
- red flags

---

## Station 5 — Pediatric Fever

**Task:** Take pediatric fever history from caregiver.

**Must ask:**
- age
- feeding
- urine output
- activity/interaction
- breathing
- rash
- vomiting/diarrhea
- immunization/chronic disease
- caregiver concern

---

## Station 6 — Pregnancy Red Flags

**Task:** Assess pregnant/postpartum patient with concerning symptom.

**Must ask:**
- gestational age/postpartum timing
- bleeding/pain
- headache/visual symptoms
- chest pain/dyspnea
- fever
- fetal movement if relevant
- syncope/seizure
- report urgent concerns

---

## Station 7 — Mental Health Safety

**Task:** Assess self-harm risk under supervision.

**Must ask:**
- suicidal thoughts
- plan
- intent
- access to means
- previous attempts
- substance use
- support
- immediate safety

**Must not do:** Promise secrecy.

---

## Station 8 — Focused Physical Exam Opening

**Task:** Demonstrate safe exam opening.

**Must include:**
- hand hygiene
- introduce self as student
- explain exam
- consent
- privacy
- pain
- position
- appropriate exposure
- chaperone if needed

---

## Station 9 — ABCDE Recognition

**Task:** Recognize an unwell patient.

**Must include:**
- airway concern
- breathing effort
- circulation/perfusion
- disability/mental status/glucose concern
- exposure/rash/trauma
- call for help
- communicate clearly

---

## Station 10 — One-Minute Case Presentation

**Task:** Present a patient to a supervisor.

**Must include:**
- age/sex
- chief complaint/duration
- key positives
- key negatives/red flags
- relevant history
- exam/vitals
- differential
- concern/plan for supervisor

---

## Station 11 — ECG Danger Recognition

**Task:** Student sees ECG for chest pain.

**Must state:**
- ECG must match patient
- possible high-risk change should be shown to senior
- do not reassure alone
- compare old ECG if available
- uncertainty should be escalated

---

## Station 12 — Infection Control

**Task:** Demonstrate infection prevention in clinical encounter.

**Must include:**
- hand hygiene
- PPE concept
- equipment cleaning
- sharps safety
- isolation respect
- needle-stick reporting

---

## Station 13 — Primary Care Safety-Netting

**Task:** Give safety-net instructions for a low-risk presentation.

**Must include:**
- specific warning signs
- what to do if worse
- follow-up timing according to local/supervisor plan
- check patient understanding
- document advice

---

## Station 14 — Overdose Recognition

**Task:** Take history from suspected ingestion.

**Must ask:**
- what, when, how much
- co-ingestions
- intent
- containers
- alcohol/substances
- current symptoms
- report urgently

---

## Station 15 — Trauma First Impression

**Task:** Observe and report trauma red flags.

**Must include:**
- mechanism
- ABCDE concern
- head injury/LOC
- anticoagulants
- chest/abdomen/pelvis symptoms
- neurovascular concern
- safeguarding when relevant

---

## Station 16 — Student Professionalism Challenge

**Scenario:** Patient asks: “Are you a doctor?”

**Expected response:**  
“I am a medical student working with the medical team. I can take your history/examine you under supervision if you agree, and I will discuss everything with the doctor.”

---

## Station 17 — Lab Interpretation Context

**Task:** Explain how to approach abnormal lab result.

**Must include:**
- identify clinical question
- compare with patient condition
- consider urgency
- report dangerous abnormalities
- avoid interpreting in isolation

---

## Station 18 — Internship Readiness Handover

**Task:** Give ISBAR-style handover.

**Must include:**
- Identify
- Situation
- Background
- Assessment
- Recommendation/request for review
