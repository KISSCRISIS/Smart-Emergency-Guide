# Medical Students Jordan Path — Clerkship & Rotation Checklists

## Universal Clinical Day Checklist

Before seeing patients:
- [ ] ID badge / dress code according to local rules
- [ ] hand hygiene
- [ ] know your supervisor/team
- [ ] know where to report concerns
- [ ] notebook without patient identifiers
- [ ] review today’s learning goal

During patient contact:
- [ ] introduce yourself as a medical student
- [ ] ask permission
- [ ] respect privacy
- [ ] ask red-flag questions
- [ ] present clearly to supervisor
- [ ] do not act beyond role

After clinical day:
- [ ] write 3 learning points
- [ ] write 1 clinical question to review
- [ ] reflect on communication/professionalism
- [ ] remove patient identifiers from notes

## Internal Medicine Rotation

Focus:
- history structure
- chronic disease context
- medication safety awareness
- vitals and deterioration
- case presentation
- labs interpretation

Must learn:
- chest pain red flags
- dyspnea red flags
- sepsis concern
- altered mental status
- diabetes/metabolic red flags
- kidney/liver failure clues

---

## Surgery Rotation

Focus:
- acute abdomen
- trauma basics
- perioperative assessment
- wound care observation
- consent/professionalism
- postoperative red flags

Must learn:
- peritonitis
- obstruction symptoms
- GI bleeding
- severe pain out of proportion
- testicular/gynecologic mimics
- postoperative fever red flags

---

## Pediatrics Rotation

Focus:
- age-specific history
- caregiver communication
- feeding/urine/activity
- respiratory distress
- dehydration
- safeguarding awareness

Must learn:
- lethargy
- poor feeding
- non-blanching rash
- reduced urine
- persistent vomiting
- seizure
- respiratory distress

## OB/GYN Rotation

Focus:
- privacy/chaperone
- pregnancy status
- LMP/gestational age
- bleeding/pain
- pregnancy red flags
- postpartum danger

Must learn:
- ectopic pregnancy concern
- preeclampsia/eclampsia red flags

- pelvic pain red flags

## Emergency Medicine Exposure

Focus:
- first impression
- ABCDE recognition
- red flags
- triage thinking without pretending to triage independently
- handover
- observation logbook

Must learn:
- sick vs not sick
- call for help
- chest pain danger
- dyspnea danger
- trauma mechanism
- overdose
- seizure
- altered mental status

## Family / Community / MOH Exposure

Focus:
- common complaints
- chronic disease
- prevention
- vaccination/public health
- referral decisions
- safety-netting
- health education

Must learn:
- when clinic is not enough
- how to safety-net
- how to communicate referral
- social determinants of health

---

## Psychiatry / Mental Health Exposure

Focus:
- respectful communication
- risk assessment
- self-harm
- psychosis/agitation
- substance use
- confidentiality limits

Must learn:
- suicide plan/intent/means
- unsafe home situation
- intoxication with risk
- medical mimics of psychiatric symptoms
