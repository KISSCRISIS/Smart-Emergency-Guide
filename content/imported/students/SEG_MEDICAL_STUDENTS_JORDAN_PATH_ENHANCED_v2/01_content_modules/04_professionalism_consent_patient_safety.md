# Professionalism, Consent & Patient Safety

## Learning Goal

Teach students safe behavior around patients, families, staff, and confidential information.

## Core Principles

- introduce yourself as a medical student
- ask permission before history or examination
- respect privacy and dignity
- use a chaperone when appropriate according to local rules
- do not expose more than needed
- do not discuss patients in public spaces
- do not photograph patient data
- do not post clinical material online
- report concerns to supervisors
- ask if unsure

## Consent Language

Useful phrase:

“Hello, I am a medical student working with the team. Is it okay if I ask you some questions and examine you under supervision?”

## Patient Safety

Report immediately if you notice:

- patient deterioration
- abnormal vital signs
- severe pain
- confusion
- breathing difficulty
- fall risk
- medication allergy concern
- wrong patient/label concern
- infection control issue
- safeguarding concern

## Common Student Trap

Thinking professionalism means only being polite. It also means protecting privacy, safety, and trust.
