# Family Medicine and Primary Care Clerkship Map

> Safety boundary: This page is for supervised medical student learning. It is not an official Ministry of Health curriculum, not an official university syllabus, and not a treatment protocol.

## Learning Goal

Show students how primary care connects prevention, chronic disease follow-up, early recognition, referral, and community health.

## Why It Matters in Jordan

Students may rotate through primary-care or community settings where the goal is not dramatic emergency management but safe recognition, prevention, follow-up, and referral when needed.

## What the Student Should Know

- Primary care is often the first point of contact.
- Chronic disease review needs structure.
- Red flags determine referral urgency.
- Prevention, vaccination, screening, and counseling matter.
- Social context affects care.

## What the Student Should Do Under Supervision

- Take focused histories for chronic disease.
- Ask about adherence, access, family support, and warning symptoms.
- Practice counseling language.
- Learn referral letter structure.
- Observe clinic flow.

## What the Student Must Not Do Alone

- Change chronic medications.
- Decide referral independently.
- Give false reassurance.
- Ignore mental health or safety concerns.

## Red Flags

- Chest pain.
- Severe shortness of breath.
- Neurologic deficit.
- Severe dehydration.
- Pregnancy danger signs.
- Suicidal thoughts.
- Severe infection signs.

## OSCE / Exam Trap

Treating primary care as “minor cases only.” Primary care is where dangerous disease can first appear.
