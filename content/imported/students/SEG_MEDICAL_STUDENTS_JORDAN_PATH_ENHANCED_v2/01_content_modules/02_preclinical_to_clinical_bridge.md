# Preclinical-to-Clinical Bridge

## Learning Goal

Help students connect basic sciences with clinical presentations.

## Why It Matters

Many students study systems separately, then feel lost when patients present with symptoms instead of textbook chapter titles.

## How to Think

Convert basic science into clinical questions:

### Cardiovascular

Basic science:
- heart pump
- coronary circulation
- conduction system
- blood pressure regulation

Clinical symptoms:
- chest pain
- dyspnea
- syncope
- palpitations
- edema

Danger patterns:
- ACS
- arrhythmia
- shock
- heart failure

### Respiratory

Basic science:
- oxygenation
- ventilation
- airway resistance
- gas exchange

Clinical symptoms:
- dyspnea
- wheeze
- cough
- hypoxia
- chest tightness

Danger patterns:
- severe asthma/COPD
- pneumonia/sepsis
- PE
- pneumothorax
- respiratory failure

### Neurology

Basic science:
- motor pathways
- cranial nerves
- consciousness
- seizures

Clinical symptoms:
- weakness
- confusion
- seizure
- headache
- dizziness

Danger patterns:
- stroke
- intracranial bleed
- meningitis
- status seizure
- raised intracranial pressure

## Student Exercise

For every disease you study, write:

- common symptom
- dangerous symptom
- exam clue
- first test often discussed
- red flag
- what must be reported to supervisor

## Common Student Trap

Learning mechanisms without knowing what the patient looks like.
