# Vitals & ABCDE for Students

## Learning Goal

Teach students a safe recognition framework, not independent resuscitation.

## ABCDE Meaning

### A — Airway

Look for:
- speaking ability
- obstruction sounds
- vomiting/secretions
- reduced consciousness

### B — Breathing

Look for:
- work of breathing
- respiratory rate pattern
- cyanosis
- chest movement
- wheeze/silent chest
- oxygenation if available

### C — Circulation

Look for:
- color
- sweating
- pulse quality
- capillary refill context
- bleeding
- shock appearance

### D — Disability

Look for:
- consciousness
- glucose concern
- pupils
- seizure
- focal deficits

### E — Exposure

Look for:
- rash
- trauma
- temperature
- hidden bleeding
- infection signs

## Student Role

Students should:
- recognize ABCDE danger
- call the team/supervisor
- communicate findings clearly
- observe interventions
- document only under rules

Students should not:
- run resuscitation alone
- give medications
- perform procedures without supervision

## Common Student Trap

Trying to name the final diagnosis before checking if the patient is unstable.
