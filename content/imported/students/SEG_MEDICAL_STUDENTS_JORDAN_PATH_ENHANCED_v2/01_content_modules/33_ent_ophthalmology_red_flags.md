# ENT and Ophthalmology Red Flags for Students

> Safety boundary: This page is for supervised medical student learning. It is not an official Ministry of Health curriculum, not an official university syllabus, and not a treatment protocol.

## Learning Goal

Give students a simple red-flag framework for ENT and eye complaints without turning them into specialists.

## Why It Matters

ENT and eye complaints can look minor but occasionally threaten airway, vision, or intracranial spread. Students must know when to escalate.

## What the Student Should Know

### Eye Red Flags

- Sudden vision loss.
- Eye trauma.
- Severe eye pain.
- Chemical exposure.
- Red eye with photophobia or reduced vision.

### ENT Red Flags

- Airway symptoms.
- Stridor.
- Drooling with toxic appearance.
- Neck swelling.
- Severe headache with ear/sinus infection.
- Facial weakness.

## What the Student Should Do Under Supervision

- Ask onset, trauma, pain, vision change, fever, swallowing difficulty, breathing difficulty, and immune risk.
- Avoid pressuring the eye.
- Report airway, vision, or neurologic concerns immediately.

## What the Student Must Not Do Alone

- Remove foreign bodies.
- Prescribe eye drops.
- Reassure vision loss.
- Examine aggressive airway-risk patients unsupervised.

## OSCE / Exam Trap

Calling every red eye “conjunctivitis” without checking pain, photophobia, and visual change.
