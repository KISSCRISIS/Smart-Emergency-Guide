# Jordan Alignment Notes — Medical Students Path

## Important Limitation

This is an SEG educational alignment note, not an official curriculum document.

Jordanian public medical universities differ in study plans, clinical rotation timing, hospital affiliations, and assessment rules. SEG should therefore avoid claiming that one structure applies to every university.

## Public Medical Education Context

The Medical Students Path should be broad enough to serve students from Jordanian public medical faculties, including students training through university hospitals, Ministry of Health hospitals/centers, Royal Medical Services sites, and affiliated clinical settings.

## Alignment Principles

### 1. Preclinical years

Emphasize:
- integrated body systems
- basic medical sciences
- linking anatomy/physiology/pathology to symptoms
- early clinical reasoning vocabulary
- ethics/professionalism
- research basics

### 2. Early clinical exposure

Emphasize:
- history taking
- physical examination
- communication
- consent
- OSCE skills
- patient presentation
- supervised clinical exposure

### 3. Clinical rotations

Emphasize:
- internal medicine
- surgery
- pediatrics
- OB/GYN
- family/community medicine
- emergency medicine exposure when present
- psychiatry/dermatology/ENT/ophthalmology/orthopedics/radiology depending on university plan

### 4. Ministry of Health / primary care exposure

Emphasize:
- common complaints
- chronic disease care
- prevention
- referral decisions
- safety-netting
- public health
- documentation
- patient flow between clinic and hospital

### 5. Emergency medicine exposure

Emphasize:
- red flags
- ABCDE recognition
- ED observation
- handover
- patient safety
- no independent treatment decisions

## SEG Adaptation

SEG should provide a universal Jordan-friendly student pathway:

- not university-specific
- not official curriculum
- not replacing lectures
- not replacing clinical supervision
- safe for students across different public universities
- useful for preclinical, clinical, and senior students
